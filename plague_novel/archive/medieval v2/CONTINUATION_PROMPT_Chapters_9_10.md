### 2. SHOW DON'T TELL - Conversion Target: <5 "felt/feel" instances per chapter

**Before/After Examples Established:**
- ❌ "Thomas felt terrified. His heart hammered with fear."
- ✅ "Thomas's hands shook. He forced them still."
- ❌ "Margarethe felt grateful but didn't want to show it."
- ✅ "Margarethe turned away. 'We should keep moving.'"
- ❌ "Thomas felt suspicion war with hope."
- ✅ "Thomas's suspicion warred with hope."

**Application Pattern:**
- Show fear through physical reactions (shaking hands, hammering heart, cold sweat)
- Show emotional walls through deflection/action, not explanation
- Show realizations through the moment of understanding, not "he realized"
- Show trust/connection through action, not stating feelings
- Use possessive form: "Thomas's heart hammered" NOT "Thomas felt his heart hammer"

**Results Achieved:**
- Ch 5: 2 instances (from ~8)
- Ch 6: 4 instances (from ~12)
- Ch 7: 3 instances (from ~10)
- Ch 8: 2 instances (from ~15)

### 3. HISTORICAL AUTHENTICITY - Period Language Replacements

### 5. SENTENCE STRUCTURE VARIETY - Breaking Monotony

**Problem to Fix:**
"Thomas walked to the door. He opened it carefully. The hallway was empty. He stepped through."

**Fixed With Varied Structure:**
"Thomas approached the door and eased it open. The hallway stretched empty before him."

**Techniques Applied:**
- Start sentences with participial phrases: "Gripping the key, Thomas..."
- Use questions for variety: "How long until the guards returned?"
- Combine short sentences with semicolons or em-dashes
- Vary paragraph length (mix 1-sentence paragraphs with longer ones)
- Avoid repetitive sentence starters (NEVER 3+ consecutive sentences with character name)
- Alternate action/description/dialogue rhythms
