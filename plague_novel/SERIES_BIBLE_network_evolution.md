## NETWORK OVERVIEW

**Two Networks, One Origin:**
- Both created 1177 BCE during Bronze Age Collapse
- Split immediately over Protocol interpretation
- 3,195-year conflict over humanity's future
- Both adapt to survive each era's dominant power structures

**Key Difference:**
- **Defensive:** Distributed, adaptive, migrates with knowledge carriers
- **Offensive (Order):** Centralized, institutional, embeds in power structures

## ERA 1: BRONZE AGE COLLAPSE (1177-1100 BCE) - **BOOK 3**

### **DEFENSIVE NETWORK (Nefertari's Faction)**

**Founded:** 1177 BCE, Pi-Ramesses, Egypt
**Founder:** Nefertari (Egyptian medical scribe, Tausret's physician)

**Philosophy:**
- Collapse is PREVENTABLE if recognized early
- Knowledge must be DISTRIBUTED (resilient)
- Protect carriers, not institutions
- Migration > Centralization

**Initial Structure (1177 BCE):**
- Medical scholars (Nefertari's colleagues)
- Loyal palace officials (Tausret's supporters)
- ~50-100 founding members
- No formal hierarchy (cells, not pyramid)

**After Split (1177-1100 BCE):**
- Fled Egypt immediately (palace purge forced diaspora)
- Scattered across Eastern Mediterranean
- Cells in: Phoenicia (Tyre, Sidon), Cyprus, Mycenaean Greece, Canaan
- Protected Tausret's daughter → Canaan settlement
- Hidden 4 defensive bronze keys across network

**Membership:** ~500-1,000 by 1100 BCE
**Strategy:** Stay mobile, embed in diaspora communities
**Status:** Surviving but hunted

---

### **OFFENSIVE NETWORK (Amenhotep's Faction) - "THE ORDER" Begins**

**Founded:** 1177 BCE, Pi-Ramesses, Egypt
**Founder:** Amenhotep (Court official, Nefertari's rival)

**Philosophy:**
- Collapse is INEVITABLE (cyclical, unstoppable)
- Knowledge must be CENTRALIZED (controlled)
- Engineer who survives, preserve power
- Breeding > Prevention

**Initial Structure (1177 BCE):**
- Court officials (Amenhotep's allies)
- Military officers (palace guard)
- Priesthood (Temple of Amun)
- ~100-200 founding members
- Hierarchical (pyramid structure)

**After Split (1177-1100 BCE):**
- STAYED in Egypt (aligned with Setnakhte's coup)
- Embedded in 20th Dynasty (Ramesses III, etc.)
- Controlled: Temple priesthoods, royal advisors, military
- Possessed 3 offensive bronze keys
- Began hunting defensive network (failed)

**Membership:** ~1,000-2,000 by 1100 BCE
**Strategy:** Institutional embedding, power consolidation
**Status:** Dominant in Egypt
