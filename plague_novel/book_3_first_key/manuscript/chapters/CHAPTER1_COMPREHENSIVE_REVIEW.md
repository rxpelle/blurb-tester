### Rating: 8.5/10

**Strengths:**

- ✅ Ancient Egyptian medical knowledge exceptional (Edwin Smith/Ebers Papyri, pulse diagnosis)
- ✅ Bronze casting details technically accurate
- ✅ Pi-Ramesses, Per Ankh, Tausret, Setnakhte all correct

**Critical Fixes Required:**
1. **Date discrepancy**: Chapter says 1177 BCE, but Tausret died ~1189 BCE
   - **Solution**: Either change to 1189 BCE or use Ramesses III instead

2. **Genetic memory framing**: Too modern/theoretical for ancient Egypt
   - **Current**: "The body remembered, somehow. Passed patterns forward through those same invisible channels"
   - **Better**: "She'd observed patterns others missed: scribes' children learned faster. Famine marked families for generations. Perhaps the metu—the vessels carrying life force—also carried something else? It was wild speculation. Amenhotep called it 'physician's fancy.'"

3. **Terminology issues**:
   - "civilizational collapse" → "the end of the world"
   - "engineer who survives" → "choose who survives" or "breed the strong"
   - "That's science" → "That is how we test truth"
   - "systems thinking" → "pattern thinking" (one instance remains)

**Verdict**: With these 3 fixes, would rate **9.5/10** for authenticity.

## AGENT 6: STEPHEN KING STYLE ANALYSIS

### Rating: 4.5/10 (for King-ness)

**Note**: This rates King's specific voice, not overall quality. The chapter is **excellent historical fiction**, just not written in King's style.

**What's Missing King Elements:**
1. **No conversational narrator**: Reads formal, not like someone telling you a story
2. **Surface POV, not deep**: Watching Nefertari, not living inside her terror
3. **No backstory chunks**: Need Nefertari's mother, childhood trauma
4. **Insufficient visceral horror**: Intellectual dread, not body-level terror
5. **Dialogue too polished**: Real speech is messier
6. **Missing prose rhythms**: Long sentence → fragment pattern

**King Would Expand:**
- Tausret's death: 500 words → **1,000 words** (make us cry)
- Amenhotep confession: 200 words → **800 words** (20 years of might-have-been)
- Opening refugee scene: Add parenthetical: "(And if you've never smelled that, Constant Reader, God love you)"

**King Would Add:**
- Parenthetical thoughts interrupting: "(She couldn't. She knew she couldn't. But she tried anyway.)"
- Repetition for emphasis: "The refugees kept coming. And coming. And coming."
- Dark humor: "The gods had a sense of timing, at least."
- Physical fear responses: "Her stomach was a small cold stone"

**Sample King Rewrite** (opening):
> The smell hit Nefertari before she heard the sound—and if you've never been in a crowd of refugees who've lost everything, Constant Reader, then God love you and keep you. The smell of three thousand unwashed bodies. Shit and piss because the latrines had overflowed six months ago. Infected wounds going septic in Egypt's brutal sun. And underneath it all: the smell of civilization dying on its feet.

**Verdict**: Chapter is Eco-level (intellectual), not King-level (visceral). For maximum impact, blend both.
