## Part 1: The A10 Algorithm — What Actually Matters in 2026

Amazon's A10 algorithm has fundamentally changed how books get discovered. Understanding this is the foundation for every tactic below.

### Three New Pillars

1. **Semantic Relevance** — A10 doesn't match keywords anymore. It matches *intent*. "Medieval conspiracy thriller" will surface your book even if those exact words aren't in your metadata — as long as your reviews, description, and content signal that meaning.

2. **External Traffic Quality** — Amazon now rewards books that bring customers *from outside Amazon*. Your Substack, blog posts, social media, and website traffic directly boost your ranking. This is a massive advantage you already have infrastructure for.

3. **Sales Consistency Over Spikes** — The algorithm now values steady daily sales over a single launch-day spike. A book selling 5 copies/day for 30 days outranks one that sells 150 copies on day 1 and nothing after.

### Rufus AI Shopping Assistant

Amazon's Rufus doesn't search keywords — it reads reviews, themes, and metadata for contextual matches. It recommends books by *explaining why* they match, referencing actual reviews and content.

**What this means for Book 3:**
- Your reviews are now marketing copy (Rufus reads them to recommend your book)
- Natural language in title/subtitle/description beats keyword stuffing
- Category fit matters more than ever

### Metadata Optimization for A10

- **Title**: Natural, grammatically correct. Voice search (Alexa) and Rufus penalize keyword stuffing.
- **Backend Keywords**: Use semantic phrases, not individual words. "medieval conspiracy thriller secret society plague" not "medieval, conspiracy, thriller, secret, society, plague"
- **Categories**: Target micro-niches with 1,000+ monthly searches but manageable competition. Don't go broad ("General Fiction").
- **Description**: Write for humans, not bots. Rufus and A10 both reward natural language that matches reader intent.

## Part 2: Pre-Order Strategy

### Why Pre-Orders Are Critical

All pre-order sales count on release day. If 50 people pre-order over 30 days, Amazon sees 50 sales on day 1. This creates the initial sales rank spike that triggers algorithmic promotion — and then your consistent marketing sustains it.

### Timeline: 30-Day Pre-Order Window

Don't use Amazon's full 90-day pre-order window. 30 days is optimal — long enough to build momentum, short enough to maintain urgency.

**CRITICAL DEADLINE**: Final manuscript must be uploaded at least **10 days before release date** or Amazon revokes your pre-order privileges for one year.

#### 30 Days Out (Pre-Order Goes Live)
- [ ] Set up KDP pre-order with final or near-final cover
- [ ] Lock in metadata (title, subtitle, categories, keywords, description)
- [ ] Announce pre-order to email list with exclusive excerpt
- [ ] Post announcement across all platforms (Substack, social media)
- [ ] Submit to pre-order promotion sites

#### 21 Days Out (ARC Distribution)
- [ ] Send ARCs to 20-50 readers (use KDP print proof or digital)
- [ ] Include review deadline: release week
- [ ] Include specific ask: "Please post your review on Amazon on [release date]"
- [ ] Follow up with ARC readers at 14 days and 7 days

#### 14 Days Out (Content Blitz)
- [ ] Release chapter 1 preview on Substack
- [ ] Run BookTok scripts (already generated — fact, hook, scene)
- [ ] Deploy content-repurposer output across all 5 platforms
- [ ] Email list: "2 weeks until The First Key" with character reveal or world detail

#### 7 Days Out (Final Push)
- [ ] Email list: countdown begins, final excerpt, pre-order CTA
- [ ] Daily social media countdown posts
- [ ] Reach out to book bloggers/reviewers for launch week coverage
- [ ] Set up Amazon Ads campaigns (paused, ready to activate on launch day)

#### Launch Day
- [ ] Activate Amazon Ads
- [ ] Email blast: "It's here" with direct Amazon link
- [ ] Post across all platforms
- [ ] ARC team posts reviews (target: 10-20 reviews on day 1)
- [ ] Run Kindle Countdown Deal on Book 1 ($0.99) to drive series read-through

#### Days 2-30 (Sustain Momentum)
- [ ] Daily social media content (use content-repurposer backlog)
- [ ] Weekly Substack posts tied to Book 3 themes
- [ ] Monitor Amazon Ads, optimize keywords, pause underperformers
- [ ] Submit to book promotion sites (Freebooksy, Bargain Booksy, etc.)
- [ ] Email list: weekly updates, reader responses, behind-the-scenes

### Pre-Order Setup on KDP

1. Go to KDP Bookshelf → Create New Kindle eBook
2. Under Pre-order: select "Make my Kindle eBook available for Pre-order"
3. Choose release date (30 days out)
4. Upload manuscript (can be draft — but upload final at least 10 days before release)
5. Upload cover (final preferred, placeholder acceptable)
6. Set price, categories, keywords
7. Submit

## Part 3: Amazon Ads Strategy

### Budget & Expectations

| Metric | Benchmark | Your Target |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Daily budget | $10-15/day starting | $10/day |
| CPC (cost per click) | $0.50-$1.00 avg | $0.25-$0.50 (historical fiction less competitive) |
| CTR (click-through rate) | 0.5% = good, 1%+ = excellent | 0.5%+ |
| ACoS target | 30-40% for profitability | 35% |
| Ramp-up time | 6-8 weeks to full potential | Start on launch day |

### Campaign Structure (3 Campaigns)

#### Campaign 1: Auto Discovery (Week 1+)
- **Type**: Sponsored Products — Automatic targeting
- **Purpose**: Let Amazon find your audience. Harvest keywords.
- **Bid**: $0.25 starting
- **Daily budget**: $5
- **Action**: After 2 weeks, pull the search term report. Move converting terms to Campaign 2.

#### Campaign 2: Manual Keywords (Week 3+)
- **Type**: Sponsored Products — Manual targeting
- **Bid**: $0.30-$0.50 on proven keywords
- **Match types**: Mix of broad, phrase, and exact
- **Daily budget**: $5-10
- **Keywords to target**:
  - Comp author names (authors of similar books)
  - Genre phrases ("medieval conspiracy thriller", "historical fiction secret society")
  - Series-adjacent terms ("plague novel", "Da Vinci Code style")
  - Book 1 & 2 title variations (to capture existing readers)

#### Campaign 3: Lottery Ads (Backlist — Ongoing)
- **Type**: Sponsored Products — Automatic
- **Purpose**: Low-cost visibility for Books 1 & 2
- **Bid**: $0.15-$0.21 (minimum viable)
- **Daily budget**: $3-5
- **Strategy**: Bundle all backlist titles. Let Amazon's algorithm find cheap impressions. Deactivate underperformers, increase budget on winners.

### Keyword Harvesting Process

1. Run auto campaign for 14 days
2. Pull Search Term Report from KDP Ads dashboard
3. Identify terms with: clicks > 10, orders > 0
4. Add those as exact match keywords in Campaign 2
5. Add poor performers as negative keywords in Campaign 1
6. Repeat monthly

### Bid Optimization

- If keyword has high impressions but low CTR → cover or title problem, not bid problem
- If keyword has high CTR but no sales → description or price problem
- If keyword converts → increase bid by 20% above current CPC
- Use "Dynamic bids — down only" for conservative spend (Amazon lowers bids when conversion unlikely)

## Part 4: Free & Low-Cost Promotion Tactics

### KDP Select Promotions (Book 1)

Since Book 1 is in KDP Select, you get these free tools every 90-day enrollment:

#### Kindle Countdown Deal (Best for Launch Week)
- Temporarily discount Book 1 to $0.99 while **keeping your 70% royalty rate**
- Amazon adds a countdown timer to your listing (creates urgency)
- Duration: up to 7 days
- **Requirement**: Book must be priced at $2.99+ normally
- **Strategy**: Run on Book 1 during Book 3 launch week. Readers buy Book 1 cheap → read through to Book 2 → buy Book 3 at full price.

#### Free Book Promotion
- Make Book 1 free for up to 5 days per 90-day enrollment
- **Strategy**: Use 1-2 weeks before Book 3 launch to maximize series discovery
- Submit free promo to promotion sites (see below)

### Book Promotion Sites

**Tier 1 — High Impact:**

|------|-------|------|

| Freebooksy | Free ebook promotion | ~$100 |

**Tier 2 — Medium Impact (many are free):**

### Amazon Author Page & A+ Content (Free)

- [ ] **Author Central** (author.amazon.com): Set up if not done. Bio (150-300 words, third person), photo, blog feed, video.
- [ ] **A+ Content**: Add to all book listings. Goes under "From the Publisher" section. Use it to:
  - Show series reading order with covers
  - Add comparison tables (if you like X, you'll love this)
  - Include author backstory that connects to the book
  - Cross-promote other books in the series
- [ ] **Series Page**: Ensure all 3 books are linked as a series on Amazon. Drives read-through.

## Part 6: Series Read-Through Strategy

The real money in a 3-book series isn't Book 3 sales — it's getting new readers to buy all 3.

### Pricing Ladder
| Book | Regular Price | Launch Week Price |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| Book 1: The Aethelred Cipher | $4.99 (ebook) | $0.99 (Countdown Deal) |
| Book 2: The Genesis Protocol | $4.99 (ebook) | $4.99 (full price) |
| Book 3: The First Key | $4.99 (ebook) | $4.99 (full price) |

**Logic**: Cheap entry point (Book 1 at $0.99) → readers who love it buy Books 2 & 3 at full price. Total series revenue per reader: $10.97.

### Back Matter Optimization
Every book's back matter should include:
- "Did you enjoy this book? Please leave a review" with direct link
- "Read the next book in the series" with Amazon link
- "Join my mailing list" with sign-up link
- Book 3 preview chapter (already in Books 1 & 2)

### Ad Targeting for Read-Through
- Run ads on Book 1 (cheap entry), not Book 3
- Track "read-through rate" in book-data: what % of Book 1 buyers also buy Book 2 and Book 3
- If read-through is high (>30%), increase Book 1 ad spend aggressively — you're profitable even if Book 1 ads lose money
