Royal physician Nefertari has seen death in all its forms, but nothing prepared her for what she witnesses as the Bronze Age burns to ash around her — a pattern in the plague that reveals humanity's most dangerous secret.

In 1177 BCE, with civilization crumbling and her beloved Egypt falling, Nefertari and her lover Amenhotep make a desperate gambit. They forge seven bronze keys to preserve knowledge that could save or damn the human race. Their choice fractures the survivors into two hidden factions: protectors and predators.

Across eight centuries of ancient upheaval — through the rise of Israel, the glory of Babylon, the conquests of Alexander — descendants carry Nefertari's burden. Each generation must guard the secret from those who would exploit it, while confronting the same impossible choice that haunted the first key-bearers.

As one network scatters the knowledge across the ancient world, their enemies evolve into something far deadlier: an institution that will shape history from the shadows.

The First Key spans 842 years of real history, following the bloodlines that preserved — or pursued — civilization's most explosive secret.

Some legacies are written in blood. Others in bronze.
