Book 3 is real. *The First Key* is up for pre-order now.

### Day 2 — The Question
Writing prompt that started Book 3:

"How do you save knowledge when the world that holds it is burning?"

That's what Nefertari was trying to answer. 846 years later, nobody's sure she got it right.

---

### Day 3 — Historical Fact
The Bronze Age Collapse (1177 BCE) destroyed every major civilization in the Mediterranean within 50 years. Egypt, Mycenae, the Hittites, Ugarit — all gone.

Nobody knows exactly why. Historians still argue about it.

I gave my characters an answer.

---

### Day 4 — Behind the Scenes
Book 3 spans 854 years in 13 chapters. Each one follows a different carrier in a different century.

The hardest part wasn't the history. It was making sure a mother in 586 BCE Babylon sounds nothing like a Spartan warrior in 480 BCE — while they're both carrying the same blood.

---

### Day 5 — Character Tease: Nefertari
Nefertari spent 47 years preaching that no network should depend on a single person.

She never noticed she'd become exactly that.

---

### Day 6 — Series Callback
If you've read Book 1, you know the Order. You know what they became.

Book 3 shows you the moment they drew their first breath.

It wasn't what I expected when I started writing it.

---

### Day 7 — Historical Fact
The Oracle at Delphi operated for over 1,000 years. Kings, generals, and emperors made decisions based on what the Pythia said.

In Book 3, the Oracle isn't divine. It's something stranger.

---

## WEEK 2: Lore Drops & Character Reveals

### Day 8 — Lore Drop
There are seven bronze keys in the series. Each one protects a different piece of knowledge.

Book 3 reveals how they were forged, who decided what to protect, and why they were scattered across three continents.

One of those decisions was a mistake.

---

### Day 9 — Character Tease: Shiphra
Shiphra bat-Hiram was a Phoenician engineer hiding something beneath Solomon's Temple.

When the king's architect started dreaming about a city he'd never visited, she knew she'd run out of time.

---

### Day 10 — Behind the Scenes
I spent two weeks researching ancient Babylonian marketplace economics for a scene that's 25 lines long.

It's a mother teaching her five-year-old to read trade patterns in a foreign market. It's the best scene in the chapter.

---

### Day 11 — Historical Fact
Sennacherib's siege of Jerusalem in 701 BCE is one of history's great mysteries. His army of 185,000 just... left.

The Bible says an angel killed them in the night. Herodotus says mice ate their bowstrings.

I had a different idea.

---

### Day 12 — Character Tease: Aristodemos
Aristodemos survived Thermopylae. Historically, that's true — he was one of two Spartans sent away before the last stand.

Sparta called him a coward. Scratched TRESANTES on his doorpost. His own neighbors wouldn't look at him.

He died at Plataea the next year. By choice.

---

### Day 13 — Lore Drop
The defensive network prevents collapse. The offensive network controls it.

They've been running in parallel for 3,000 years under a single agreement: don't kill each other's carriers.

In Book 3, that agreement almost breaks. Twice.

---

### Day 14 — Series Callback
Book 2 readers met the modern Order — the institution that runs everything.

Book 3 shows the vote that created it. Twelve people in a room in Babylon, standing over the body of the most dangerous man who ever lived.

---

## WEEK 3: Deep Cuts & Emotional Hooks

### Day 15 — Character Tease: Rachel
Rachel walked out of burning Jerusalem carrying her infant daughter and the blood memory of every collapse her ancestors witnessed.

She'd warned the king. He didn't listen.

They never listen.

---

### Day 16 — Behind the Scenes
The hardest chapter to write was Alexander's death scene. Not because of the history — because I'd spent 6,000 words making you care about him as a sixteen-year-old kid who liked beetles and cyclamen flowers.

Then I had to show you what he became.

---

### Day 17 — Historical Fact
Artemisia I of Halicarnassus fought at the Battle of Salamis for the Persian side. She was the only woman commanding a fleet.

When cornered, she rammed one of her own allied ships to escape. Xerxes watched and said: "My men have become women, and my women men."

She's in Book 3. She's not what you'd expect.

---

### Day 18 — Lore Drop
"I have seventeen mothers in my head and I can't find which one is mine."

That's what genetic memory does to you after 600 years of inheritance. The memories pile up. Your own childhood gets buried under other people's.

---

### Day 19 — Character Tease: Xanthe
Socrates wasn't a carrier. The network tracked him for three years. Wrong guy.

The real carrier was a potter in the Kerameikos district, having dreams she couldn't explain while centering clay on a wheel.

She refused to join.

---

### Day 20 — Behind the Scenes
Every chapter in Book 3 ends with an interlude from the other side — the offensive network watching, planning, failing, adapting.

Twelve interludes. 842 years. One shadow narrative that doesn't become clear until the last page.

---

### Day 21 — Historical Fact
Alexander the Great died at 32 in Babylon. The cause of death is still debated — poison, typhoid, alcohol, or something else entirely.

His empire fractured within a decade.

In Book 3, his death isn't the tragedy. It's what was found in his hand.

---

## WEEK 4: Final Push & Countdown

### Day 22 — Lore Drop
The Pattern Eye. The Memory Bridge. The Distribution Network.

Three of the seven keys. Three different continents. Three thousand years of people killing each other to find them.

Book 3 shows where they were hidden first.

---

### Day 23 — Character Tease: Theophrastus
Theophrastus knew Alexander when he was sixteen — a kid who crouched in a garden watching beetles, who whispered "Don't tell anyone, it would undermine my conquest brand."

Twenty years later, Theophrastus found him in the ashes of Persepolis.

Same person. Different creature entirely.

---

### Day 24 — Behind the Scenes
The series bible for "The Architecture of Survival" tracks 3,289 years of continuity across three books. Every bloodline. Every key location. Every network vote.

One wrong date and the whole thing collapses.

Kind of like the civilizations in the story.

---

### Day 25 — Historical Fact
The Temple of Solomon was destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar in 586 BCE. Whatever was inside was either looted, destroyed, or moved before the siege.

In Book 3, someone arrives at the vault 400 years too late. The look on his face when he finds it empty is the turning point of the entire offensive network.

---

### Day 26 — Series Callback
Books 1 and 2 happen in the modern era. Secret societies. Conspiracies. People with abilities they don't understand.

Book 3 is the origin story. 1177 BCE to 323 BCE. The ancient world, told through the people who were trying to save it.

Same blood. Different millennium.

---

### Day 27 — Character Tease: Judith
Judith is the first carrier who asks the question nobody else will:

"Is this even worth it?"

She watches Micah walk into a plague camp knowing he won't come back. She holds him while the buboes bloom. And she wonders if 500 years of sacrifice has actually prevented anything.
