In 1177 BCE, the world burns.

As the Bronze Age collapses and civilizations fall like dominoes, Egyptian royal physician Nefertari witnesses something in the dying that no one else can see — patterns in the plague, rhythms in the chaos, a biological code written into human suffering itself.

With her lover Amenhotep, she forges seven bronze keys to preserve this dangerous knowledge. But their creation sparks an ideological schism that will echo for eight centuries: one network devoted to preventing catastrophe, another determined to control it.

Spanning 842 years — from the fall of Egypt to the death of Alexander the Great — The First Key follows the carriers of an inherited genetic memory as they navigate the ancient world's most devastating upheavals. Generation after generation, they must protect a secret that powerful men will kill to possess, while confronting an impossible question: Does humanity deserve to be saved from itself?

As the defensive network scatters its knowledge across Phoenicia, Israel, Babylon, and Greece, their rivals grow from patient observers into something far more dangerous — an institution that will one day call itself the Order.

Some secrets are older than civilization. This one shaped it.
