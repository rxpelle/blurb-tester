# 410 CE — Sack of Rome

Alaric the Visigoth sacks Rome in August 410 CE — the first time the city itself has fallen in ~800 years. Triggers [Augustine](../characters/augustine_of_hippo.md)'s *City of God* project. Does **not** itself move any of the seven keys (Pattern Eye remains in Hippo; five are already distributed; two — Time Chain and Truth Seal — are at risk but their loss is not dramatized on-page).

- **Book:** 5 (Augustine Protocol)

## What actually happens in Book 5 ch 7

The sack itself is offstage. Chapter 7 opens with a messenger arriving at Hippo during morning prayers. The book's portrayal of the fall is entirely through its effects on the defensive network's operations in North Africa:

| Scene | Location | Date | What changes |
|-------|----------|------|--------------|
| News arrives | Hippo basilica | Aug 410 | Augustine + [Possidius](../characters/possidius.md) recognize the collapse pattern; *City of God* is conceived in the immediate aftermath |
| Refugees arrive | Hippo harbor | Sept 410 | Public debate between Augustine and senator [Volusianus](../characters/volusianus.md); Augustine wins the crowd to the "two cities" framing |
| Network meeting | Hippo side chapel | Sept 410 | [Melania the Younger](../characters/melania_the_younger.md) self-identifies to Augustine; proposes funding the North African monastery system |
| Drafting begins | Hippo scriptorium | Nov 410 → 413 | Augustine scaffolds first three books of *City of God* (the refutation of pagan causation) |
| Jerome's endorsement | Letter Bethlehem → Hippo | Spring 413 | [Jerome](../characters/jerome.md), age 83, reluctantly endorses Augustine's dismantling of the Constantine Church-Empire synthesis |
| Monastery founded | Outside Hippo | Summer 413 | Augustine teaches ~20 students the encoding methodology; the plan to embed the seven key locations in *City of God* is conceived |

## Why the fall matters (book's thesis)

Book 5 frames Rome's fall as **structural collapse, not moral judgment.** Augustine's argument, in his own voice in ch 7:

> "Every empire falls regardless of its gods. Overextension. Economic collapse. Military exhaustion."

The Order's error was **tying eternal truth to temporal power** — hitching Christianity to the imperial apparatus Constantine built. When the apparatus collapses, anything fused to it collapses with it. The defensive model's strength is distribution:

> "The pattern can survive — if we encode it well enough, distribute it widely enough."

Chapter 7 is where the book's "centralization is fragility, distribution is resilience" thesis (set up in Helena's chapter 1 dialogue) gets its proof and becomes the operative methodology for the remaining five chapters.

## Network consequences

- **Melania the Younger's capital relocates to North Africa.** Her pre-sack liquidation of Roman estates becomes the financial base for Augustine's monastery system and the Carthage scriptorium.
- **Volusianus's cousin (unnamed)** begins drafting an encoded history of Rome — suggests a parallel literary preservation effort, possibly network-aligned.
- **Jerome shifts** from offensive operative to ambivalent ally. The 413 letter is the pivot — he endorses *City of God* before his 420 CE death.
- **Time Chain and Truth Seal status:** implied at risk, not yet lost on-page. Earlier wiki entries saying they are "lost in Rome's collapse" overstate what ch 7 actually dramatizes. Manuscript may detail their loss in a later chapter — flag.
