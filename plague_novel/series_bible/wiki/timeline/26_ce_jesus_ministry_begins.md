# 26 CE — Jesus's Ministry Begins

- **Date (in-world):** 26 CE — 33 CE (crucifixion) — 70 CE (Jerusalem destroyed)
- **Book(s):** Book 4 — Love Remembers in Silence (Nazarene Protocol)
- **Location:** Galilee / Judea / Jerusalem
- **Raw sources:** `../../SERIES_BIBLE_master_timeline.md` (lines 173–221)

## What happens

[Jesus](../characters/jesus.md), 41st-generation descendant of Tausret, begins ministry with unprecedented *spontaneous keyless access* to the Protocol — no one in 1,203 years has done this. His teachings are the defensive mode expressed as spiritual wisdom.

Three factions react:
- **Essenes** (defensive network): recognize prophesied carrier, support him
- **Temple Sadducees** (offensive network): see uncontrollable threat
- **Roman state**: sees political problem

**33 CE:** Crucifixion via Roman-Sadducee cooperation. [Miriam of Magdala](../characters/miriam_of_magdala.md) (Nefer's bloodline, defensive carrier) inherits 2 defensive keys — Pattern Eye and Memory Bridge. Disciples preserve the *method* (pattern recognition) but lose the *meaning* (genetic memory).

**70 CE:** First Jewish-Roman War. Temple destroyed. Offensive network's base gone — both networks pivot to infiltrating the early Church. 279-year gap to Book 5 opens.

## Who is present

- [Jesus](../characters/jesus.md)
- [Miriam of Magdala](../characters/miriam_of_magdala.md) — defensive carrier, Book 4 protagonist
- Marcus Cassius Longinus — Roman census official, discovers Jesus's genetic markers, Book 4 protagonist

## What changes as a result

- Christianity emerges — preserves defensive mode as unconscious spiritual practice
- Miriam's bloodline scatters across Roman Empire (diaspora)
- Both networks now embed in Christian institutions instead of Temple
- Keys 2 and 3 disperse: Pattern Eye → Antioch, Memory Bridge → Alexandria (Church Fathers)

## Continuity notes

- Jesus = first *keyless* Protocol access in series. Mechanism should be explicit in Book 4 vs implied.
- Miriam's bloodline (Nefer) ≠ Jesus's bloodline (Tausret) — two different defensive lineages converge in Book 4. Worth clarifying in draft.
- Longinus's descendants will surface in Book 5 via Constantine's bloodline — plant seed now.
