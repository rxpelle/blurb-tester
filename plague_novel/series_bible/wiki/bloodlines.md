# Bloodlines

The defensive bloodline runs 112 generations across 3,195 years. Four branches — one main line and three offshoots. Two parallel generation-counting systems.

- **Raw sources:** `../SERIES_BIBLE_bloodline_tracker.md`

## Two counting systems

Two numbering schemes coexist in-world. **This wiki uses Absolute as canonical.** Book 1 characters use Local in dialogue/internal thought.

### Absolute system (Bronze Age origin)
- **Gen 1:** [Tausret](characters/tausret.md) — 1177 BCE
- **Gen 42:** [Jesus](characters/jesus.md) — ~4 BCE–33 CE
- **Gen 64:** [Maria of Toledo](characters/maria_of_toledo.md) — 1333–1405 CE
- **Gen 112:** [Sarah Chen](characters/sarah_chen.md) — 1990–present

### Local system (Medieval network — Roman-era origin)
Used by the Eltville network because pre-400 CE records were lost/never held in Germanic territories. Counts from [Adalbert von Eltville](characters/adalbert_von_eltville.md).

- **Gen 1 (Local) = Gen 53 (Absolute):** Adalbert (~400 CE)
- **Gen 40 (Local) = Gen 62 (Absolute):** [Wilhelm von Eltville](characters/wilhelm_von_eltville.md) (1287–1347)
- **Gen 41 (Local) = Gen 63 (Absolute):** [Thomas of Eltville](characters/thomas_of_eltville.md) (1320–1350)

In-book usage: Thomas says "forty-one generations" — correct within medieval network's knowledge; they genuinely don't know they're Gen 63 from a Bronze Age origin. [Maria of Toledo](characters/maria_of_toledo.md)'s blood memory in Book 1 reveals network is "much older" than medieval members believe.

## Four branches

### Main Line — Tausret → Sarah Chen

**112 generations. Unbroken defensive carrier genetics.** The Living Key lineage.

- Egypt (Gens 1–2) → Canaan (3–5) → Israelite (6–40) → Jewish Galilee (41–48) → post-diaspora (49–64) → Spain (Maria) → Rafael's Silk Road migration → Chen family Guangzhou (69 onward) → Sarah Chen (112).
- Cultural shifts: Egyptian → Canaanite → Israelite → Jewish → Chinese
- Geographic journey: Egypt → Canaan → Judea → Babylon → Judea → Spain → Venice → Constantinople → Silk Road → China (~15,000+ km over 3,000 years)

### Branch 1 — Medieval German Network (Adalbert's Line)

Diverged from main line ~100–300 CE (early Christian era). Settled Rhineland after Rome's collapse. **Lost conscious knowledge of Bronze Age origins** — uses Local counting from Adalbert.

- Gen 53 (Absolute): [Adalbert von Eltville](characters/adalbert_von_eltville.md) (~370–450 CE) — founder
- Gen 62: [Wilhelm von Eltville](characters/wilhelm_von_eltville.md) (1287–1347) — cipher master, possessed "Iron Key"
- Gen 63: [Thomas of Eltville](characters/thomas_of_eltville.md) (1320–1350) — Book 1 protagonist
- After Thomas's death: line may have ended or been absorbed

Thomas and Maria are **contemporaries (both ~Gen 63–64)** but never meet — defensive network deliberately keeps bloodlines separate for protection.

### Branch 2 — Jesus's Half-Siblings

Diluted by Gen 45 (~100 CE). Possible carriers in early Church. Lost track by 200 CE.

- Gen 52: [Augustine of Hippo](characters/augustine_of_hippo.md) (354–430 CE) — **canonical per 2026-04-23 resolution** (was Gen 54 in agent read; Gen 52 wins per BOOK_BASELINE). Living Key candidate, encoder of *Confessions* + *City of God*

## Genetic memory mechanics

**Strengthens with:**
- Trauma/crisis (Bronze Age Collapse, Babylonian Exile, Black Death encoded into inheritance)
- Unbroken carrier-to-carrier transmission
- Intellectual tradition (pattern recognition selected for)
- Endogamous communities (Jewish diaspora, Chen scholarly family)
- Physical keys (amplify but not required for strong carriers)

**Weakens with:**
- Dilution through marriage into non-carrier populations
- Cultural disruption / loss of oral tradition
- Persecution destroying family lineages
- Time (natural decay without reinforcement)
