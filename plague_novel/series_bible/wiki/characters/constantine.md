# Constantine the Great

Book 5 secondary character. **Has weak carrier blood through his mother [Helena](helena.md) (Gen 50)** — making Constantine ~Gen 51 with diluted defensive markers. Per the Jan 2026 Book 5 manuscript (chapter 1, "The Empire's Pattern"), his Milvian Bridge "vision" is reframed as low-level genetic memory activation surfacing through the conversion experience — not a fully conscious Protocol awakening, but not purely mortal either.

- **Bloodline:** Maternal (Helena, Gen 50, weak carrier). Generation likely ~51, weak markers.
- **Dates:** ~272–337 CE
- **Location:** Roman Empire (Britain → Gaul → Rome → Constantinople)
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapter 1 — "Constantine Era"
- **Status at end of Book 5 era:** Dies 337 CE; institutional decisions outlast him by centuries
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_01_constantine_era.md`, `../../SERIES_BIBLE_master_timeline.md`

## Book 5 role (312–337 CE)

- **Milvian Bridge (312 CE):** vision combines genuine spiritual experience with low-grade genetic memory activation inherited via [Helena](helena.md). The chi-rho is read by the defensive network as Protocol-adjacent symbolism — Constantine himself doesn't fully understand what he's experiencing.
- **Edict of Milan (313 CE):** legalizes Christianity. Both networks now have an institutional shell to operate inside. Helena is the one who quietly understands what's happening on the network level.
- **Council of Nicaea (325 CE):** offensive network pushes hierarchical/centralized Church doctrine; defensive network pushes contemplative theology. Compromise: both survive in orthodox Christianity. Constantine is brokering a power dispute he only half-perceives the depth of.
- **Founding of Constantinople (330 CE):** unintentionally creates the eastern center where Key 3 (Memory Bridge) will sit for the next 1,000 years.
- He is **not** a primary POV — the Book 5 chapter 1 POV is split between Helena and (later) [Augustine](augustine_of_hippo.md).
