# Rufius Antonius Agrypnius Volusianus

Roman senator. Refugee from the Visigothic sack of Rome (410 CE). Spokesman for the **offensive narrative blaming Christianity for Rome's fall** — the "Christianity weakened Rome" thesis that *City of God* is written to refute. Not a carrier; not confirmed as Order-affiliated, but functions as the voice the Order would have used. Appears on-page only in Book 5 chapter 7.

- **Bloodline:** Not a carrier (as far as manuscript states).
- **Dates:** Historical. Flourishes ~400–437 CE.
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapter 7, scene 2 — Hippo harbor, Sept 410 CE
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_07_fall_of_rome.md` (scene 2, debate with Augustine; scene 6, cousin appears drafting encoded history)

## Book 5 role

- **Hippo harbor debate with Augustine (Sept 410 CE):** Volusianus articulates the orthodox-pagan accusation — Rome fell because it abandoned its ancestral gods for Christianity. Augustine wins the crowd with the "two cities" framing, pivoting the blame from religion to structural cause (empire overextension, military exhaustion, economic collapse).
- Function in the book: Volusianus is the *foil* whose public argument forces Augustine to articulate *City of God*'s thesis in front of witnesses. The book's ten-year writing project effectively begins in this scene.
- **Cousin (unnamed, scene 6):** A second senator, implied North Africa resident by 413 CE, drafting an encoded history of Rome. Suggests Volusianus's family orbit has both offensive-aligned and defensive-aligned members — not yet resolved in-text.

## Continuity notes

- Historical Volusianus is the addressee of Augustine's Letter 132 (~412 CE) and Letter 137, which argue the same case the chapter 7 debate does. The manuscript is consistent with the historical record.
- No on-page indication Volusianus is Order-affiliated beyond articulating the Order-convenient narrative. Keep him as "useful idiot / offensive-narrative mouthpiece" unless a later chapter re-roles him.
