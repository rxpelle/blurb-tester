# Atticus

Patriarch of Constantinople; defensive carrier; guardian of the **Speaking Scroll** (one of the seven keys per the Book 5 manuscript taxonomy — see [seven_keys.md](../seven_keys.md) for naming conflict). Receives Pattern Eye from [Possidius](possidius.md) post-430 CE and consolidates the Eastern preservation node that survives until Constantinople's fall (1453 CE).

- **Bloodline:** Gen 53 (per agent read); also cited as Gen 54 elsewhere — flag.
- **Dates:** historical Patriarch of Constantinople 406–425 CE; manuscript timeline implies he survives or is succeeded into the late 430s+
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapter 8 (scenes 3–4); referenced chapter 9
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_08_city_of_god.md`, chapter_09_final_years.md

## Book 5 role

- **Recognizes Augustine's encoding methodology** in *City of God* — the Constantinople network reads Book XVII (Constantine, founding of Constantinople) as the coordinate-key for the Speaking Scroll
- **Provides Augustine with exact coordinates** for the Speaking Scroll: Hagia Sophia, northern crypt, sealed vault accessible through the patriarch's private chapel
- **Receives Augustine's final letter** (chapter 9) with handover instructions
- **Receives Pattern Eye from Possidius** post-430 CE — both Pattern Eye and Speaking Scroll now consolidated under Constantinople / Hagia Sophia, where they sit for the next 1,000+ years

## Continuity notes

- Constantinople becomes the eastern preservation refuge — the structural reason the keys survive the Western collapse. Per the master_timeline / current `seven_keys.md`, Memory Bridge (the System A name for what may or may not be the Speaking Scroll) is in Constantinople 1380, then Samarkand 1420, then Guangzhou 1450 with Maria's son Rafael. **Reconciling that journey with the manuscript's "Pattern Eye + Speaking Scroll both in Hagia Sophia post-430"** is an open continuity question.
