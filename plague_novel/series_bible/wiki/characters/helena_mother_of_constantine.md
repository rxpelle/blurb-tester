# Helena (Mother of Constantine)

Constantine's mother. Defensive carrier (Gen 50). Book 5 chapter 1 POV character; early defensive network organizer; receives Pattern Eye from an Alexandria carrier ~312 CE. Her weak carrier blood is what gives her son [Constantine](constantine.md) the low-grade genetic-memory component of his Milvian Bridge vision.

- **Bloodline:** Gen 50. Weak carrier (per BOOK_BASELINE.md).
- **Dates:** ~250–330 CE (historical). Age 47 when she takes possession of Pattern Eye in Book 5 chapter 1.
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapter 1 — "The Empire's Pattern" (Constantine era, 312 CE)
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_01_constantine_era.md`, BOOK_BASELINE.md

## Book 5 — Augustine Protocol

- Receives Pattern Eye from an Alexandria carrier ~312 CE
- Teaches the 4th-century defensive network and Miriam (Syrian, Gen 51) about genetic memory and network structure
- Advocates **distribution over centralization**: *"Distribution is resilience. Centralization is fragility."* (chapter 1)
- Reads Constantine's conversion as institutional opportunity AND danger — legalization of Christianity creates the shell both networks then operate inside
- Hands Pattern Eye onward ~325 CE to **[Marcus Publius's 4th-c descendant — name TBD]** (per 2026-04-23 resolution — see [marcus_publius.md continuity notes](marcus_publius.md#continuity-notes); original Marcus Publius is 1st-c only and was never the recipient)

## Continuity notes

- **Name clash:** there is also a "Helena" who is a Gen 56 student of [Possidius](possidius.md) post-Augustine (chapter 8/9, North African aristocrat). They are different people. Disambiguate carefully when referencing.
- **Constantine's vision:** Helena's status as weak carrier is the mechanism for Constantine's vision having a genetic-memory component (see [constantine.md](constantine.md)). Without Helena there is no carrier path into Book 5's chapter 1.
- Historical Helena is venerated as a saint; tradition credits her with finding the True Cross in Jerusalem. Book 5 doesn't lean on the True Cross legend; her Book 5 role is network-organizational, not relic-hunting.
