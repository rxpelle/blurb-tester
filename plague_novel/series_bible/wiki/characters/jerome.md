# Jerome of Stridon

Translator of the Latin *Vulgate* Bible; offensive network's primary Church-level operative through most of Book 5. **Paradox:** also a Gen 51 defensive carrier himself — recognizes [Augustine](augustine_of_hippo.md)'s *City of God* encoding before he dies (420 CE) and approves it.

- **Bloodline:** Gen 51 defensive carrier; ambivalent — operates as offensive network agent for ~70 years.
- **Dates:** 347–420 CE (dies September 30, 420, age 86)
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapter 3 — "The Offensive Network." Constant background presence chapters 3–8.
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_03_offensive_network.md`, chapter_06_pelagian_controversy.md, chapter_08_city_of_god.md

## Book 5 role

- **Vulgate translation:** appears orthodox; functionally a network-preservation vehicle (encoded Septuagint scholarship preserved in canonical Latin)
- **Heresy enforcement:** coordinates suppression campaigns against [Pelagius](pelagius.md), Gospel of Mary, Gospel of Thomas, Secret Book of John, ~37 Gnostic/apocryphal texts (papal edict ~397 CE)
- **Tests Augustine** through theological correspondence (chapter 3) — "your unorthodox ideas are noted"
- **Spring 413 CE letter from Bethlehem (ch 7 scene 5):** age 83, reluctantly endorses Augustine's dismantling of the Constantine Church-Empire synthesis. Canon-weight lines: *"If the City of God is separate from every earthly power, then the Church cannot claim political authority… Only the Church that remains separate, networked, distributed — only that survives."* This is the earliest on-page moment Jerome functions as a defensive voice; shifts him from pure offensive operative to ambivalent ally seven years before his death.
- **Provides codices to Augustine** for *City of God* research (chapter 8) — the cooperation moment that consolidates his shift
- **Death (420 CE):** sends Augustine his personally annotated Septuagint; final letter praises Augustine's work. Recognizes the encoding strategy was right and his own 70-year offensive-network career was a partial failure. Augustine's later assessment: *"Jerome spent seventy years building Christendom. Believed Christian empire would preserve truth. Realized it failed."*

## Continuity notes

- **Functional paradox:** Jerome is *both* offensive operative *and* defensive carrier with full genetic memory potential. This is the book's clearest illustration that the carrier/network distinction is not deterministic — bloodline doesn't dictate which side a person serves.
- His death (420 CE) is 10 years before Augustine's. Augustine writes *City of God* knowing Jerome may live to read and judge it — that constraint shapes the encoding choices.
