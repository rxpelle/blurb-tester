# Alypius

Augustine's childhood friend from Thagaste; later bishop of Calama. Defensive network coordinator and Augustine's strategic advisor in Book 5. Holds Pattern Eye temporarily as guardian after [Monica](monica.md); later escorts it toward Constantinople.

- **Bloodline:** Gen 55. Distant branch — weak genetic memory ("fragments, not full visions").
- **Dates:** historical figure, ~360–430 CE
- **First appears:** Book 5 chapters 3–5 (major scenes), recurring through chapter 9
- **Raw sources:** `../../book_5_augustine_protocol/manuscript/chapters/chapter_03_offensive_network.md`, chapter_04_the_bishop.md, chapter_05_confessions.md

## Book 5 role

- **Network signal:** "three quick, two slow" knock pattern (chapter 4) — used to identify himself at Augustine's door
- **391 CE Hippo arrival:** brings intelligence that [Jerome](jerome.md) is "systematically mapping" defensive carriers via theology survey — confirms the offensive network is conducting a Christian-intellectual census
- **Pelagian advisor:** counsels Augustine on the public-condemn / privately-support strategy that becomes Augustine's signature move
- **Bronze key:** carries one in his bishop's staff (per outline; manuscript treats as defensive key)
- **Pattern Eye relay:** Augustine hands it to Alypius temporarily for safekeeping; Alypius later coordinates the eastward transfer toward Constantinople

## Continuity notes

- Childhood-friendship-turned-network-partnership is a recurring beat — same dynamic repeats across the series (cf. Thomas/Margarethe in Book 1).
- Weak genetic memory means Alypius's contribution is operational and analytic, not visionary — he's the network's strategist where Augustine is its encoder.
