# Wilhelm von Eltville

Cipher master and network coordinator of the medieval German branch. Gen 62 (Absolute) / Gen 40 (Local). [Thomas of Eltville](thomas_of_eltville.md)'s grandfather. Dies in the Black Death year (1347 CE).

- **Bloodline:** Main Tausret line → Adalbert branch → Wilhelm
- **Dates:** 1287–1347 CE (dies age 60)
- **First appears:** Book 1 — The Aethelred Cipher (as backstory / mentor figure)
- **Raw sources:** `../../SERIES_BIBLE_bloodline_tracker.md` (lines 453–483)

## Character annotation from Book 1

> *Wilhelm von Eltville (1287–1347): Mind for space: strong. Eye for patterns: rare. Memory: keen. Wit: sharp. Marked for cipher-work.*

## Genetic memory

**STRONG** — manifests as exceptional pattern-recognition and cipher-breaking ability. Could break complex codes intuitively. "Eye for patterns: rare" is the medieval network's description of unconscious genetic memory expression.

## Network role

- Taught [Thomas](thomas_of_eltville.md) pattern recognition from childhood
- Maintained defensive knowledge fragments
- Protected Distribution Network cipher fragments
- Possessed an **Iron Key** (referred to in raw as "one of seven bronze keys" — see [seven_keys.md](../seven_keys.md) continuity note)

## Family

- Son: Friedrich (1261–1312)
- Grandson: [Thomas](thomas_of_eltville.md) (1320–1350)

## Death

1347 CE — Black Death or Order's purge. Raw is ambiguous. Age 60.

## Legacy

- Trained Thomas to see patterns
- Passed the Iron Key
- Never knew he was Gen 62 in the Absolute system — thought of himself as Gen 40 from Adalbert

## Continuity notes

- "Iron Key" vs. "one of seven bronze keys" — bronze ≠ iron. Either material is wrong, or medieval network used iron cipher artifacts distinct from the original seven bronze keys. Flag for Randy.
- His exact death cause (plague vs. purge) should be decided before Book 1 finalizes — affects tone of Thomas's arc.
