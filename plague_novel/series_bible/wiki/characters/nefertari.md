# Nefertari ("Nefer")

Medical scribe and physician to Pharaoh [Tausret](tausret.md). Architect of the defensive Protocol. Founder of [the defensive network](../factions/defensive_network.md).

- **Bloodline:** Not a Living Key carrier herself — she *builds the system*
- **First appears:** Book 3 — The First Key (1177 BCE)
- **Status at end of latest canon:** Alive at age 82 in 1100 BCE (meets Taharqa); death date TBD in draft
- **Raw sources:** `../../SERIES_BIBLE_master_timeline.md` (lines 34–58, 77–115)

## Book 3 — The First Key

**1177 BCE:** Creates the defensive Protocol in response to [Amenhotep](amenhotep.md)'s offensive version. Forges the seven bronze keys. Flees Egypt with the Mediterranean diaspora.

**1100 BCE (ch 3):** Age 82. Taharqa reaches Egypt with 217 Mycenaean refugees. Nefertari admits them to the defensive network. Presides over Aristomache's Question — network votes 7–5 for **empirical encoding** over ideological encoding. This philosophical commitment defines the defensive network for the next 3,195 years.

## Book 4 — Love Remembers in Silence

Her bloodline (not Tausret's) runs through [Miriam of Magdala](miriam_of_magdala.md). Miriam is Nefer's descendant, not Jesus's.

## Continuity notes

- "Nefer" = nickname, use consistently in-draft per raw
- She founded two things distinct from each other: (1) the defensive *Protocol* as technology, (2) the defensive *network* as organization. Both share her origin.
- Her bloodline diverges from Tausret's — two separate defensive lineages that re-converge in Book 4 via Miriam and Jesus
