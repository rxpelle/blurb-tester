# Adalbert von Eltville

Founder of the medieval German defensive network chapter. Gen 53 (Absolute) / Gen 1 (Local). Establishes the Rhineland branch during Rome's collapse — but **lost conscious knowledge of Bronze Age origins**. The medieval network's counting system starts with him.

- **Bloodline:** Main Tausret line, diverged in early Christian era (~100–300 CE)
- **Dates:** ~370–450 CE
- **First appears:** Referenced in Book 1 — The Aethelred Cipher
- **Raw sources:** `../../SERIES_BIBLE_bloodline_tracker.md`

## Historical context

Roman Empire collapsing (400–476 CE). Germanic tribes migrating into former Roman territories. Adalbert's family already present in Rhineland. Creates the medieval defensive network in response to the chaos.

## What he establishes

- Monthly gatherings (tradition continues to Thomas's time, 900+ years later)
- Pattern recognition taught as *practical wisdom* — not as genetic memory (no one in the branch knows the Bronze Age origin anymore)
- Cipher artifacts preserved across generations (iron/bronze — see [seven_keys.md](../seven_keys.md) for material discrepancy)

## Legacy

- Medieval network measures all its generations from him
- Book 1 establishes Thomas is "forty-one generations from Adalbert" (Local counting)
- Adalbert himself = Gen 53 (Absolute) — unknown to his own descendants
- Genetic memory **present but not consciously accessed** in his line
- [Maria of Toledo](maria_of_toledo.md)'s blood memory visions in Book 1 reveal the Bronze Age origin the medieval network has forgotten
