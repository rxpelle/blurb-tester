### Genesis Protocol
The genetic-memory encoding technology created in 1177 BCE. Exists in two incompatible modes: defensive (empirical, preservative) and offensive (ideological, engineering).
- First appears: Book 3
- Also used: Books 1, 2, 4, 5+

### Defensive mode
The version of the Protocol that encodes *experience* without prescribing interpretation. Architected by [Nefertari](characters/nefertari.md). Philosophy: empirical encoding. Network: scattered, decentralized, preservationist.
- First appears: Book 3

### Offensive mode
The version of the Protocol that encodes *values* — uses breeding programs and population engineering. Architected by [Amenhotep](characters/amenhotep.md). Network: centralized, institutional, proactive.
- First appears: Book 3

### Living Key (Defensive Key 1)
A *person*, not an object. The strongest genetic memory carrier of a given generation within Tausret's bloodline. Canonical carriers: Tausret's daughter → Jesus (41 gen) → Maria of Toledo (41 gen from Jesus) → Sarah Chen (28 from Maria).
- First appears: Book 3
- Also used: Books 1, 2, 4

### Keyless access
Spontaneous activation of the Protocol without requiring a bronze key. Only Jesus has done this (26 CE). Makes him uncontrollable to both networks — neither can block him by denying him a key.
- First appears: Book 4

### The Order
The offensive network's medieval-through-modern name. Earlier names: "Amenhotep's network", "Temple Sadducees". Modern corporate form: GenVault (1952+).
- First appears: Book 3 (as network); named "the Order" in Books 1, 5+

### GenVault
The Order's 1952 corporate transformation. Seven corporations, all Order-controlled. Public face: disease prevention and genetic medicine. Actual purpose: offensive Protocol breeding program.
- First appears: Book 2

### THRESHOLD
The 15%-of-global-population-engineered trigger point for the Order's population reduction event. At 12.8% as of 2018 CE (Book 2). Reaching 15% triggers 1.4 billion planned deaths.
- First appears: Book 2

### Pattern Eye
Defensive Key 2. Physical bronze key. See [seven_keys.md](seven_keys.md#key-2-pattern-eye).
- First appears: Book 3

### Memory Bridge
Defensive Key 3. Physical bronze key. Travels the Silk Road with Maria's son Rafael's line, ending up in the Chen family archive in Guangzhou. See [seven_keys.md](seven_keys.md#key-3-memory-bridge).
- First appears: Book 3

### Distribution Network
Defensive Key 4. Fragmentary — encoded across multiple defensive families and later across Thomas of Eltville's 11-city distribution in 1347. See [seven_keys.md](seven_keys.md#key-4-distribution-network).
- First appears: Book 3

### Blood Register
Offensive Key 1. Breeding records. Held by the Order continuously 1177 BCE → 2018 CE.
- First appears: Book 3

### Catastrophe Clock
Offensive Key 2. Collapse timing methodology. Held by the Order.
- First appears: Book 3

### Culling Method
Offensive Key 3. Population engineering methodology. Held by the Order.
- First appears: Book 3

### Empirical encoding
The defensive network's foundational philosophy, established at the 1100 BCE Greek branch vote (7–5). Genetic memory preserves *experience as witnessed*, not interpretation. Future carriers are trusted to interpret the data themselves. Contrast: ideological encoding.
- First appears: Book 3

### Aristomache's Question
The 1100 BCE Greek branch dilemma: should genetic memory encode ideology or just data? Resolved 7–5 in favor of data.
- First appears: Book 3

### Greek branch
The defensive network's Mycenaean refugee wing, formed 1100 BCE when Taharqa (Thrasymedes of Pylos) reached Egypt with 217 refugees on 12 ships.
- First appears: Book 3

## Book 5 additions (ingested 2026-04-22)

### *Nous* meditation / "Prayer of the Heart"
The contemplative technique used by carriers to access genetic memory. Greek *nous* = spiritual eye, understanding between soul and spirit. Taught publicly as orthodox Christian contemplation; functions as the actual genetic-memory access methodology. Transmitted by Mary Magdalene → Marcus Publius → Monica → Augustine. Becomes Hesychasm in Eastern Orthodoxy and the Western contemplative tradition (Benedictine, Cistercian).
- First appears: Book 5
- Raw: Book 5 chapters 2, 4, 5, 9

### City of God (concept)
Augustine's two-cities framework. Surface meaning: theological eschatology (City of God = divine community, City of Man = earthly empire). Encoded meaning: defensive network (distributed, resilient, divine-love-built) vs. offensive network (hierarchical, efficient, self-love-built). The book *City of God* (413–426 CE, 22 books) doubles as systems-thinking analysis of collapse + map of five key locations.
- First appears: Book 5
- Raw: Book 5 chapter 8

### Encoding / Double-layer writing
The Book 5 strategy for hiding defensive methodology in plain sight inside orthodox Christian theology. Both layers genuine — surface satisfies the Church, depth instructs future carriers. Templates: Augustine's *Confessions*, *City of God*, anti-Pelagian treatises, and Possidius's *Vita Augustini*.
- First appears: Book 5
- Raw: Book 5 chapters 5, 6, 8, 9

### Seven Powers (collapse modes)
Seven failure-pattern archetypes identified in the Gospel of Mary; Augustine encodes them into both spiritual and systemic frames in *City of God*: (1) Darkness (inability to see patterns), (2) Desire (short-term over long-term), (3) Ignorance (refusal to learn from past), (4) Zeal for Death (glorification of violence), (5) Kingdom of Flesh (material greed), (6) Foolish Wisdom (efficiency over resilience), (7) Wrathful Wisdom (pride refusing correction).
- First appears: Book 5
- Raw: Book 5 chapter 5 (Gospel of Mary reading), chapter 8 (City of God encoding)

### Speaking Scroll

### Bronze Mirror

### Memory Stone
**Per Book 5 manuscript taxonomy only.** Possibly the same artifact as System A's Memory Bridge — see [seven_keys.md](seven_keys.md). Hidden in Mount of Olives baptistery foundation, Jerusalem.

### Time Chain
**Per Book 5 manuscript taxonomy only.** Location unknown at end of Book 5. Properties: connects present to past and future (temporal-pattern navigation).
- First appears: Book 5

### Truth Seal
**Per Book 5 manuscript taxonomy only.** Location unknown at end of Book 5. Properties: makes truth permanent, uncorruptible, transmissible.
- First appears: Book 5

### Marcus Publius
Roman census official; defensive carrier; the Book 4 → Book 5 bridge character. Witnessed Jesus's crucifixion 32 CE; ancestor of Augustine. See [marcus_publius.md](characters/marcus_publius.md).
- First appears: Book 4
- Also used: Book 5 (extensively)
