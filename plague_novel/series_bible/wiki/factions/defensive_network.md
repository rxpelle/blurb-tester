# The Defensive Network

Preservation-oriented faction. Founded 1177 BCE by [Nefertari](../characters/nefertari.md). 3,195 years of continuous operation — scattered, adaptive, decentralized.

- **First appears:** Book 3 — The First Key
- **Status at latest canon:** Dormant until 2018 CE; Sarah Chen is first activation attempt in 670 years
- **Raw sources:** `../../SERIES_BIBLE_master_timeline.md`, `../../SERIES_BIBLE_network_evolution.md`

## Origin

**1177 BCE, Pi-Ramesses:** Nefertari creates the defensive Protocol after Tausret's death. Network flees Egypt in Mediterranean diaspora. Holds 4 of the 7 bronze keys.

**1100 BCE, Greek branch formation:** Taharqa (formerly Thrasymedes of Pylos) reaches Egypt with 217 Mycenaean refugees. The network's defining philosophical moment happens here — **Aristomache's Question:** should genetic memory encode ideology (values) or just data (experience)? **Network votes 7–5 for empirical encoding.** Commitment: preserve data, trust future carriers to interpret. This distinguishes defensive from [the Order](the_order.md)'s value-encoding approach. Sets the foundation for Greek philosophical tradition (observation over dogma).

## Institutional forms across eras

- **1177 BCE:** Egyptian exiles → Mediterranean diaspora
- **1100 BCE:** + Greek branch (Mycenaean refugees)
- **1100–900 BCE:** Bloodline settles Canaan hill country → integrates into Israelite tribes → connects to Davidic line (~900 BCE)
- **586 BCE:** Survives Babylonian exile
- **500 BCE:** Essene community at Qumran; settlements in Galilee
- **26–33 CE (Book 4):** Essenes support Jesus
- **100–300 CE:** Church Fathers era — Origen, Clement of Alexandria possibly members
- **325 CE:** Pushes contemplative theology at Council of Nicaea
- **500–800 CE:** Irish monasteries preserve ancient texts
- **800–1200 CE:** Benedictine, Cistercian orders
- **1347 CE (Book 1):** Protecting Maria of Toledo; scattered
- **1500+:** Further dispersal, partial loss of coordination
- **1952–2018:** Dormant
- **2018 CE (Book 2):** Sarah Chen activates

## Continuity notes

- **Empirical encoding** is the foundational philosophy — must be baked into how the network behaves across every era. They don't tell carriers what to think; they preserve what happened.
- Two bloodlines within defensive network: **Tausret's** (Living Key carriers — Jesus, Maria, Sarah) and **Nefer's** (Miriam's line). Don't merge them.
- Network is always *reactive and preservationist* — contrast with Order's *proactive and engineering*.
