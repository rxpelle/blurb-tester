**Subject:** The research that didn't make it into the book

**Body:**

{{ subscriber.first_name }},

Quick question: Have you read *The Edmund Fragments* yet?

While you're exploring the world of the series, I wanted to share some of the wild historical research that *almost* made it into *The Aethelred Cipher* but got cut for pacing:

**1. Medieval Lock-Picking Was Incredibly Sophisticated**

That scene where Thomas picks the bishop's treasury lock? Based on actual 14th-century mechanisms that would give modern locksmiths nightmares. The "Bavarian wheel-and-pin" Thomas uses is a real design that wouldn't be surpassed until the 1800s.

**2. Blood Memory Isn't Entirely Fiction**

Okay, forty-generation genetic memory is fiction. But epigenetic inheritance — where trauma and experience literally change your DNA and pass to children — is real science. Mice taught to fear cherry blossoms passed that fear to their grandchildren. The medieval network just took it to an extreme.

**3. The Plague's Actual Death Toll Was Worse Than I Wrote**

I *downplayed* it for the novel. Real estimates: 30-60% of Europe's population. In some cities, 80%. That's not a catastrophe — that's near-extinction.

Want more historical rabbit holes? I post these on the blog: https://randypellegrini.com/blog.html

Still reading?
