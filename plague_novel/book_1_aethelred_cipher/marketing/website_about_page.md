## The Longer Version

### Why I Write Historical Thrillers

I've always been fascinated by the patterns in history—not just what happened, but *why* certain catastrophes seem to repeat across centuries. Why do civilizations collapse in similar ways? Why does knowledge get lost, then rediscovered, then lost again?

*The Genesis Protocol* series emerged from a simple question: What if someone was keeping track? What if there was a network that *knew* the pattern—and worse, profited from it?

### The Research Rabbit Hole

For *The Aethelred Cipher*, I spent [TIME] researching:
- Medieval cipher systems and cryptography
- The Black Death's actual timeline and spread patterns
- Byzantine manuscript preservation techniques
- 14th-century merchant networks and trade routes
- The real conspiracies of the medieval period (which were fascinating enough without embellishment)

Every cipher in the book is based on real historical encoding methods. The plague timeline is accurate. The locations are real. The conspiracy... well, that's where fiction takes over.
