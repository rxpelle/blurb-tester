# Cover Design Brief: The Aethelred Cipher

## BOOK INFORMATION

**Title:** The Aethelred Cipher
**Subtitle:** Book 1: The Genesis Protocol Series
**Author:** Randy Pellegrini
**Genre:** Historical Thriller / Medieval Mystery
**Length:** 70,000 words
**Setting:** Medieval Europe (1347-1350) - Mainz, Strasbourg, Prague, Constantinople, York

---

## TARGET AUDIENCE

**Primary:** Adults 30-60 who read literary historical thrillers
**Comparable readers:** Fans of Umberto Eco (*Name of the Rose*), Ken Follett (*Pillars of the Earth*), Kate Mosse (*Labyrinth*)
**Psychographics:** Educated, curious about history, appreciate complex plots, value craft alongside entertainment

---

## COMP TITLES (Visual Reference)

**Study these covers:**
1. *The Name of the Rose* by Umberto Eco - mysterious, medieval, intellectual
2. *Labyrinth* by Kate Mosse - dual timeline hints, historical mystery
3. *The Historian* by Elizabeth Kostova - scholarly adventure, European settings
4. *The Pillars of the Earth* by Ken Follett - medieval epic, cathedral imagery

**What works in this genre:**
- Medieval/ancient aesthetic (parchment, stone, old manuscripts)
- Mysterious symbols or cipher elements
- European architectural elements (cathedrals, monasteries)
- Muted color palettes (browns, grays, golds, deep reds)
- Sense of secrecy and forbidden knowledge

**What to AVOID:**
- Too modern/slick (this isn't Dan Brown)
- Too fantasy (no dragons, magic symbols)
- Too dark/horror (this is thriller, not horror)
- Cliché images (hooded figures, obvious conspiracies)

## DESIGN DIRECTION

### Concept 1: The Cipher (PREFERRED)
**Central Image:** Ancient manuscript page with colored dots cipher system visible in margins
**Background:** Aged parchment texture, stained and weathered
**Overlays:** Faint map of medieval Europe showing the route
**Color Palette:** Aged cream/brown parchment, deep red/blue cipher dots, gold accents
**Typography:** Medieval-inspired but readable serif font

**Mood:** Mysterious, scholarly, secretive, urgent

**Visual Elements:**
- Cipher dots (red, blue, yellow) in geometric patterns
- Latin text fragments (authentic period script)
- Subtle key symbol (the iron key with eye motif)
- Worn edges, ink stains, authenticity

### Concept 2: The Keys
**Central Image:** Ornate medieval iron key with eye symbol in handle
**Background:** Dark stone (monastery wall or cathedral)
**Overlays:** Faint cipher patterns or map
**Color Palette:** Dark grays, iron/silver for key, gold/red accents
**Typography:** Strong serif, medieval influence

**Mood:** Dark, ominous, treasure-hunt feel

### Concept 3: The Network Map
**Central Image:** Stylized medieval map of Europe with cipher symbols at key locations
**Background:** Parchment
**Overlays:** Network lines connecting cities, plague spread patterns
**Color Palette:** Sepia tones, red for plague/danger, gold for locations
**Typography:** Clean but period-appropriate

**Mood:** Conspiracy, scope, interconnected mystery

## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Ebook Cover
- **Dimensions:** 2560 x 1600 pixels minimum (Amazon KDP standard)
- **Format:** JPG or TIFF, RGB color, 72 DPI minimum
- **File size:** Under 50MB
- **Safe area:** Keep all text/important elements within inner 90% of image

### Print Cover (if doing paperback)
- **Trim size:** 5.5" x 8.5" or 6" x 9" (standard trade paperback)
- **Format:** PDF, CMYK color, 300 DPI
- **Spine width:** Calculate based on page count and paper type
- **Bleed:** 0.125" on all edges
- **Back cover:** Space for description, barcode, author bio/photo

### Series Branding
- **Consistency:** Design should be template-able for Books 2-X
- **Variations:** Allow for color shifts while maintaining core look
- **Logo:** Consider series logo/icon for "The Genesis Protocol"

## BUDGET (EBOOK + PAPERBACK PRINT WRAP)

**Professional print wrap covers for this genre:** $600-$1,500

**Tier 1 ($500-700):** Pre-made template customization + print wrap
- Pros: Affordable, fast turnaround
- Cons: Less unique, may see similar covers, basic back cover
- Best for: Budget launches, testing market
- **Note:** Print wrap adds $200-300 to ebook-only price

**Tier 2 ($700-1,000):** Semi-custom design with full print wrap (RECOMMENDED)
- Pros: Original composition, professional quality, custom back cover
- Cons: Limited revision rounds
- Best for: Most self-pub authors launching ebook + paperback
- **This is the sweet spot for historical thriller launch**

**Tier 3 ($1,000-1,500+):** Fully custom illustration with premium print wrap
- Pros: Completely unique, series-ready branding, standout bookstore presence
- Cons: Expensive for unproven author
- Best for: Series with guaranteed sequels, trad-pub quality aspirations

**Important:** Ebook-only covers cost $300-800. Print wrap adds $300-500 but is ESSENTIAL for paperback launch and gives you bookstore-quality presentation.
