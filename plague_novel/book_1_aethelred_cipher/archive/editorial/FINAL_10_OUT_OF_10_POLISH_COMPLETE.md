## 3. Thomas Vulnerability Moment

### Objective
Add one final vulnerability moment for Thomas to balance his character growth with ongoing fear, preventing him from becoming unrealistically heroic.

### Moment Added: Nightmare and Fear Admission (Chapter 7, after night watch scene)

**Location:** Chapter 7, lines 73-87 (inserted after romantic night watch scene)

**Content:**
```markdown
But sleep brought dreams.

Thomas saw Brother Hamo bleeding in the scriptorium. Wilhelm on the rack.
Johannes captured. The Gray Robes closing in. And in the dream, he was
running—always running—but his feet wouldn't move fast enough. The monastery
walls closed in. The torture chamber's walls pressed closer. He tried to scream
but couldn't draw breath.

He woke with a gasp, heart hammering, Margarethe's hand gentle on his shoulder.

"Nightmare?" she asked quietly.

Thomas nodded, not trusting his voice. The fear was still there—would always be
there, he suspected. He'd grown stronger, yes. More capable. But the terror of
what they faced, the certainty that this quest would likely end in capture and
execution—that truth pressed close in the darkness.

"I'm terrified," he admitted in a whisper, the words escaping before he could
stop them. "Every day. Margarethe says I've gotten braver, but that's not quite
right. I'm still frightened. I've just learned to keep walking anyway."

Margarethe's hand squeezed his shoulder. "That's what courage is, Thomas. Not
the absence of fear—doing what needs to be done despite it. Now sleep. I've got
watch. You're safe for a few more hours at least."

And somehow, that promise—limited as it was—helped him find rest again.
```

**Strategic Placement:** Immediately after the intimate night watch scene, creating emotional complexity—romance developing alongside ongoing trauma.

**Key Elements:**
1. **Nightmare content:** Specific references to fallen allies (Hamo, Wilhelm, Johannes)
2. **Physical manifestation:** Heart hammering, gasping awake
3. **Direct admission:** "I'm terrified. Every day."
4. **Redefinition of courage:** Not absence of fear, but action despite it
5. **Margarethe's response:** Validates his fear, reframes courage, offers limited but honest comfort

**Character Arc Impact:**
- Prevents Thomas from becoming unrealistically heroic
- Shows growth (keeps walking anyway) while maintaining vulnerability
- Deepens Thomas/Margarethe relationship through honest emotional exchange
- Demonstrates PTSD-like symptoms from violence witnessed/committed
