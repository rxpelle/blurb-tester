## Writing Principles

1. **Physical/sensory first.** Every scene begins with what the body feels — warmth, cold, bread smell, stone texture
2. **Show the dual-layer teaching, never explain it.** Mary's face changes. Don't have her explain systems terminology
3. **Mary gets the most room.** Her arc is the emotional backbone
4. **Sarah as witness.** Not wise beyond her years. Present, observing, asking seven-year-old questions
5. **Marcus as outsider narrator.** Military precision, emotional suppression. Gap between what hands do and chest feels
6. **Judas as rescuer who can't rescue.** Every beat builds toward the tragedy
7. **No lecture-format systems theory.** Show through character behavior, not dialogue exposition
8. **Pacing rule: the closer to death, the slower the prose.** Chapters 10-13 = slowest, most detailed writing
