## CHAPTER 9 OPENING (Suggested)

*The Pattern Eye showed him everything.*

*Jesus sat in the teaching house's upper room, the bronze artifact resting in his palms—small enough to conceal in a fist, heavy enough to anchor him to reality when blood memory threatened to sweep him away. Dawn light filtered through the window, touching the geometric patterns etched into metal that had survived twelve hundred years.*

*Forty-one generations since Nefertari first encoded the Protocol. Forty-one unbroken links from Tausret's daughter to this Galilean carpenter who'd spent thirty-six years not knowing what he carried.*

*Now he knew too much.*

*The Pattern Eye pulsed in his hands—not physically, but in the blood memory space where inherited knowledge lived. He closed his eyes and let the cascade begin.*

*He saw Nefertari in the temple workshop, encoding defensive knowledge into bronze. Felt her certainty: Seven keys unlock the Protocol completely. Felt her fear: What if the keys are lost?*

*And then—a memory he'd never accessed before, even with partial Protocol access:*

*Nefertari speaking to her daughter: "The keys are tools for teaching, child. But a perfect carrier needs no tools. Forty generations of unbroken bloodline, every ancestor a carrier—that carrier would BE the Protocol. Living key. Complete access without bronze."*

*Jesus opened his eyes, breath coming fast.*

*Mary stood in the doorway, watching him with the expression she got when she recognized deep blood memory: concern mixed with documentation instinct.*

*"How long have you been here?" she asked quietly.*

*"Since before dawn." Jesus set the Pattern Eye aside carefully. His hands were shaking. "Mary, I think I understand something Nefertari knew. Something the networks have forgotten."*

*"About the keys?"*

*"About not needing them."*

*Mary crossed the room, sat across from him. "Explain."*

*And Jesus told her what blood memory had just revealed: That everything they believed about the bronze keys might be wrong.*
