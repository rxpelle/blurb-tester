### Weakest (B- to B average)
- **Voice & Narrative Persona (B-):** The most consistent problem. Six chapters score C+ (Ch 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13). The narrative voice lacks distinctiveness — too often reads as "competent literary fiction" rather than a specific person telling a specific story.
- **Dialogue (B):** Preface D+, Ch 12 C-, Ch 3/7/9/13 all B-. Characters sound too similar, exposition still leaks through dialogue.
- **Deep POV (B):** Ch 7 (C+) worst. Filtering words ("Marcus noticed," "Marcus recognized") still present. Not deep enough inside characters' heads.
- **Prose Polish (B):** Ch 1 C+. King's critique: overwriting, too many words, elaborate metaphors that call attention to themselves. "The prose should be invisible."

## New Problems Identified

1. **Voice flatness (B- avg):** The biggest remaining issue. The narrative voice is competent but anonymous. Chapters don't sound like they're told by a specific person with a specific worldview. Fix: strengthen POV-specific voice in each chapter's dominant narrator.
2. **Dialogue homogeneity:** Characters often sound alike. Peter, Thomas, Judas, Mary — their spoken cadences aren't distinct enough. Fix: character-specific speech patterns, idioms, rhythms.
3. **Prose overwriting:** King's consistent critique. Too many words, metaphors that announce themselves, passive voice, adverbial clutter. Fix: 10% cut per chapter focused on trimming, not adding.
4. **Deep POV filtering:** "Marcus noticed," "Mary recognized," "Jesus saw" — filtering language that creates distance. Fix: embed observations directly as thought/experience.
