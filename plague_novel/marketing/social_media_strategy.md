# Social Media Strategy for Author Platform

## PLATFORM PRIORITIES (Focus on 2-3 MAX)

### PRIMARY: X/Twitter
**Why:** Authors, readers, agents all active. Great for historical tidbits and writing updates.
**Time commitment:** 15-30 min/day
**Goal:** 500-1000 engaged followers by launch

### SECONDARY: Instagram
**Why:** Visual storytelling. Show medieval maps, manuscripts, research photos.
**Time commitment:** 3-4 posts/week
**Goal:** 300-500 followers by launch

### OPTIONAL: Goodreads
**Why:** Where readers live. Critical for reviews.
**Time commitment:** Weekly updates
**Goal:** 100+ "want to read" adds before launch

### SKIP (For Now): TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn
**Why:** Time sink. Focus effort where it matters. Add later if book takes off.

---

## CONTENT PILLARS (Rotate These)

### 1. Historical Facts (30% of content)
"Did you know medieval lock-picking was incredibly sophisticated? Thread on 14th-century security systems 🧵"

**Examples:**
- Real cipher systems from 1300s
- Plague statistics that sound unbelievable
- Medieval travel logistics
- Byzantine manuscript preservation
- How genetic memory actually works (vs. fiction)

### 2. Writing Process (25% of content)
"Cutting 5,000 words today. This is fine. Everything is fine. 🔥📚"

**Examples:**
- Word count updates
- Revision struggles
- Research rabbit holes
- "Today I Googled..." moments
- Reader feedback responses

### 3. Book Updates (20% of content)
"Chapter 3 is live on my website. Thomas meets Margarethe. It goes... poorly."

**Examples:**
- Cover reveals
- Sample chapter releases
- Character spotlights
- Map reveals
- Launch countdown

### 4. Community Building (15% of content)
"Question for historical thriller readers: What's the most ridiculous historical conspiracy you've heard?"

**Examples:**
- Questions to followers
- Polls (medieval vs. modern timeline preference)
- Retweet reader reactions
- Share other authors' books
- Participate in writing community hashtags

### 5. Personal/Behind-Scenes (10% of content)
"Writing about the Black Death while my kid has a cold. The irony is not lost on me."

**Examples:**
- Writing space photos
- Coffee/tea habits
- What you're reading
- Non-writing life (sparingly)
- Wins and struggles

---

## POSTING SCHEDULE

### X/Twitter (Daily)
- **Morning (7-9am):** Historical fact or writing update
- **Afternoon (2-4pm):** Engage with writing community, retweet readers
- **Evening (7-9pm):** Book update or community question

### Instagram (3x/week)
- **Monday:** Historical photo + caption (medieval maps, manuscripts)
- **Wednesday:** Writing process (desk setup, research books, edits)
- **Friday:** Book content (character art, cover reveals, quotes)

### Goodreads (Weekly)
- Update reading list
- Write reviews of similar books
- Post blog excerpts
- Answer reader questions


**Week 3: Share Content**
```
Day 1: "New blog post: The Real Conspiracies of Medieval Europe (Way Weirder Than Fiction) [LINK]"

```

## RED FLAGS TO AVOID

❌ Posting only about your book (spam)
❌ Buying followers (dead engagement)
❌ Being on every platform (burnout)
❌ Arguing about politics (alienates readers)
❌ Oversharing personal drama
❌ Ignoring comments/engagement
❌ Posting inconsistently (then vanishing)

✅ DO: Be helpful, interesting, and authentic
✅ DO: Engage more than you promote
✅ DO: Share others' work generously
✅ DO: Be consistent even if growth is slow

---

## METRICS TO TRACK

**Weekly:**
- Follower growth
- Engagement rate (likes + comments / followers)
- Link clicks to website
- Newsletter signups

**Monthly:**
- Best-performing posts (what resonated?)
- Worst-performing posts (what didn't work?)
- Time spent vs. results
- Adjust strategy accordingly

**Goal by Launch:**
- 500+ X followers
- 300+ Instagram followers
- 100+ newsletter subscribers
- 50+ Goodreads "want to read" adds
- 3-5 local bookstores interested in consignment

**Tip:** Historical thriller readers trend older (40-60). They prefer print 2:1 over ebook. Mentioning paperback availability increases conversion.
