## ARCHITECTURAL ENCODING VS DOT NOTATION

### Manuscript Dot Notation (476 CE - Present):
- **Invented**: Book 6, Ciarán of Clonmacnoise, ~476 CE
- **Method**: Colored dots in manuscript margins, decoded with bronze templates
- **Advantage**: Precise, detailed information encoding
- **Vulnerability**: Manuscripts can be burned, destroyed
- **Primary Use**: Books 1, 6, 7, 8 prominently feature this system

### Architectural Encoding (1258 CE - Present):
- **Developed**: Book 9, Maryam al-Qurtubi, post-Baghdad catastrophe
- **Method**: Mathematical proportions, tile patterns, water systems in buildings
- **Advantage**: Survives conquest, book burnings, regime changes
- **Vulnerability**: Requires blood-memory readers, buildings can be demolished
- **Primary Use**: Book 9 establishes this as response to manuscript vulnerability

### Strategic Complementarity:
- Dot notation = precise, distributable, detailed knowledge
- Architectural encoding = durable, conquest-resistant, infrastructure-based
- Both systems exist simultaneously after 1258 CE
- Defensive network uses both for redundancy
