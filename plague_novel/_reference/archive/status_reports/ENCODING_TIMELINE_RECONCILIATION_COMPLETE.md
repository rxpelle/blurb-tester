## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### **Semantic Encoding (Augustine's Method):**
- **Era:** 400-1600 CE
- **Medium:** Written texts (theology, philosophy)
- **Mechanism:** Multiple valid meanings
- **Survives:** Translation, copying variations
- **Encodes:** Methodology, philosophy, thinking patterns
- **Cannot encode:** Precise coordinates, specific dates

### **Dot Notation (Ciarán's Method):**
- **Era:** 500-1400 CE (peak usage)
- **Medium:** Manuscript margin decorations
- **Mechanism:** Colored dots + geometric patterns + physical keys
- **Survives:** Exact copying only
- **Encodes:** Locations, dates, connections, lists
- **Cannot encode:** Abstract philosophy easily

### **Why Both Needed:**
Different information requires different encoding:
- **Augustine** preserves "how to think" → semantic encoding
- **Ciarán** preserves "what to know" → dot notation
- **Together** = complete knowledge preservation system
