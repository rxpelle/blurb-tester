# CHAPTER 6 - THE EXTERNAL WAR

**Modern Timeline - Multiple Locations**
**November 18, 2019 - 48 Hours After Sarah's Capture**

---

## Scene 1: The Underground Network

**Boston Safe House - 11:47 PM**

Martin Reeves sat in a basement apartment three miles from GenVault headquarters, surrounded by screens showing security footage from seventeen federal buildings.

He'd been Maya Chen's handler for six years. CDC special investigations. Bioterrorism preparedness. Pattern recognition for emerging threats.

And now: the largest coordinated raid in CDC history.

Maya's last transmission had come in forty-seven hours ago. GPS tracker active. Sarah Chen inside GenVault. Morrison's office, twelfth floor, conference room B.

Then: nothing.

Tracker still transmitting. Location unchanged. No calls, no messages, no emergency signals.

Sarah was either deep undercover or dead.

Martin pulled up the tactical map Maya had compiled:

**THRESHOLD DEPLOYMENT HUBS (Confirmed)**
- Lagos, Nigeria - Hub 1 (23M population)
- Mumbai, India - Hub 2 (21M population)
- São Paulo, Brazil - Hub 3 (22M population)
- Karachi, Pakistan - Hub 4 (17M population)
- Cairo, Egypt - Hub 5 (21M population)
- Dhaka, Bangladesh - Hub 6 (22M population)
- Mexico City, Mexico - Hub 7 (22M population)
- Beijing, China - Hub 8 (21M population)
- Manila, Philippines - Hub 9 (14M population)
- Istanbul, Turkey - Hub 10 (16M population)

Ten hubs confirmed. Sarah had died—or been captured—before finding the other thirty-seven.

But ten was enough to start.

Martin's phone buzzed. Text from Dr. Elena Santos (WHO Geneva):

*"Hub 4 team in position. Karachi water facility secured. No resistance. Awaiting go-order."*

Seventeen teams. Seventeen cities. All positioned simultaneously.

Because if GenVault detected early raids, they'd accelerate deployment on remaining hubs.

Martin texted back: *"Stand by. Waiting on FBI green light."*

Another message came in—this one from Agent Torres (FBI Counter-Terrorism):

*"DOJ approved simultaneous warrants. Ready to execute 06:00 GMT."*

Four hours.

Martin pulled up the secure channel with all seventeen team leads:

**REEVES:** *All teams: Execute raids 06:00 GMT. Objectives: Secure water facilities, detain GenVault personnel, preserve pathogen samples for analysis. Rules of engagement: non-lethal unless fired upon. CDC biocontainment protocols apply.*

Acknowledgments flooded in from Mumbai, Lagos, São Paulo, Karachi, Cairo—

Martin's screen flickered.

New GPS signal. Sarah's tracker.

Moving.

Twelfth floor → elevator → basement level.

Then: stopped. Parking garage, Level B3.

Martin grabbed his phone. Called Maya directly.

Three rings.

"She's moving," Maya said immediately. "I see it."

"Basement parking. Could be transfer to secondary location."

"Or execution." Maya's voice was tight. "Morrison doesn't keep prisoners long-term."

"Do we abort raids? Extract Sarah first?"

Silence on the line. Then: "No. Sarah knew the risks. She went in to get hub locations. We have ten. That's 200 million people we can save right now."

"And Sarah?"

"If she's alive, raids will force Morrison's hand. He'll either negotiate or run." Maya paused. "If she's dead, we honor her sacrifice by stopping THRESHOLD."

Martin closed his eyes. Cold calculus. Brutal pragmatism.

Sarah Chen had walked into GenVault knowing she might not walk out.

"Raids proceed at 06:00 GMT," Martin confirmed. "All teams execute simultaneously."

"Acknowledged." Maya hung up.

Martin stared at the GPS dot on his screen. Still stationary. Parking garage, B3.

Either Sarah was about to escape.

Or Morrison was about to kill her.

Four hours until they'd know which.

## Scene 2: The WHO Coordination

**Geneva, Switzerland - WHO Emergency Operations Center**
**November 19, 2019 - 2:14 AM (Local Time)**

Dr. Elena Santos reviewed pathogen specifications for the fourteenth time.

**THRESHOLD Pathogen Profile (Estimated):**
- Vector: Waterborne retrovirus
- Incubation: 14-21 days
- Transmission: Direct contact + water supply
- Mortality: 90% (specific genetic profiles)
- Treatment: Unknown (likely none)

GenVault had engineered a plague that killed selectively. Targeted genetic markers correlated with historical defensive Protocol patterns.

People whose ancestors survived the Black Death.

The 1918 flu.

Bronze Age collapse.

262 million people marked for extinction because their DNA said they'd survived past catastrophes.

Elena pulled up the tactical deployment map. Seventeen teams positioned across four continents:

**AFRICA:**
- Lagos (Hub 1) - Team secured main water treatment facility
- Cairo (Hub 5) - Team positioned at Nile pumping stations
- Karachi (Hub 4) - Team at Lyari water plant

**ASIA:**
- Mumbai (Hub 2) - Team at Bhandup filtration complex
- Dhaka (Hub 6) - Team at Sayedabad treatment plant
- Beijing (Hub 8) - Team at Ninth Water Plant
- Manila (Hub 9) - Team at Balara facility
- Istanbul (Hub 10) - Team at Ömerli dam

**SOUTH AMERICA:**
- São Paulo (Hub 3) - Team at Guarapiranga reservoir
- Mexico City (Hub 7) - Team at Lerma system

Each team: three CDC biocontainment specialists, two local health officials, four federal law enforcement.

Each hub: one water treatment facility identified by Sarah's stolen GenVault data.

Elena's secure line buzzed. Director-General calling from home.

"Dr. Santos. Status?"

"All teams report ready. Warrants approved. Biocontainment protocols active."

"And the pathogen itself?"

Elena hesitated. "Unknown strain. Sarah Chen's data suggests retroviral modification—possibly incorporating endogenous viral elements. We won't know specifics until we secure samples."

"Can we contain it if they've already deployed?"

"If it's waterborne as suspected? Shut down treatment plants, quarantine affected populations, chlorination protocols." Elena pulled up containment models. "But we're talking about cities with populations over 15 million. If THRESHOLD activates before we raid—"

"Then 200 million people die," the Director-General finished. "Which is why raids happen at 06:00 GMT. Simultaneously. No advance warning."

"Understood."

"Dr. Santos? The woman who infiltrated GenVault—Dr. Chen—is she still alive?"

Elena checked the shared tactical feed. GPS tracker: still transmitting. Location: GenVault parking garage, B3 level.

Stationary for two hours.

"Unknown, sir. Her tracker's active but she hasn't made contact."

"If she survives this, WHO owes her a medal."

"If she survives this," Elena said quietly, "she'll have nightmares for the rest of her life."

The line went dead.

Elena returned to her screens. Three hours, forty-six minutes until raids executed.

And Sarah Chen's GPS dot hadn't moved in 127 minutes.

## Scene 3: The FBI Tactical Brief

**FBI Counter-Terrorism Division - Washington, D.C.**
**November 19, 2019 - 3:22 AM**

Special Agent Marcus Torres stood in front of a wall-sized tactical display showing seventeen cities lit up in red.

Forty-three agents filled the briefing room. Counter-terrorism specialists. Bioweapon response teams. Hostage rescue.

"At 06:00 GMT—exactly two hours and thirty-eight minutes from now—we execute simultaneous raids on GenVault facilities in seventeen cities across four continents."

Torres clicked to the next slide: Sarah Chen's photograph.

"This is Dr. Sarah Chen. MIT genetics PhD. She infiltrated GenVault Biopharma forty-eight hours ago carrying a CDC tracking device. Her mission: confirm THRESHOLD deployment hubs and gather intelligence on pathogen specifications."

Another click: GenVault headquarters, Boston.

"Dr. Chen's tracker is currently transmitting from GenVault's underground parking garage. She has not made contact in over two hours. We assess she is either detained, incapacitated, or maintaining cover."

An agent raised her hand. "Do we extract her before raids?"

"Negative. Extraction risks alerting GenVault to our operation. If they detect early intervention, they accelerate THRESHOLD deployment on the thirty-seven hubs we haven't identified yet."

"So we leave her there."

"We prioritize the mission," Torres said flatly. "Dr. Chen knew the risks. Her infiltration gave us ten confirmed hub locations. That's 200 million lives we can save right now."

Torres pulled up the hub list:

"Each team targets one water treatment facility. Objectives: secure facility, detain GenVault personnel, preserve pathogen samples for CDC analysis. Rules of engagement: non-lethal unless fired upon."

Another agent: "What if GenVault personnel resist?"

"They won't. According to Dr. Chen's intelligence, GenVault operates through shadow coordinators embedded in local infrastructure. They're scientists and engineers—not soldiers. We expect minimal resistance."

Torres clicked to a photo of Dr. James Morrison.

"This is Dr. James Morrison. GenVault CEO. Primary suspect in THRESHOLD conspiracy. If Dr. Chen is alive, she's likely in his custody. Do not—I repeat, do not—engage Morrison directly unless he poses immediate threat. We need him alive for interrogation."

The room fell silent.

Torres checked his watch: 3:26 AM. Two hours, thirty-four minutes until go-time.

"All teams: final equipment check at 04:00. Secure comms established at 05:00. Execute at 06:00 GMT on my mark."

Agents filed out.

Torres stayed behind, staring at Sarah Chen's photo on the screen.

Forty-eight hours ago, she'd walked into GenVault headquarters voluntarily. Alone. Wired with a GPS tracker and no backup.

Either the bravest person he'd ever met.

Or the most reckless.

Either way, she'd given them the intelligence to stop a genocide.

If she died in that parking garage, Torres would make damn sure it counted.

## Scene 4: The Thirty-Seven Missing Hubs

**Boston Safe House - 4:47 AM**

Martin Reeves couldn't sleep.

Sarah's GPS tracker: still transmitting. Still stationary. Parking garage, B3.

Four hours. No movement.

He pulled up the tactical map again. Ten hubs confirmed, seventeen teams positioned.

But GenVault's THRESHOLD deployment plan called for forty-seven hubs total.

**MISSING HUBS (Estimated Locations):**
- Jakarta, Indonesia (~11M)
- Tokyo, Japan (~14M)
- Delhi, India (~32M)
- Bangkok, Thailand (~11M)
- Tehran, Iran (~9M)
- Bogotá, Colombia (~11M)
- Lima, Peru (~11M)
- Kinshasa, DRC (~15M)
- Lahore, Pakistan (~13M)
- Khartoum, Sudan (~6M)

Thirty-seven cities. 300+ million people.

If raids succeeded on the first ten hubs, Morrison would accelerate deployment on the remaining thirty-seven. Sarah had said THRESHOLD was scheduled for seventy-two hours from her infiltration.

Which meant: twenty-four hours left.

Martin pulled up Maya's last message:

*"Sarah went dark. If raids trigger early deployment, we'll have saved 200M but killed 300M. Morrison will pull the trigger on every remaining hub simultaneously."*

Brutal math.

Save the people you can identify.

Sacrifice the people you can't.

Martin's phone buzzed. Text from Elena Santos (WHO Geneva):

*"All teams report ready. Execute in 73 minutes. God help us if Morrison has a dead man's switch."*

Martin typed back: *"Sarah said hubs deploy on schedule, not on trigger. If we move fast, we can shut down the network before Morrison adapts."*

*"And if you're wrong?"*

Martin stared at the GPS dot. Still stationary.

*"Then Sarah died for nothing, and 300 million people follow her."*

He sent the message.

Waited.

Elena's response came back thirty seconds later:

*"Raids proceed as planned. We trust Sarah's intel. She hasn't let us down yet."*

Martin looked at the clock: 4:51 AM.

Sixty-nine minutes until seventeen teams executed simultaneous raids across four continents.

And Sarah Chen's GPS tracker still hadn't moved.

## Scene 5: The Final Hour

**Multiple Locations - 5:45 AM GMT**

**Lagos, Nigeria:**
Dr. Amara Okonkwo stood outside the Adiyan Water Treatment Facility with four CDC biocontainment specialists and six Nigerian police officers.

"Facility processes 220 million gallons daily," Amara reported via secure channel. "Serves 18 million people. If GenVault deployed pathogen here, contamination would reach critical mass in 72 hours."

*"Stand by for go-order,"* Martin Reeves's voice crackled through her earpiece.

**Mumbai, India:**
Agent Priya Sharma watched the Bhandup Filtration Complex through night-vision goggles. Massive concrete structures. Pipes running underground to serve 21 million people.

*"Team 2, confirm position,"* Martin's voice.

"Confirmed. Perimeter secure. Ready to breach on your mark."

**São Paulo, Brazil:**
Colonel Andreas Silva positioned his team around Guarapiranga Reservoir. Water supply for 22 million people. If GenVault had contaminated this—

*"Team 3, status?"*

"Ready."

**Karachi, Beijing, Cairo, Dhaka, Mexico City, Manila, Istanbul—**

Seventeen teams. Seventeen water facilities. 200 million people.

All waiting on one man's order.

Martin Reeves checked the GPS tracker one final time.

Sarah Chen. Parking garage, B3. Stationary for 6 hours, 11 minutes.

He opened the secure channel to all seventeen teams:

"This is Reeves. Execute raids in two minutes. Objectives: secure facilities, detain GenVault personnel, preserve pathogen samples. Rules of engagement: non-lethal unless fired upon. Good luck."

Martin checked his watch.

5:59 AM.

Thirty seconds.

Twenty.

Ten.

He took a breath.

"All teams: Execute. Execute. Execute."

Doors breached.

Facilities secured.

GenVault personnel detained.

And in a parking garage in Boston, Sarah Chen's GPS tracker started moving again.

Toward the exit.

Toward freedom.

Or toward whatever James Morrison had planned next.
