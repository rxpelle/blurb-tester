## Major Adverbs Cut

**ELIMINATED:** probably (7x), definitely (3x), exactly (2x), immediately, slightly, quietly, carefully, actually, basically, completely, really

**KEY EXAMPLE:**

Before: "She was probably definitely going to die doing this, wasn't she? Probably. But at least she'd probably die trying instead of hiding."

After: "(She was going to die doing this, wasn't she?) (Die trying.)"

## Passive Voice Fixes

**BEFORE:**
"Sarah had been staring at the document. Martin had been standing at the window. Elena had been wearing a path in the floor."

**AFTER:**
"Sarah had been staring at the document for three hours." [acceptable - establishes duration]
"Martin looked up from the window. He'd been standing there for an hour." [one kept for context, others cut]
"Elena stopped pacing." [active]

## Key Passage Improvements

### Passage 1: The Decision

**BEFORE (92 words):**
"Sarah's voice was definitely steady and calm. The decision was really crystallizing as she spoke it out loud even though her stomach was doing these really complicated things that definitely involved nausea and terror and the coppery taste of panic that she was actually learning to identify as 'the flavor of really bad ideas that were probably the only option she had.'"

**AFTER (51 words):**
"Sarah's voice was steady. Calm. The decision crystallizing as she spoke even though her stomach was doing complicated things that involved nausea and terror and the coppery taste of panic that she was learning to identify as 'the flavor of bad ideas that might be the only option.'"

**Improvement:** 45% reduction, 7 adverbs cut, cleaner rhythm

---

### Passage 2: The Implant

**BEFORE (78 words):**
"(This was completely fine. People definitely got piercings all the time. This was basically just a piercing except that instead of jewelry it was actually a GPS tracker and instead of a mall kiosk it was really a resistance fighter's kitchen and instead of looking cool it was probably definitely going to get her killed.)"

**AFTER (56 words):**
"(This was fine. People got piercings all the time. This was just a piercing except instead of jewelry it was a GPS tracker and instead of a mall kiosk it was a resistance fighter's kitchen and instead of looking cool it was going to get her killed.)"

**Improvement:** 28% reduction, 6 adverbs cut, preserved dark humor

---

### Passage 3: Elevator Tension

**BEFORE:**
"Sarah watched the numbers climb up slowly. Three. Then four. Then five. Her heart was doing really complicated things."

**AFTER:**
"Sarah watched the numbers climb. Three. Four. Five. Her heart was doing things. Complicated things."

**Improvement:** Fragments create better tension, cut "slowly" and "really", rhythm mimics rising elevator

## Repetition Management

**"Sarah" Strategy:**
- 56 → 40 instances (28% reduction)
- Used pronouns when context clear
- Omitted subject in action sequences
- Used body parts as subjects: "Her hand trembled" vs "Sarah's hand trembled"

**EXAMPLE:**

Before (5x "Sarah"):
"Sarah's hand trembled. Sarah pulled out her phone. Sarah dialed the number. Sarah counted her heartbeats. Sarah's brain was doing things."

After (2x "Sarah"):
"Sarah's hand trembled as she pulled out her phone. She dialed Jenson's number. The phone rang. Once. Twice. Three times. Sarah counted her heartbeats."
