# CHAPTER 13 - THE FRAGMENTATION

**November 22, 2019 - Global Operations**
**9 days until THRESHOLD deployment**

---

**São Paulo, Brazil - 6:14 AM**

Elena Santos watched from across the street as fire trucks surrounded the Instituto Butantan.

Anonymous tip called in thirty minutes ago. Suspicious biological materials. Possible safety violation. Local fire marshal demanding entry for emergency inspection.

(Elena had made the call herself. Used a burner phone. Portuguese accent perfect after twenty years coordinating Brazilian resistance networks.)

The facility manager stood at the entrance arguing with firefighters. Behind him, through the windows, Elena saw people moving. Panic. Equipment being wheeled toward loading docks.

(Trying to relocate samples before inspectors found them.)

(Too late.)

The fire marshal pushed past the manager. Firefighters poured into the building. Twenty minutes later, they emerged carrying sealed containers marked with biohazard symbols.

Elena pulled out her encrypted phone. Texted Martin in Hartford:

**São Paulo secured. Samples recovered. Local authorities detaining facility personnel for questioning.**

She got in her car. Drove three blocks. Ditched the burner phone in a trash can.

One hub down. Forty-one remaining.

(The war had begun.)

---

**Tokyo, Japan - 9:47 PM**

Dr. Kenji Yamamoto stood outside Haneda Airport Cargo Terminal 2 with Japanese National Police.

He'd been awake for thirty-six hours. Ever since Inspector Tanaka called asking for help identifying suspicious biological research at the cargo facility.

(Tanaka didn't say how he knew about the facility. Yamamoto didn't ask. Some things were better left unspoken when stopping genocide.)

The cargo terminal was locked down. Corporate lawyers from "GenVault Holdings Japan" screaming about diplomatic immunity and illegal search. Inspector Tanaka holding firm with emergency health authority.

Yamamoto walked through the facility with biohazard containment team. Found the cryogenic storage in exactly the location Sarah Chen's intelligence specified.

Twelve sealed containers. Pathogen samples. Genetic targeting parameters written in technical shorthand that made Yamamoto's stomach turn.

(CCR5-Delta32 resistance. Targeting populations without European plague-survival markers. Weaponizing three thousand years of unequal selective pressure.)

"Secure everything," Tanaka ordered. "This is evidence for international criminal prosecution."

Yamamoto pulled out his phone. Texted Martin:

**Tokyo secured. 12 containers recovered. Corporate lawyers detained for obstruction.**

Another hub down.

Forty remaining.

---

**GenVault Executive Conference Room, Boston - 11:47 AM**

Wells stood at the windows watching snow fall.

Behind her, the conference room was chaos.

Dr. Sarah Okoro on the phone with Lagos hub coordinator. "I don't care about local authorities—relocate the samples NOW. Yes, I understand there are protesters outside. Relocate anyway."

Dr. James Wong coordinating Asian operations. "Shanghai hub is compromised. Singapore hub under investigation. Seoul secured but samples confiscated. We've lost three hubs in six hours."

Dr. Viktor Petrov managing European response. "Paris facility raided by Interpol. London facility shut down by Scotland Yard. Berlin coordinator is asking for evacuation protocols."

Wells turned to face them.

"How many hubs compromised?" she asked.

Morrison pulled up the operational map. Green dots spreading like infection across the display.

"Fourteen secured by authorities. Eight more under active investigation. Six coordinators have stopped responding to communications." His voice was flat. Clinical. "We're losing three hubs per hour."

"Defensive Protocol networks," Wells said. "Chen escaped yesterday. Twenty-four hours later, forty-two facilities are being raided simultaneously across four continents. That's not FBI coordination—that's distributed resistance networks acting on Chen's intelligence."

She pulled up Elena Santos's file. Brazilian activist. Resistance coordinator. Connected to fifteen local networks. Known associate of Martin Reeves.

"Santos is orchestrating this. Anonymous tips. Health inspections. Fire code violations. Environmental complaints. She's weaponizing local authorities against us without requiring international coordination."

"Clever," Okoro admitted. "No diplomatic complications. No warrant requirements. Just concerned citizens reporting suspicious biological facilities to local authorities who are obligated to investigate."

"It's defensive Protocol at scale," Morrison said quietly. "Distributed cooperative action. Multiple independent networks choosing prevention. Exactly what I was testing."

Wells turned on him. "You're TESTING? James, we're losing THRESHOLD. Chen stole deployment intelligence and is using resistance networks to shut us down hub by hub. This isn't a test—it's operational failure."

"It's proof," Morrison corrected. "That prevention works when networks cooperate. That defensive Protocol scales beyond individual action. That maybe—just maybe—we chose wrong."

The room went silent.

Okoro spoke carefully. "James, are you saying you want Chen to succeed?"

"I'm saying I gave her every advantage to prove prevention works." Morrison closed the operational map. Fourteen hubs lost. Twenty-eight under investigation. Five still secure. "If she stops THRESHOLD—if defensive Protocol networks prevent deployment through distributed cooperation—then thirty years of mathematics were wrong and genocide was unnecessary."

"And if we deploy anyway?" Petrov asked.

"Then I confirm that optimization beats prevention when stakes get big enough." Morrison looked at Wells. "Catherine, what's our minimum viable deployment?"

"Original plan required forty-seven hubs for global coverage," Wells said. "With redundancy, we could deploy with thirty-two hubs. Below that, coverage becomes spotty—we'd have geographic gaps, population centers that aren't reached, cascade effects that don't propagate properly."

"How many hubs do we still control?"

"Five secured. Eight relocatable if we move in the next twelve hours. Thirteen total." Wells pulled up the reduced deployment map. "Not enough for Phase I. We'd need to abort and rebuild infrastructure."

"How long to rebuild?"

"Eighteen months minimum. Two years realistically." Wells's voice was cold. "And that assumes Chen's networks don't locate our backup facilities. Assumes our internal personnel don't defect when they see defensive Protocol working. Assumes the Order survives the morale crisis of watching prevention succeed."

Morrison was quiet.

Then: "Chen has nine days until December 1st. If she secures thirty-two hubs—if she drops us below minimum viable deployment—THRESHOLD aborts."

"You're giving up," Wells said.

"I'm acknowledging reality. Chen proved defensive Protocol scales through distributed networks. If she stops deployment, she proves prevention works better than optimization."

"And Lagos?"

"Released if Chen succeeds. Executed if she fails." Morrison stood. Walked to the windows beside Wells. "Nine days, Catherine. Either Chen secures thirty-two hubs and proves me wrong, or we deploy with reduced coverage and prove genocide was necessary. Either way, we'll know."

Wells looked at the operational map. Five hubs secure. Eight relocatable. Thirteen total.

Nineteen hubs short of minimum deployment.

Chen winning.

"I'm not giving up," Wells said quietly. "I'm relocating the remaining eight hubs. Securing backup facilities. Holding the line until December 1st."

"And if Chen finds the backup locations?"

"Then you were right." Wells's voice was bitter. "That defensive Protocol works. That thirty years of planning were wrong. That we became monsters for nothing."

She left the conference room.

Morrison stood alone at the windows.

Watching snow fall.

Counting down.

Nine days until he knew whether Sarah Chen proved genocide was unnecessary or confirmed it had been inevitable all along.

---

**Hartford Safe House - 3:17 PM**

Sarah watched the operational map update in real-time.

Fourteen hubs secured. (Green dots spreading.)

Eight under active investigation. (Yellow dots flickering.)

Twenty-five sites reported to local authorities. (Orange dots pending.)

Forty-seven sites total. Nine days remaining.

Martin stood beside her coordinating with Elena's network. "São Paulo secured. Tokyo secured. Mumbai under investigation. Lagos authorities raiding facility now. Singapore samples confiscated. We're averaging three hubs per hour."

"Not fast enough," Sarah said. "Wells is relocating samples from exposed sites. We secure fourteen hubs, she moves eight to backup facilities. We're racing against her logistics."

"Then we move faster." Martin pulled up Elena's contact list. "She has networks in thirty-two cities. Local activists ready to report facilities. We activate all of them. Forty-seven simultaneous reports. Overwhelm Wells's ability to relocate."

"That burns every resistance contact Elena has. No operational security. No fallback positions."

"That's defensive Protocol," Martin said. "Distributed networks sacrificing operational security to prevent collapse. Exactly what Morrison's testing."

Sarah looked at Lagos's detention status. Still alive. Still held at GenVault. Survival dependent on whether Sarah stopped THRESHOLD before December 1st.

"Do it," she said. "Activate all thirty-two networks. Simultaneous operations. Make it impossible for Wells to relocate faster than we can raid."

Martin started making calls.

Sarah watched the map.

(Nine days. Forty-seven hubs. Race against Wells's logistics.)

(Lagos's life depending on whether they won.)

Her phone buzzed.

Morrison: **14 hubs secured. Impressive. Wells is relocating 8 to backup facilities. The test continues—can distributed networks move faster than centralized logistics? 9 days to find out.**

Sarah deleted the message.

Typed coordinates to Martin.

"Tell Elena: go loud. All networks. Every city. Simultaneous chaos. We end this in forty-eight hours or we lose."

Martin nodded. Started coordinating.

The operational map exploded with activity.

Thirty-two networks activated.

Forty-seven facilities reported.

Local authorities investigating.

Wells scrambling to relocate.

Defensive Protocol scaling globally.

The war accelerating.

(Nine days until Morrison learned whether prevention worked or genocide was inevitable.)

(Sarah racing against time to prove Lagos's sacrifice meant something.)
