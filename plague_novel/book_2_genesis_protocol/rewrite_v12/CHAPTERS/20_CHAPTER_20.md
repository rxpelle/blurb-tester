# CHAPTER 20 - THE TRIAL

**June 15, 2020 - International Criminal Court, The Hague**
**Six Months After THRESHOLD Aborted**

---

The courtroom was silent.

Morrison sat in the defendant's chair. No handcuffs. No restraints. Just a sixty-four-year-old man who'd spent thirty years building genocide and six months proving it had been unnecessary.

The prosecutor stood. "Dr. James Morrison, you're charged with conspiracy to commit genocide. Four point two billion intended casualties. How do you plead?"

Morrison looked at the gallery.

Sarah sat in the front row. Lagos beside her. Maya. Martin. Elena. Dr. Hassan. Dr. Yamamoto. Everyone who'd stopped THRESHOLD.

"Guilty," Morrison said.

The courtroom erupted.

The judge called for order. "Dr. Morrison, your attorney advised a not guilty plea pending cooperation negotiations—"

"I'm pleading guilty," Morrison interrupted. "To all charges. Conspiracy to commit genocide. Weapons development. Human rights violations. Every charge filed. Guilty."

His attorney stood. "Your Honor, I request recess to consult with my client—"

"Denied," Morrison said. "No recess. No consultation. I'm pleading guilty and testifying completely. Everything the Order did. Everyone involved. Full cooperation without reduced sentence negotiation."

The judge looked at Morrison's attorney. The attorney sat down, defeated.

"Proceed," the judge said.

Morrison stood. "I spent thirty years building THRESHOLD. Thirty years believing genocide was necessary to prevent collapse. Thirty years convinced that offensive deployment—killing four billion people with targeted genetics—was the only way to save humanity."

He pulled up evidence on the courtroom screens. Hub locations. Casualty projections. Genetic targeting parameters. Everything Sarah had memorized.

"The mathematics were clear," Morrison continued. "Population exceeded carrying capacity. Resource depletion accelerated. Climate collapse inevitable. Without intervention, seven billion deaths over thirty years. With THRESHOLD, four billion deaths immediately but three billion survivors positioned for recovery."

"The math was compelling. The math was wrong."

He pulled up Sarah's research. Defensive Protocol analysis. Distributed network coordination. Prevention versus optimization models.

"Dr. Sarah Chen proved it. Defensive Protocol prevents collapse through distributed cooperative networks without requiring genocide. She secured forty-seven hubs in eleven days using coordination across thirty-two countries. Prevented four point two billion deaths through prevention I claimed was impossible."

Morrison looked at Sarah. "She proved thirty years of mathematics were wrong. That my daughter died for nothing. That I became a monster unnecessarily. That defensive Protocol works when tested at scale."

"Your Honor, I'm guilty. Of conspiracy. Of planning genocide. Of sacrificing my humanity to mathematics that were wrong. I'm not requesting reduced sentence. I'm not negotiating cooperation. I'm testifying completely because Dr. Chen taught me that defensive Protocol demands complete truth even when partial truth would be easier."

The prosecutor approached. "Dr. Morrison, you're aware that complete testimony without cooperation agreement guarantees life imprisonment?"

"I'm aware."

"You're choosing life sentence over twenty years with cooperation?"

"I'm choosing complete truth over partial compromise." Morrison pulled up more evidence. "The Order has existed for two thousand years. Forty-seven operational hubs. One hundred sixty-three personnel. Financial networks across eighteen countries. Political influence in forty-two governments. I know everything. I'm testifying to everything. Not for reduced sentence. Because it's the truth."

"Why?" the prosecutor asked. "Why guarantee life imprisonment when cooperation offers release?"

Morrison looked at Sarah again. "Because she refused to stop at forty-six hubs. Risked David Lagos's life pushing for complete prevention. Forty-six would have prevented deployment. Forty-seven prevented rebuilding. She chose complete prevention over partial success even when it risked everything."

"I'm doing the same. Complete testimony over partial cooperation. Complete truth over comfortable compromise. If she can refuse pragmatism for principle, I can refuse reduced sentence for complete disclosure."

The judge was quiet. Then: "Dr. Morrison, this court accepts your guilty plea. Sentencing will follow complete testimony. You're remanded to custody pending trial proceedings."

"Thank you, Your Honor." Morrison sat down.

(Life imprisonment. Complete testimony. No compromise.)

(That was defensive Protocol applied to truth-telling.)

(That was what Sarah had taught him.)

---

**June 22, 2020 - One Week Later**

Morrison testified for six days straight.

Everything. The Order's history. Personnel networks. Financial structures. Political influence. Hub development. Casualty projections. Genetic targeting. Every operation. Every conspiracy. Every death.

Sarah sat in the gallery watching him destroy his own organization with complete honesty.

"Day six," Maya whispered. "He's documented forty-seven facilities, one hundred sixty-three personnel, and eighteen financial networks. Complete organizational exposure."

"He's guaranteeing life imprisonment," Martin added. "Cooperation deal would have given him twenty years. He's choosing principle over pragmatism."

"He learned that from you," Lagos said to Sarah. "Refusing to compromise even when compromise would be easier."

Sarah watched Morrison testify about Emily. About Melissa. About thirty years of mathematics that proved wrong. About becoming a monster to prevent worse monsters and discovering the monsters weren't necessary.

"He's not defending himself," Sarah said quietly. "He's explaining. There's a difference."

"What's the difference?" Maya asked.

"Defense is 'I was right.' Explanation is 'I was wrong but this is why.'" Sarah looked at Morrison. "He's not claiming THRESHOLD was necessary. He's explaining why he believed it was. That's not defense. That's confession."

Morrison continued. "I lost my daughter Emily to pediatric leukemia when she was seven years old. Twenty-seven years ago. I spent twenty-seven years building THRESHOLD believing her death could prevent billions more deaths. Believing that if I optimized human genetics fast enough, other children wouldn't die like Emily died."

"I was wrong. Emily died because she had leukemia. Not because I failed to engineer genocide fast enough. Her death was a tragedy. I turned tragedy into mathematics because mathematics felt better than meaninglessness. I built THRESHOLD to make her death mean something. Dr. Chen proved her death meant nothing. That THRESHOLD was unnecessary. That I sacrificed thirty years and my humanity for mathematics that were wrong."

He looked directly at Sarah. "Thank you for that. Thank you for proving Emily didn't need THRESHOLD. That her death was just a death. That I didn't need to become a monster to honor her. That's painful truth. But it's truth. And defensive Protocol demands truth even when truth hurts."

The courtroom was silent.

Morrison finished his testimony.

Sat down.

Waited for sentencing.

---

**June 29, 2020 - Sentencing**

The judge read the verdict.

"Dr. James Morrison, this court finds you guilty of conspiracy to commit genocide. Planning to kill four point two billion people through targeted biological weapons. The scale of intended casualties is unprecedented in human history."

"However, this court acknowledges your complete cooperation. Your testimony exposed the entire Order network. Your evidence enabled arrests of one hundred sixty-three additional conspirators. Your disclosure prevented future threats."

"Standard sentencing would be life imprisonment without possibility of parole."

Morrison sat still. Steady. Accepting.

"However," the judge continued, "this court also acknowledges that no casualties occurred. Zero deaths from THRESHOLD deployment. Dr. Chen's intervention prevented the genocide you planned."

"Therefore, this court sentences you to life imprisonment with possibility of parole after thirty years. You will be seventy-four years old at earliest release consideration. You will serve full sentence unless granted clemency for continued cooperation."

"Do you have anything to say, Dr. Morrison?"

Morrison stood. "Yes, Your Honor. I accept this sentence. I deserve this sentence. I planned to kill four billion people because I believed it was necessary. Dr. Chen proved it wasn't. I was wrong. I accept the consequences."

He looked at the gallery. At Sarah. Lagos. Maya. Martin.

"Thank you," he said. "For stopping me. For proving me wrong. For saving four billion people I would have killed. For teaching me that defensive Protocol demands complete prevention even when partial success would be easier."

He sat down.

The judge banged the gavel. "Court is adjourned."

Guards escorted Morrison out.

Sarah sat in the gallery watching him leave.

"Thirty years minimum," Maya said. "He'll be seventy-four. Might die in prison."

"He chose that," Lagos said. "Could have taken cooperation deal. Twenty years. Out at sixty-four. He refused. Chose complete testimony over reduced sentence."

"Defensive Protocol," Sarah said quietly. "He applied my lesson. Complete truth over partial compromise. Even when it cost him fourteen extra years."

"Was he right to do that?" Martin asked.

Sarah thought about that. "I don't know. But it was principled. Complete testimony exposed the entire Order. His cooperation deal would have protected some personnel in exchange for his reduced sentence. He chose complete exposure over personal benefit."

"That's defensive Protocol," Lagos said. "Choosing collective good over individual benefit even when individual benefit would be easier."

They left the courtroom.

Morrison sentenced. Order exposed. THRESHOLD aborted.

(Justice served.)

(Or as close to justice as preventing genocide allowed.)

---

**Federal Prison - That Night**

Morrison sat in his cell writing to Sarah.

**Dr. Chen—**

**Thank you for attending the trial. Thank you for stopping THRESHOLD. Thank you for proving thirty years of mathematics were wrong.**

**I know this sounds strange, but your proving me wrong was the kindest thing anyone's done for me in thirty years. You proved Emily's death didn't require genocide. That I didn't need to become a monster to honor her. That's painful. But it's truth.**

**I'm serving life with parole possibility at thirty years. I'll be seventy-four. I'll probably die in prison. That's appropriate. That's just. I planned to kill four billion people. Life imprisonment is getting off easy.**

**But I want you to know: I'm grateful. For the first time in thirty years, I know Emily died because she had leukemia. Not because I failed to build THRESHOLD fast enough. Her death was meaningless. That used to terrify me. Now it's relief.**

**I don't have to make her death mean something through genocide. I can just miss her. Mourn her. Remember her without turning her into justification for mathematics.**

**You gave me that. By proving me wrong. By stopping THRESHOLD. By showing me defensive Protocol works.**

**Coffee is terrible in federal prison. Worse than GenVault. But it's consistently terrible. And consistency matters when you're serving life.**

**Thank you, Dr. Chen. For everything.**

**- James Morrison**

He sealed the letter. Gave it to the guard for mailing.

Sat back on his cot.

Thirty years minimum. Probably life.

But at least he knew Emily's death meant nothing.

At least the mathematics were honest.

At least defensive Protocol proved true.

(That was worth life imprisonment.)
