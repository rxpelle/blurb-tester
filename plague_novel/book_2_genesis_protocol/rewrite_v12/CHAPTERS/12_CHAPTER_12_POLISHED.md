# CHAPTER 12 - THE EXTERNAL WAR

**Hartford Safe House**
**November 21, 2019 - 2:47 PM**
**10 days until THRESHOLD deployment**

---

Sarah had been talking for six hours.

FBI Special Agent Rebecca Torres sat across from her with a laptop, three legal pads, and a coffee that had gone cold two hours ago. Recording everything. Hub locations. Security protocols. Casualty projections. Deployment vectors.

Forty-seven hubs.

Sarah recited them from memory. Atlanta first. Then Chicago. Los Angeles. Seattle. Working through the list methodically while Torres typed and Maya Chen cross-referenced against genetic distribution data and Martin Reeves built probability models for simultaneous raids.

"Hub 23: São Paulo, Brazil," Sarah said. Her voice was hoarse. Six hours of continuous debriefing. "Instituto Butantan, Sublevel 3. Biometric plus retinal plus voice recognition security. Pathogen storage in cryogenic units marked 'Research Sample - Virology Department.' Projected casualties: 52.1 million across South American corridor."

Torres kept typing. "Deployment timing?"

"December 1st, 0400 local time. Synchronized with Atlanta hub to create simultaneous Western Hemisphere outbreak."

"Security response protocols?"

"Lethal force authorized. Armed guards 24/7. If breach is detected, facility locks down and samples get destroyed. We'll have one chance to raid each location—if they know we're coming, they'll eliminate evidence and relocate to backup sites."

Torres stopped typing. Looked at Sarah. "You're saying we need to hit all forty-seven locations simultaneously? Coordinated across four continents?"

"Yes."

"That's—" Torres shook her head. "That's impossible. International coordination alone takes weeks. Warrants. Diplomatic clearances. Local authorities. We can't move that fast."

"Then 4.2 billion people die." Sarah's voice was flat. Dead. "Morrison gave me eleven days. If we don't shut down all forty-seven hubs before December 1st, THRESHOLD deploys and everything I did—everything Lagos sacrificed—means nothing."

Maya looked up from her genetic data. "Rebecca, we don't need perfect coordination. We need chaos. Hit eight hubs immediately—the ones in friendly jurisdictions. Make noise. Force the Order to react. While they're scrambling to protect the obvious targets, we hit the remaining thirty-nine."

"That still requires international cooperation I can't deliver in eleven days."

"Then we go around official channels." Martin pulled up his resistance network contacts. "Elena Santos in Hartford has connections. Inspector Dubois at Interpol. Dr. Kim in Seoul. Scotland Yard contacts who worked the breeding program investigation. We use the networks Sarah built getting here."

Torres was quiet for a long moment.

Then: "If I coordinate unofficial operations without State Department approval, I'm violating about seventeen federal laws."

"If you don't, you're complicit in genocide," Sarah said quietly.

Torres looked at her laptop. At forty-seven hub locations typed from Sarah's memory. At casualty projections totaling 4.237 billion deaths. At deployment protocols that would start killing people in ten days unless someone stopped it.

"Fuck it," Torres said. "Let's go commit some international incidents."

She picked up her phone. Started dialing.

---

**6 Hours Later - FBI Emergency Operations Center, Quantico**

Torres stood in front of a wall of monitors showing eight simultaneous operations.

**ATLANTA - 08:17 EST**: FBI tactical team breaching Cargo Facility 7 at Hartsfield-Jackson Airport. Biohazard containment active. CDC personnel ready.

**CHICAGO - 08:19 EST**: Federal warrant executed at O'Hare South Cargo Complex. Airport security cooperating. No resistance.

**LOS ANGELES - 05:23 PST**: LAPD SWAT plus CDC entering LAX Terminal B. Air filtration access point located.

**SEATTLE - 05:31 PST**: Sea-Tac maintenance tunnels secured. Moving to International Terminal Level 3.

Four domestic operations proceeding.

The international operations were messier:

**TOKYO - 22:42 JST**: Japanese National Police coordinating with airport security. Corporate lawyers from "GenVault Holdings Japan" claiming diplomatic immunity.

**SEOUL - 23:15 KST**: South Korean authorities executing emergency health inspection warrant. Facility management resisting entry.

**PARIS - 14:33 CET**: Interpol plus French National Police at Charles de Gaulle. Legal challenges delaying access to Terminal 2E.

**LONDON - 13:47 GMT**: Scotland Yard at Heathrow. GenVault UK lawyers blocking Terminal 5 entry with injunctions.

Torres's phone rang. Field supervisor from Atlanta.

"We found it," he said. Voice tight. "Cryogenic storage exactly where Chen said. Twelve sealed containers. Biohazard markers. CDC running preliminary analysis now."

Torres felt cold wash through her. It was real. All of it. Sarah's intelligence was accurate.

"Secure everything," Torres ordered. "Full containment. No samples leave FBI custody. Document everything—this is evidence for a genocide trial."

"Chicago and LA are reporting similar findings. Seattle's still searching but confident they'll locate storage within the hour."

Three confirmed. Possibly four. Out of eight.

Torres pulled up her operational map. Eight dots turning green as sites were secured. Thirty-nine red dots remaining.

Her second phone rang. Martin Reeves from Hartford.

"I'm watching the feeds," Martin said. "Four domestic hubs secured. International operations hitting legal resistance."

"Corporate lawyers," Torres confirmed. "GenVault has contingency plans. They're using diplomatic immunity claims and corporate ownership structures to slow us down."

"Which buys them time to relocate samples from the remaining thirty-nine hubs."

"Exactly." Torres watched Paris operation stall on legal challenges. "We need the other thirty-nine locations hit before GenVault realizes the scope of what we're doing."

"Elena's coordinating." Martin pulled up his network map. "She's got resistance fighters positioned at fifteen locations. Not official operations—local activists who'll make sure those facilities get 'anonymously' reported to health authorities for suspicious biological research."

"That's not legal."

"Neither is genocide." Martin's voice was flat. "We go loud on eight sites. GenVault panics, tries to protect their major hubs. Meanwhile Elena's people trigger health inspections, fire code violations, environmental complaints at thirty-nine 'random' facilities that happen to be THRESHOLD storage locations."

Torres looked at her operational map.

At eight green dots. Thirty-nine red dots. Ten days until deployment.

"Do it," she said. "I'll handle the diplomatic fallout later."

"Copy. Elena's activating her network now." Martin paused. "Sarah wants to know about Lagos. Any word from GenVault?"

"Morrison's holding him. Not executed—detained. Wells wanted immediate termination but Morrison overruled her."

"Why would Morrison protect someone who helped Sarah escape?"

"Because Morrison's running an experiment," Torres said quietly. "Lagos bet his life that defensive Protocol works. Morrison's keeping him alive until we prove it does or doesn't."

"So Lagos lives if we stop THRESHOLD?"

"If we stop all forty-seven hubs. If we prove prevention works at scale. If we demonstrate that cooperative networks prevent collapse better than optimized genocide." Torres watched another hub turn green on her map. "No pressure."

Martin laughed. Dark. Exhausted. "Five hubs secured. Forty-two remaining. Ten days. We've had worse odds."

"Have we?"

"No," Martin admitted. "But Sarah beat five percent probability this morning. Maybe we can beat this too."

The line went dead.

Torres watched her monitors.

Five hubs secured now. Seattle confirmed. Forty-two remaining.

Her operations coordinator looked over. "Next wave?"

"Launch it," Torres said. "Elena's network hits fifteen locations in six hours. Local authorities, anonymous tips, health inspections. Make them think it's random. Make GenVault scramble trying to figure out which sites are compromised."

"And the remaining twenty-seven?"

"We'll get to them." Torres pulled up international contacts. Inspector Dubois at Interpol. Dr. Kim in Seoul. Scotland Yard. MI6. "I'm going to make some very uncomfortable phone calls."

She started dialing.

Ten days to stop forty-two hubs before THRESHOLD deployed and 4.2 billion people died.

(Lagos's survival depending on whether they succeeded.)

(Morrison watching, waiting to see if defensive Protocol worked.)

(Sarah's sacrifice meaning something only if they moved fast enough.)

Torres made her calls.

The war had begun.

---

**Hartford Safe House - 11:47 PM**

Sarah couldn't sleep.

Maya had gone to bed two hours ago. Martin was in the operations room coordinating with Elena. FBI operations continuing overnight.

Sarah sat alone in the small bedroom staring at her phone.

Five hubs secured. Forty-two remaining. Lagos detained at GenVault, fate dependent on whether Sarah's intelligence stopped THRESHOLD before December 1st.

(Ten days. Forty-two hubs. Four hubs per day. International coordination that normally took months compressed into days.)

Her phone buzzed.

Encrypted message. Unknown sender.

She opened it.

**Eight hubs compromised. You move faster than expected. Wells is relocating samples from exposed sites to backup facilities. I'm not stopping her. The test continues: can defensive Protocol prevent collapse when the target is moving? You have ten days. Make them count. - Morrison**

Sarah stared at the message.

Morrison wasn't helping them. Wasn't stopping Wells from relocating samples. Wasn't making it easier.

(Because the test wasn't whether Sarah could stop THRESHOLD when Morrison cooperated. The test was whether prevention worked when optimization resisted.)

She typed a response:

**Lagos?**

Morrison's reply came three minutes later:

**Alive. Detained. Wells wants him executed. I'm holding her off until December 1st. If you stop THRESHOLD, he lives. If you fail, you prove me right and he dies knowing defensive Protocol failed when tested at scale. No pressure.**

Sarah deleted the conversation.

Looked at the map Martin had left on the desk. Forty-two red dots. Five green dots. Ten days counting down.

(Lagos alive. Morrison watching. Wells relocating samples to backup facilities. The Order fighting back with every resource they had.)

Sarah picked up her phone. Called Martin in the operations room.

"We need to move faster," she said. "Morrison just confirmed Wells is relocating samples from exposed sites. The forty-two remaining hubs aren't static targets—they're moving."

"Fuck." Martin's exhaustion was audible. "That changes the timeline. If Wells relocates samples, we lose the intelligence advantage."

"Then we hit everything at once. Forty-two simultaneous operations. Tomorrow. Before Wells finishes relocating."

"Sarah, that's impossible. We don't have authorization. Don't have tactical teams. Don't have—"

"We have Elena's network. We have resistance fighters at fifteen locations. We have local activists who'll report suspicious facilities to health authorities. We have international contacts who'll help if we ask." Sarah's voice was steady. Cold. "We don't need perfect operations. We need chaos. Forty-two facilities reported for biological safety violations in twenty-four hours. Local authorities investigate. Samples get discovered. Order can't relocate if everyone's watching."

Martin was quiet.

Then: "That's not an FBI operation. That's global guerrilla activism."

"That's defensive Protocol working at scale. Cooperative networks preventing collapse through distributed action. Isn't that what Morrison's testing?"

"Yes," Martin admitted. "But it's also wildly illegal, diplomatically catastrophic, and likely to start international incidents."

"So is genocide." Sarah looked at the map. Forty-two targets. Ten days. "Make the calls, Martin. Elena's network. Every contact. Every resistance fighter. Every person who believes stopping THRESHOLD is worth risking everything. We go loud tomorrow. All forty-two sites. Simultaneous chaos."

"And if it fails? If Wells relocates before we hit them?"

"Then Lagos dies and Morrison was right all along—that optimization beats prevention when the stakes get big enough." Sarah's hands weren't shaking anymore. Just cold. Steady. Determined. "But we're not failing. We're proving defensive Protocol works. Tomorrow. Forty-two sites. Final push."

Martin sighed. "I'll call Elena. Start coordinating. This is going to be messy."

"Everything worth doing is messy."

She hung up.

Looked at the map one more time.

(Tomorrow. Forty-two simultaneous operations. Every resistance contact activated. Every network mobilized. Defensive Protocol tested at global scale.)

(Lagos's life depending on whether it worked.)

(4.2 billion lives depending on whether they stopped THRESHOLD before December 1st.)

Sarah lay down on the bed.

(Still couldn't sleep. Too much at stake. Too much to do. Tomorrow coming too fast.)

But she closed her eyes anyway.

Let exhaustion take her.

Ten days counting down.
