## Major Adverbs Cut

**ELIMINATED:** definitely, probably, actually (3x), genuinely (2x), slightly (2x), exactly, literally, really

**KEY EXAMPLES:**

Before: "(She was definitely looking like that.) (Morrison probably knew she was looking like that.)"
After: "(She was looking like that.) (Morrison knew she was looking like that.)"

Before: "Morrison genuinely believed he was saving the world. She was actually terrified he might be genuinely right."
After: "Who genuinely believed he was saving the world." [kept one for emphasis] "And she was terrified he might be right."

## Key Passage: The Trolley Problem

**BEFORE (82 words):**
"Her brain was trying really hard to process the numbers but it was failing completely. Actually crashing. Because how do you actually choose between two different versions of apocalypse when both of them are completely terrible?"

**AFTER (46 words):**
"Her brain was trying to process the numbers. Failing. Crashing. Because how do you choose between two versions of apocalypse?"

**Why This Works:**
- Cut adverbs: "really", "actually" (2x), "completely" (2x)
- Fragments show brain malfunction
- Question is sharper, more direct
- 44% shorter, more impact
