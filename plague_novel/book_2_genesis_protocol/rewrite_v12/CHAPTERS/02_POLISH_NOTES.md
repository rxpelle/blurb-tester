## Major Cuts and Fixes

### 1. Adverb Elimination

**CUT:** definitely, probably, basically, actually, really, carefully, exactly, finally, just, nearly, simply, still, very

**EXAMPLES:**

Before: "Sarah was definitely planning a break-in and she was basically standing there like an idiot."
After: "(She was planning a break-in.)"

Before: "She tried walking very carefully on the edges where there was less creaking."
After: "She tried walking on the edges—less creaking."

Before: "Maya's face went really pale. Actually, not just pale. Very gray."
After: "Maya's face went pale. Not just pale. *Gray*."

---

### 2. Passive Voice Fixes

**BEFORE:**
"MIT had been very educational about this particular skill that was taught in their security seminars."

**AFTER:**
"MIT had been educational."

**BEFORE:**
"The files were being loaded by Maya and analysis was being run on the data."

**AFTER:**
"Maya loaded the files. Started running analysis."

---

### 3. Sticky Sentence Reduction

**BEFORE (glue ratio: 52%):**
"Sarah's brain was doing this thing where it was trying to process all of the information that was coming at her and it kept hitting walls that were like a mouse that was in a maze that had just discovered that all of the exits that it could see were leading back to the center that it started from."

**AFTER (glue ratio: 31%):**
"Her brain was doing this thing where it tried to process information and kept hitting walls. Like a mouse in a maze that discovered all the exits led back to the center."

---

### 4. Repetition Management

**"Sarah" Reduction Strategy:**
- Used "she" when subject is clear from context
- Omitted subject in fragment sentences
- Used actions instead of names ("Pulled the door open" vs "Sarah pulled")

**EXAMPLE:**

Before (7x "Sarah"):
"Sarah pulled the door open. Sarah slipped inside. Sarah closed it behind her. Sarah's pulse hammered. Sarah needed to stop thinking like that. Sarah found the stairs. Sarah started down."

After (2x "Sarah"):
"She pulled the door open. Slipped inside. Closed it behind her.

Sarah's pulse hammered in her throat.

(She needed to stop thinking like that. Paranoia was useful when running from Morrison but counterproductive when breaking into a library to steal century-old documents that might—*might*—contain the only evidence that could stop genocide.)"

---

### 5. Dialogue Cleanup

**BEFORE:**
"Dr. Maya Chen," she said, introducing herself. "CDC Emerging Infectious Diseases division," she added, explaining her credentials.

**AFTER:**
"Dr. Maya Chen. CDC Emerging Infectious Diseases division." (Description replaces attribution)

**Kept Natural Rhythm:**
- Removed unnecessary "said" when action shows who's speaking
- Preserved attributions when needed for clarity
- Used voice description once: "A man's voice. Cautious."

## Key Passages: Before/After

### Passage 1: The Break-In

**BEFORE (134 words):**
"Sarah very carefully pulled the door open and then she slipped inside as quietly as she possibly could and then she closed it behind her, making sure it was shut properly. The silence that was inside the library hit her like it was actually a physical thing that had mass and weight. Libraries after hours always had this quality of quiet that felt really wrong to Sarah—it was like the absence of sound was creating its own kind of noise that filled the space."

**AFTER (57 words):**
"She pulled the door open. Slipped inside. Closed it behind her.

The silence hit like a physical thing.

Libraries after hours had a quality of quiet that felt wrong—like the absence of sound was its own noise."

**Improvement:** 57% reduction, Grade 10 → Grade 6, zero adverbs, active verbs throughout

---

### Passage 2: Meeting Maya

**BEFORE (98 words):**
"Sarah's stomach basically dropped. It just dropped straight down. It was like the floor had completely disappeared underneath her and she was falling through space except that her feet were somehow still on the ground and that actually made it feel worse somehow because her body couldn't figure out what was happening to it."

**AFTER (45 words):**
"Sarah's stomach dropped.

Just. Dropped.

Like the floor had disappeared and she was falling except her feet were still on the ground and that made it worse somehow."

**Improvement:** 54% reduction, fragments create physical sensation, no adverbs
