**What makes it work:**

1. **Visceral horror** - Container dropped, seal fails, vapor spreading, people exposed
2. **Real-time escalation** - Death toll climbs throughout: 3 → 73 → 127 → 189 → 247 → 312 → 357 → 389 → 412
3. **News coverage** - Sarah watches live, can't look away, counts every death
4. **Personalization** - Fatima Al-Rashid, age 34, medical coordinator, mother of Noor and Hassan
5. **Interview scene** - Children crying "I want my mama" breaks the heart
6. **Funeral scene** - Sarah watches burial, sees children at mother's grave
7. **Sarah's guilt** - "I killed their mother. Someone needs to answer for Fatima. That someone is me."

**The accident transforms everything:**
- Stakes no longer abstract (not "4 billion number" but "Fatima's face")
- Morrison's offers genuinely tempting ("Stop before you kill more mothers")
- Sarah's choice impossible (risk 800 deaths vs. Wells rebuilding)
- Victory bittersweet (47/47 secured but 412 dead)
- Sarah carries visible scars (remembers every name forever)
