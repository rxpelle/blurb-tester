- Stakes felt abstract (4 billion number)
- Choices felt academic
- Middle pacing sagged

**New Structure (V2):**
- Chapters 12-14 (3 chapters, ~10,400 words)
- Wuhan outbreak (Hub 47) adds visceral stakes
- Li Mei family personalizes cost
- GenVault survival and profiteering adds corporate horror
- Morrison's suspicious death adds moral complexity
- Middle devastates instead of sags

## KEY THEMES

1. **Prevention vs. Optimization**
   - Defensive Protocol (prevent collapse) vs. Offensive Protocol (engineer survivors)
   - Sarah proves prevention works imperfectly but works

2. **Complete vs. Partial Success**
   - 98% success (46/47 hubs) = 2% failure
   - Hub 47 escaped, causing global pandemic
   - 35M dead from the one hub that got away
   - Complete threat elimination or catastrophic failure

3. **Cost of Incomplete Prevention**
   - Wuhan: 35M dead over 5 years (Li Mei family and millions more)
   - GenVault profits $189B from pandemic they created
   - Morrison's suspicious death ("convenient timing, unprovable murder")
   - Wells escapes with Archive, still at large
   - "35M dead AND 4B saved AND justice failed"

4. **Institutional Power Surviving**
   - GenVault survives: only 12 executives detained, company intact
   - Develops vaccine using THRESHOLD research to profit
   - Morrison takes sole blame as "rogue geneticist"
   - Order scattered but ideology embedded in corporate structures
   - Individuals blamed while institutions endure

5. **Visible Scars**
   - Sarah watches pandemic death tolls climb
   - Morrison dies suspiciously, Sarah believes murdered
   - Coffee tastes wrong after 5 years
   - "Always yes" - because 4B saved, despite 35M dead
   - "That's what you did when you saved the world imperfectly"
