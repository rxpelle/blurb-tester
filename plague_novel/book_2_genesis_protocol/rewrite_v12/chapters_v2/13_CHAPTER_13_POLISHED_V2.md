# CHAPTER 13 - THE SPREAD

**November 24-December 1, 2019**
**Week One: Wuhan Outbreak**
**46 hubs secured, 1 hub escaped**

---

**Hour 60 - GenVault Headquarters, Detention Wing**
**November 24, 2019 - Morning**

Morrison sat in detention reading Sarah's message.

**Tell me what escaped. Pathogen characteristics. Transmission vectors. Mortality rates. Help me contain it or prove you only care about being right.**

He'd spent thirty years building THRESHOLD. Thirty years believing genocide was necessary. Thirty years convinced that offensive deployment was the only way to prevent collapse.

Sarah had proved him wrong. Secured forty-six hubs in seventy-two hours using distributed networks he'd claimed were impossible to coordinate. Prevented 4.15 billion deaths through defensive Protocol he'd spent decades arguing against.

Forty-six out of forty-seven.

But Hub 47 had escaped. Wuhan facility evacuated too early. Containment breach during transport. Pathogen released at Huanan Market five days ago.

Seventeen infected. Three dead. Numbers climbing.

Morrison pulled out pen and paper. Started writing.

**THRESHOLD Hub 47 (Wuhan) - Technical Specifications:**

**Pathogen designation**: SARS-CoV-2 variant, engineered for enhanced transmission
**Primary transmission**: Respiratory droplets, airborne in enclosed spaces
**Incubation period**: 2-14 days (median 5 days)
**Symptomatic progression**: Fever, cough, respiratory distress, pneumonia, ARDS in severe cases
**Mortality rate**: 2-4% overall population, 15-25% in genetically vulnerable groups
**Genetic targeting**: ACE2 receptor affinity, enhanced binding in East Asian genetic markers
**Projected spread**: Exponential growth, R0 approximately 2.5-3.5 without intervention
**Containment window**: 14-21 days before community transmission becomes uncontrollable

**Estimated casualties if uncontained**: 35-50 million over 24-36 months

Morrison stared at the numbers.

Thirty-five to fifty million dead. Not the eighty-nine million Hub 47 was designed to kill, but still catastrophic. Still millions of people dying because Wells had panicked and evacuated Wuhan too quickly.

He'd built this. He'd designed THRESHOLD to be precise, controlled, optimization through elimination.

And now it was spreading as chaos. Uncontrolled outbreak. Exactly what he'd spent thirty years trying to prevent.

(Sarah was right. Offensive Protocol always fails in practice. Even when the mathematics are perfect.)

He signed the document. Wrote across the bottom: **Release Lagos. I'll provide everything.**

Then he called for the guard.

"Get this to FBI immediately. Tell them I'm ready to cooperate completely. Full intelligence. Every detail. No conditions except one: David Lagos goes free."

The guard took the document. Left.

Morrison sat back.

(Thirty years building genocide. Four days watching Sarah prove it was unnecessary. Now helping her contain the outbreak his own weapon created.)

(Defensive Protocol in action. What being wrong looked like when you chose to learn from it.)

(Emily would have hated him. Sarah was trying to save the people Emily would have wanted saved.)

He closed his eyes.

Waited for FBI interrogation to begin.

---

**Hour 64 - Wuhan Municipal Hospital**
**November 24, 2019 - Afternoon**

Li Mei died at 2:47 PM.

Patient Zero. Age thirty-four. Mother of Jian (7) and Xiao (4). Forty-seven hours from first symptoms to death.

Morrison's mathematics made flesh.

---

**Hour 66 - Hartford Safe House**

Sarah watched the news.

Chinese state media reporting four deaths from "unusual pneumonia" at Huanan Market. Twenty-three cases confirmed. Forty-seven suspected. Investigation ongoing.

Four deaths. Including Li Mei.

Sarah had found Li Mei's name in Chinese CDC reports. Age thirty-four. Produce vendor. Mother of two children. Died 2:47 PM Wuhan time from respiratory failure.

Children Jian (7) and Xiao (4) in critical condition.

Sarah opened her tracking file. Added:

**Li Mei - Age 34, Produce vendor, Huanan Market. Deceased November 24, 2:47 PM. Children: Jian (7) and Xiao (4), both infected, critical condition.**

She stared at the entry.

(Li Mei died because Sarah was too slow securing Hub 47. Died because Wells evacuated Wuhan before the raids began. Died because Sarah secured forty-six hubs but missed the forty-seventh.)

Her phone buzzed. Torres.

"Morrison's cooperating. Full intelligence on Hub 47. Pathogen specifications, transmission characteristics, mortality projections. He's giving us everything."

"In exchange for Lagos?"

"Yes. Morrison says forty-six hubs secured proves defensive Protocol works. Lagos bet his life on that. Morrison's honoring the bet."

Sarah felt something twist in her chest. "Release him. We need Morrison's intelligence."

"Already done. Lagos is being transported out of GenVault now. He'll be debriefed and released within six hours."

"What did Morrison tell you about Hub 47?"

Torres's voice went flat. "Estimated casualties if uncontained: thirty-five to fifty million over twenty-four to thirty-six months. Respiratory transmission. Two to four percent mortality rate overall, higher in vulnerable populations. Containment window is fourteen to twenty-one days before it spreads beyond control."

Thirty-five to fifty million deaths.

From one hub.

From the one hub Sarah failed to secure in time.

"We have two weeks to contain it," Sarah said.

"Chinese authorities are trying. Isolation protocols. Contact tracing. But Sarah, they don't know they're dealing with engineered bioweapon. They're treating it as natural outbreak. That limits their response options."

"We can't tell them the truth without exposing the entire THRESHOLD conspiracy."

"Exactly. So we coordinate quietly. CDC sending technical advisors. WHO deploying epidemiological teams. Everyone helping China contain the outbreak without explaining why we know so much about this specific pathogen."

Sarah looked at the news. Four dead. Twenty-three infected. Forty-seven suspected.

Numbers climbing.

"Morrison's intelligence—can we use it to help containment?"

"Yes. Transmission characteristics, incubation period, symptomatic progression. We're feeding it to Chinese CDC through back channels. Technically we're advising based on 'similar coronavirus outbreaks.' Actually we're using THRESHOLD engineering specifications."

"How long until they contain it?"

Torres was quiet.

"Torres. How long?"

"We don't know. Morrison says containment window is two weeks. But that assumes perfect isolation, zero community transmission, complete contact tracing. If it's already spreading beyond Huanan Market cluster..."

"Then it becomes pandemic."

"Yes."

Sarah closed her eyes.

(Forty-six hubs secured. Four point one five billion lives saved. But Hub 47 escaped. Li Mei dead. Thirty-five to fifty million more deaths possible if containment fails.)

"Keep me updated," Sarah said. "Every new case. Every death. I'm tracking all of it."

She ended the call.

Pulled up Chinese news.

Death toll updated: **5 dead, 31 infected, 67 suspected.**

Five deaths. Li Mei plus four more. Sarah added their names to her tracking file.

(If she couldn't prevent Hub 47 completely, she'd document it completely.)

(If people were going to die from the one hub she missed, she'd remember every name.)

---

**Day 3 - November 26, 2019**

**Wuhan Outbreak Status: 47 confirmed cases, 12 deaths, 134 suspected**

Li Mei's children—Jian (7) and Xiao (4)—died within twelve hours of each other.

Qiang survived. Sat in an empty apartment for three weeks before Li Mei's mother came to take him home. He would spend the next five years wishing he hadn't survived at all.

Morrison's mathematics proving accurate. Mortality rate tracking at 25% for genetically vulnerable populations.

Sarah tracked every death. Added every name to her file.

(This was the cost of partial prevention. This was two percent failure measured in families.)

---

**Day 5 - November 28, 2019**

**Wuhan Outbreak Status: 89 confirmed, 23 deaths, 267 suspected**

Morrison's interrogation sessions had been running twelve hours daily.

FBI. CIA. CDC. Interpol. Everyone wanting intelligence about THRESHOLD. Hub locations, personnel networks, pathogen specifications, deployment plans.

Morrison told them everything.

No resistance. No bargaining. Complete cooperation.

"Hub 47 specifications," Morrison said, reading from memory. "SARS-CoV-2 variant engineered for enhanced ACE2 binding. Spike protein modifications increasing transmission efficiency. Furin cleavage site insertion improving cellular entry. Designed for respiratory transmission with two to four percent baseline mortality, higher in targeted genetic groups."

The FBI interrogator typed rapidly. "You designed this to kill fifty million people?"

"I designed it to kill eighty-nine million. Hub 47 was targeted at Asia-Pacific region. Genetic markers common in East Asian populations. The version that escaped during evacuation is incomplete—only sixty percent lethality optimization. That's why Morrison's projections are thirty-five to fifty million instead of eighty-nine."

"You're saying the version spreading in Wuhan is the WEAKER version?"

"Yes. Full THRESHOLD deployment would have been worse. Sarah prevented that by securing forty-six hubs. Hub 47 escaped but incomplete pathogen is less lethal than designed."

The interrogator stared at Morrison. "You built a weapon to kill eighty-nine million people and you're treating thirty-five million like a victory?"

Morrison met his eyes. "I built a weapon to kill four point two billion people. Sarah prevented four point one five billion deaths by securing forty-six hubs. If Hub 47 kills thirty-five million, that's still ninety-nine percent prevention success. That's defensive Protocol proving effective even when imperfect."

"You sound like you're proud of her."

"I am. She proved thirty years of mathematics wrong. Proved offensive Protocol fails. Proved defensive cooperation works at scale. That's worth everything."

"Including thirty-five million deaths?"

Morrison looked at his hands. At sixty-four years of building genocide. At Emily's photograph he carried in his wallet.

"I don't know," he said quietly. "Ask me again when we see if Chinese authorities contain the outbreak. If they do, casualties might be hundreds instead of millions. If defensive Protocol can contain even escaped bioweapon, that proves prevention works better than my precision ever did."

The interrogator closed his laptop. "You're never leaving detention. You understand that?"

"Yes."

"No trial. No public proceedings. Permanent classified detention. You'll die in federal custody."

"I know. That's appropriate. I planned to kill four billion people. Life imprisonment is getting off easy."

"Why are you cooperating?"

Morrison thought about that. About Emily. About Sarah. About Lagos betting his life that defensive Protocol worked.

"Because Sarah proved me wrong. That's the kindest thing anyone's done for me in thirty years. I'm returning the favor by helping her contain the damage from Hub 47. It's not redemption. It's just data exchange."

The interrogation ended.

Morrison returned to his cell.

Sat down with terrible federal detention coffee.

(Worse than GenVault coffee. But consistent.)

(Sarah was out there trying to contain Hub 47 with incomplete information. He'd given her everything. Pathogen specs, transmission data, mortality projections. Everything she needed to help Chinese authorities respond.)

(Defensive Protocol distilled: cooperation over optimization. Truth over victory.)

(He'd learned that from watching her refuse to stop at forty-six hubs.)

(Even though forty-six would have been better than his zero.)

---

**Day 7 - November 30, 2019**

**Wuhan Outbreak Status: 178 confirmed, 47 deaths, 412 suspected**

Sarah sat in FBI emergency operations center watching case numbers climb.

One hundred seventy-eight confirmed. Forty-seven deaths. Four hundred twelve suspected. Numbers doubling every forty-eight hours.

Containment window was fourteen days. They were on day seven. Halfway through.

Chinese authorities implementing full outbreak response. Isolation protocols. Contact tracing. Hospital surge capacity. Everything possible to prevent spread beyond Wuhan.

But Morrison's intelligence showed this pathogen was engineered for transmission. R0 of 2.5 to 3.5 meant each infected person spread to two or three more. Exponential growth.

"CDC assessment," Torres said, reviewing reports. "Containment is possible but requires perfect execution. Any community transmission beyond identified contacts breaks isolation strategy. We're at critical threshold."

"What's the probability of containment success?" Sarah asked.

"Thirty to forty percent. Most likely scenario: outbreak spreads beyond Wuhan, becomes regional epidemic in China, possible global pandemic if international travel isn't restricted."

"And if it becomes pandemic?"

"Morrison's projections: thirty-five million deaths over three years assuming no vaccine development. Possibly higher if health systems collapse."

Sarah looked at the map. Red zone over Wuhan. Orange zones spreading through Hubei province. Yellow zones in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou.

"The Chinese government doesn't know this is engineered bioweapon," Sarah said. "They're responding to what they think is natural outbreak."

"Correct. We can't tell them without exposing THRESHOLD conspiracy."

"So we're letting them fight engineered pandemic with natural outbreak protocols."

"We're providing technical assistance. CDC advisors using Morrison's intelligence to guide response strategy. But publicly, it's natural coronavirus outbreak. That's the coverup."

Sarah felt sick.

(Forty-seven deaths so far. Li Mei. Her children. Forty-four more. All dying from Hub 47 escape while the world believed this was natural emergence.)

(The truth—that Wells evacuated bioweapons facility and released THRESHOLD during panicked transport—would never be public.)

(Governments were already coordinating coverup. Natural outbreak narrative. No mention of the Order. No mention of forty-six hubs prevented. No mention of Sarah Chen.)

Her phone buzzed.

Morrison, from detention: **Day 7 case count suggests containment is failing. Community transmission detected outside Huanan Market cluster. Projected timeline: regional epidemic by Week 3, national by Week 6, international by Week 12. Defensive Protocol is containing damage but can't prevent pandemic from escaped hub. I'm sorry.**

Sarah stared at the message.

(Morrison was sorry. As if sorry meant anything now.)

She typed back:

**How do we minimize casualties?**

Morrison's response:

**Early warning systems. International coordination. Hospital surge capacity. Isolation protocols. Everything defensive Protocol does well. You prevented four billion deaths. Now minimize the thirty-five million I designed Hub 47 to cause. That's all anyone can do.**

Sarah closed her eyes.

(Partial victory haunted by partial failure. Both permanent.)

She opened her tracking file.

Added the day seven statistics.

**November 30, 2019: 178 confirmed, 47 deaths, 412 suspected. Containment failing. Community transmission detected. Pandemic likely.**

Then she pulled up Li Mei's photograph.

Looked at her face. Thirty-four years old. Mother. Wife. Dead because Hub 47 escaped.

"I'm sorry," Sarah whispered to the photograph. "I secured forty-six hubs. I was too late for yours."

Coffee was waiting.

It tasted like failure.

---

**Day 8 - December 1, 2019**

THRESHOLD deployment date.

The day forty-seven hubs would have released coordinated bioweapons killing 4.237 billion people.

Instead: Forty-six hubs secured. One hub escaped. One hundred ninety-four confirmed cases. Fifty-three deaths. Outbreak spreading through Wuhan.

Sarah sat in Hartford safe house watching news coverage.

**"Unusual Pneumonia Outbreak in Wuhan - 194 cases, 53 deaths, Chinese authorities investigating"**

Not genocide. Not coordinated deployment. Not four billion casualties.

Just one escaped hub killing hundreds. Maybe thousands. Possibly millions if containment failed.

Partial prevention.

Her phone rang. Lagos.

"You did it," he said. Voice tired but steady. "December 1st. THRESHOLD deployment date. And forty-six hubs are secured instead of deployed. Four billion people are alive who would have died."

"Hub 47 escaped," Sarah said. "Fifty-three dead. Numbers climbing."

"I know. I'm tracking it. But Sarah, you prevented ninety-eight percent of THRESHOLD. That's massive success."

"Tell that to Li Mei's family."

Lagos was quiet.

"Her children died," Sarah continued. "Jian was seven. Xiao was four. Both gone within hours of each other. And Qiang—he survived, but he lost everything. His wife and both children in one week. Because I was too late for Hub 47."

"You couldn't have known Wells would evacuate early."

"I should have hit all forty-seven simultaneously instead of eight then thirty-nine. If I'd coordinated better, faster, we might have caught Wuhan before evacuation."

"Or Wells would have destroyed samples at all forty-seven hubs and rebuilt in six months. Sarah, you made the right strategic choice. Forty-six secured is better than zero secured because you waited for perfect simultaneous coordination."

Sarah knew he was right.

But Li Mei was still dead.

"Morrison's cooperating," Lagos said. "Giving intelligence about Hub 47. Helping containment efforts."

"He's trying to prove defensive Protocol works even for escaped bioweapon. Trying to prove I can minimize his thirty-five million to something smaller."

"Can you?"

Sarah looked at the case numbers. One hundred ninety-four confirmed. Probably six hundred actual infections. Growing exponentially. Containment window closing.

"I don't know," she said. "But I'm going to try. Coordinate with WHO, CDC, Chinese authorities. Use Morrison's intelligence to guide response. Minimize casualties even if I can't prevent outbreak."

"That's defensive Protocol."

"That's partial success haunted by partial failure."

Lagos was quiet.

Then: "I'm alive because you secured forty-six hubs. Morrison released me because defensive Protocol proved effective. That's worth acknowledging."

"You're alive. Li Mei is dead. I don't know how to balance that math."

"Neither do I. But Sarah, four billion people are alive today who would have died if you'd stopped at forty-five hubs. Or forty. Or settled for partial success. You pushed for complete prevention. That's better than anyone's ever done."

Sarah closed her eyes.

(The math didn't balance. The math never balanced.)

"I need to go," she said. "Tracking outbreak updates."

"Sarah—"

"I secured forty-six hubs and failed one. I'm living with both. Talk later."

She ended the call.

Pulled up her tracking file.

**December 1, 2019 - THRESHOLD Deployment Date - Status: PREVENTED**
**Hubs secured: 46/47 (98% success rate)**
**Casualties prevented: 4.15 billion**
**Hub 47 (Wuhan) status: ESCAPED**
**Current outbreak: 194 confirmed, 53 deaths, pandemic likely**
**Projected casualties: 35-50 million over 24-36 months**

She stared at the numbers.

(This was victory. This was failure. This was both simultaneously.)

(This was Li Mei dead and her children dead and Sarah would carry that weight forever.)

Coffee was waiting.

Always waiting.

Sarah drank it anyway.

**Day 12 - December 3, 2019**

## LAGOS VISITS MORRISON IN DETENTION

Lagos arrived at federal detention facility in Newark at 2:00 PM on December 3, 2019.

Twelve days since Hub 47 started spreading. Four hundred twelve confirmed cases in Wuhan. Eighty-seven deaths. International spread beginning—Thailand, Japan, South Korea. Morrison's mathematics proving accurate with every new death count.

(Morrison had predicted this. Down to the transmission vectors. Down to the exponential growth rate. Down to the demographics of who would die first.)

The guard led Lagos through three security checkpoints to detention wing's interview room. Same room where Morrison had provided Hub 47 intelligence two weeks ago. Same metal table. Same fluorescent lights humming overhead like mechanical insects.

Morrison sat waiting. Federal detention jumpsuit. Hands cuffed in front. Face thinner than Lagos remembered—weight loss from facility food or stress or both.

(Sixty-five years old. Looked seventy now. Stress aging him in real-time.)

The guard stepped outside. Left the door open six inches. Standard supervision protocol.

"David," Morrison said. Voice flat. Dead. The voice Morrison used when emotion was luxury he couldn't afford.

"How are you holding up?"

"I'm in federal detention awaiting trial for designing genocide. How do you think I'm holding up?"

Lagos sat across from him. The table between them was metal institutional furniture bolted to floor. Cold. Permanent. Like everything in this place was designed to communicate: You're not leaving.

"I came to update you on Hub 47," Lagos said. "Four hundred twelve confirmed cases. Eighty-seven deaths. Your intelligence about ACE2 receptor binding helped Chinese CDC identify the pathogen faster but containment's failing."

Morrison nodded. Expected. "R-naught?"

"2.7. Your estimate was 2.5 to 3.7. You were accurate."

"Of course I was accurate. I designed it."

The bitterness in Morrison's voice was new. Or maybe not new. Maybe just not hidden anymore.

Lagos pulled out printed graphs. Epidemic curves. Case counts. Death projections. He spread them on the table.

"China's implementing quarantine measures but they're too slow. International cases suggest the pathogen's already seeded in fifty-three countries. If they don't lock down Wuhan within two weeks—"

"Then my mathematics prove correct," Morrison interrupted. "Thirty-five million dead. Maybe more if healthcare systems collapse. Which they will."

He stared at the graphs. Didn't touch them. Just looked.

(Those were his equations. His design. His legacy spreading across epidemic curves like signature written in corpses.)

"Sarah wanted me to ask if you have additional intelligence," Lagos said. "Anything about containment strategies, vaccine vulnerabilities, therapeutic targets. Anything that might help."

"I already gave you everything. Transmission vectors, receptor binding, incubation periods, demographic targeting. What more do you want? A cure? I designed a weapon, not a vaccine. GenVault has the cure data but they're not sharing. Patent applications filed three months ago. They're going to profit from this."

Lagos already knew that. Sarah had found the patent applications. GenVault had vaccine candidates ready before outbreak began because they'd designed the pathogen. Standard bioweapon strategy—create problem, monopolize solution, profit from fear.

"We're working on exposing that," Lagos said.

"Good luck. They have lawyers. They have shell companies. They have plausible deniability. I'm the only one taking the fall. 'Rogue geneticist.' That's what the indictment says. Not corporate conspiracy. Not institutional genocide. Rogue geneticist. Singular."

Morrison's hands shook. Just slightly. Tremor in his fingers that hadn't been there two weeks ago.

Lagos noticed.

"You okay?"

"Fine."

"You're shaking."

"I'm fine." Morrison pulled his hands off the table. Hid them in his lap. "Just coffee. They changed the coffee three days ago. New supplier. Tastes wrong. Chemical aftertaste. Probably cheap institutional blend but it makes my hands shake."

Lagos frowned. "Have you reported it?"

"Reported what? That the coffee tastes bad? They'd think I'm paranoid." Morrison laughed. Hollow sound. "Maybe I am paranoid. Maybe federal detention makes everyone paranoid. You start noticing things. Guards acting different. Cameras in corners you didn't see before. Coffee tasting wrong."

"What do you mean guards acting different?"

Morrison leaned forward. Voice dropped to whisper even though the door was open and guard was right outside.

"Two new guards started Sunday. Don't talk. Just watch. Not the way normal guards watch. Different. Like they're waiting for something. And yesterday someone went through my cell. I can tell. Papers moved. Books spine-down instead of spine-up. Little things. Things you'd only notice if you were paying attention."

"Have you filed a complaint?"

"About what? 'Guards are looking at me funny and someone moved my books?' They already think I'm crazy. Crazy enough to design genocide. If I start complaining about guards and coffee they'll put me in psych eval and then my testimony becomes inadmissible and GenVault wins."

Lagos studied Morrison's face. Looked for signs of actual paranoia versus legitimate concern.

(Hard to tell. Detained geneticists who'd designed global genocide probably had reason to be paranoid. Also probably had reason to develop actual paranoia. The line between reasonable caution and mental breakdown was thin in federal detention.)

"Do you think they're trying to silence you?" Lagos asked.

"I think I'm the only person who can testify about THRESHOLD's full scope. I know the names. The facilities. The money trails. Wells disappeared but I know where the bodies are buried—literally and figuratively. That makes me valuable to prosecutors and dangerous to GenVault."

"You're in federal custody. They can't—"

"Jeffrey Epstein was in federal custody." Morrison's voice was flat. Dead serious. "Federal detention. Suicide watch. Guards fell asleep. Cameras malfunctioned. Convenient timing. Convenient narrative. Convenient closure."

The room went quiet.

Lagos wanted to say *that won't happen to you*. Wanted to reassure. But the reassurance would be lie and Morrison would know it was lie and lies were insult to someone facing mathematics of his own mortality.

"I'm writing everything down," Morrison said. "Names. Dates. Facility locations. Money transfers. Everything. If something happens to me—if the coffee keeps tasting wrong or the guards keep watching differently or I mysteriously die of heart failure at sixty-five years old—someone needs to know. Sarah needs to know. You need to know."

He reached into his jumpsuit pocket. Pulled out folded papers. Three pages. Handwritten. Dense text.

"Take this. Hide it. Don't read it here. Don't read it anywhere with cameras. But if I die suspiciously—and you'll know if it's suspicious because healthy sixty-five-year-olds don't just die in federal custody—give this to Sarah. She'll know what to do with it."

Lagos took the papers. Folded them smaller. Slipped them into his jacket pocket.

"You're not going to die," Lagos said.

"Everyone dies, Marcus. The question is whether it's convenient timing."

The guard appeared in doorway. "Five minutes."

Morrison nodded. Looked at Lagos. Eyes that had seen his mathematics manifest in real-time epidemic curves. Eyes that knew exactly how many people were going to die because Hub 47 escaped. Eyes that understood he might be next.

"Tell Sarah I'm sorry," Morrison said. "For Hub 47. For everything. I thought I was right. I was wrong. But being wrong doesn't undo the damage and thirty-five million people are going to die anyway and I deserve whatever happens to me."

"You don't deserve to be murdered."

"Maybe. Maybe not. But if the coffee keeps tasting wrong and the guards keep watching differently and I die mysteriously in six months—remember this conversation. Remember the papers. Remember that convenient timing is another word for murder."

Lagos stood. Wanted to say something comforting. Couldn't find the words.

Morrison watched him leave.

Through the six-inch door gap Lagos saw Morrison lift his coffee cup—facility coffee, lukewarm, chemical aftertaste—and drink it anyway.

(Because what else could he do?)

(Stop drinking coffee and confirm his paranoia was justified?)

(Or keep drinking and hope paranoia was just paranoia?)

Morrison kept drinking.

Lagos walked out thinking about Epstein. About convenient timing. About how easy it would be to slip something into coffee that tasted wrong already. About how no one would question sixty-five-year-old geneticist dying of heart failure after stress of detention and guilt of genocide.

About how the papers in his pocket might be Morrison's last testament.

About how Morrison's mathematics were proving accurate with every death count.

About how inconvenient Morrison's testimony would be for GenVault's vaccine profits.

Lagos drove back to New York thinking about coffee.

Chemical aftertaste.

Guards watching differently.

Papers moved spine-down instead of spine-up.

Lagos would remember this conversation.

Right now it was December 3, 2019.

Day 12 of the outbreak.

Four hundred twelve cases.

Eighty-seven deaths.

And Morrison was still alive drinking chemical coffee wondering if paranoia was survival instinct or breakdown.

(Turned out it was survival instinct.)

(Turned out he was right.)

(Turned out the coffee really did taste wrong.)
