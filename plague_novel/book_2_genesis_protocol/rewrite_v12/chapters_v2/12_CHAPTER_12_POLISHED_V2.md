# CHAPTER 12 - THE BREACH

**Hartford Safe House**
**November 21, 2019 - 2:47 PM**
**10 days until THRESHOLD deployment**

---

Sarah had been talking for six hours.

FBI Special Agent Rebecca Torres sat across from her with a laptop, three legal pads, and coffee gone cold two hours ago. Recording everything. Hub locations. Security protocols. Casualty projections. Deployment vectors.

Forty-seven hubs.

Sarah recited them from memory. Atlanta first. Then Chicago. Los Angeles. Seattle. Working through the list methodically while Torres typed and Maya Chen cross-referenced genetic distribution data and Martin Reeves built probability models for simultaneous raids.

"Hub 23: São Paulo, Brazil," Sarah said. Her voice was hoarse. "Instituto Butantan, Sublevel 3. Biometric security. Pathogen storage in cryogenic units. Projected casualties: 52.1 million."

Torres kept typing. "Deployment timing?"

"December 1st, 0400 local time. Synchronized with Atlanta hub."

"Security response?"

"Lethal force authorized. If breach detected, facility locks down and samples destroyed. We'll have one chance—if they know we're coming, evidence gets eliminated and Wells relocates to backup sites."

Torres stopped typing. Looked at Sarah. "You're saying we need to hit all forty-seven locations simultaneously? Coordinated across four continents?"

"Yes."

"That's impossible. International coordination takes weeks. Warrants. Diplomatic clearances. We can't—"

"Then 4.2 billion people die." Sarah's voice was flat. Dead. "Morrison gave me eleven days. If we don't shut down all forty-seven hubs before December 1st, THRESHOLD deploys and everything—everything Lagos sacrificed—means nothing."

Maya looked up from genetic data. "Rebecca, we don't need perfect coordination. We need chaos. Hit eight hubs immediately in friendly jurisdictions. Make noise. Force the Order to react. While they're scrambling, we hit the remaining thirty-nine."

"I can't coordinate unofficial operations without State Department approval. I'd violate about seventeen federal laws."

"If you don't," Sarah said quietly, "you're complicit in genocide."

Torres looked at her laptop. At forty-seven hub locations typed from Sarah's memory. At casualty projections totaling 4.237 billion deaths.

"Fuck it," Torres said. "Let's go commit some international incidents."

She picked up her phone. Started dialing.

---

**6 Hours Later - FBI Emergency Operations Center, Quantico**

Torres stood in front of a wall of monitors showing eight simultaneous operations.

**ATLANTA - 08:17 EST**: FBI tactical team breaching Cargo Facility 7 at Hartsfield-Jackson. Biohazard containment active. CDC personnel ready.

**CHICAGO - 08:19 EST**: Federal warrant executed at O'Hare South Cargo. Airport security cooperating.

**LOS ANGELES - 05:23 PST**: LAPD SWAT plus CDC entering LAX Terminal B.

**SEATTLE - 05:31 PST**: Sea-Tac maintenance tunnels secured. Moving to International Terminal.

Four domestic operations proceeding clean.

The international operations were messier:

**TOKYO - 22:42 JST**: Japanese National Police coordinating. Corporate lawyers claiming diplomatic immunity.

**SEOUL - 23:15 KST**: South Korean authorities executing emergency warrant. Facility management resisting.

**PARIS - 14:33 CET**: Interpol plus French police at Charles de Gaulle. Legal challenges delaying access.

**LONDON - 13:51 GMT**: MI5 coordinating with health authorities. GenVault subsidiary claiming protected research status.

Torres's phone rang. Field supervisor from Atlanta.

"We found it," he said. Voice tight. "Cryogenic storage exactly where Chen said. Twelve sealed containers. CDC running preliminary analysis."

Torres felt cold wash through her. It was real. All of it.

"Secure everything," Torres ordered. "Full containment. This is evidence for genocide trial."

"Chicago and LA reporting similar findings. Seattle's still searching."

Three confirmed. Possibly four.

Torres pulled up her operational map. Eight dots turning green. Thirty-nine red dots remaining.

Her second phone rang. Martin from Hartford.

"Four domestic hubs secured," Martin said. "International operations hitting legal resistance."

"Corporate lawyers. GenVault has contingency plans—using diplomatic immunity claims to slow us down."

"Which buys them time to relocate samples from the remaining thirty-nine."

"Exactly." Torres watched Paris operation stall. "We need those thirty-nine hit before GenVault realizes the scope."

"Elena's coordinating. She's got resistance fighters positioned at fifteen locations. Not official operations—local activists making sure facilities get anonymously reported to health authorities."

"That's not legal."

"Neither is genocide." Martin's voice was flat. "Elena's people trigger health inspections, fire code violations, environmental complaints at thirty-nine facilities that happen to be THRESHOLD storage locations."

Torres looked at her map. Eight green dots. Thirty-nine red. Ten days until deployment.

"Do it," she said.

"Elena's activating now." Martin paused. "Sarah wants to know about Lagos."

"Morrison's holding him. Detained, not executed. Wells wanted immediate termination but Morrison overruled her."

"Why would Morrison protect someone who helped Sarah escape?"

"Because Morrison's running an experiment. Lagos bet his life defensive Protocol works. Morrison's keeping him alive until we prove it does or doesn't."

"So Lagos lives if we stop THRESHOLD?"

"If we stop all forty-seven hubs." Torres watched another dot turn green. "No pressure."

The line went dead.

Torres watched her monitors. Five hubs secured. Forty-two remaining.

Ten days.

---

**Hour 36 - Hartford Safe House**
**November 22, 2019**

Sarah hadn't slept.

Just watched operational feeds. Tracked hub confirmations. Counted successes.

**Hubs secured: 42**
**Hubs remaining: 5**

Forty-two facilities neutralized across four continents in thirty-six hours. Elena's resistance networks moving faster than GenVault could counter. Anonymous tips. Health inspections. Fire code violations. Environmental complaints.

Distributed chaos versus centralized logistics.

Defensive Protocol versus Offensive Protocol.

Sarah's phone buzzed. Torres.

"Forty-two confirmed secure," Torres said. "Samples seized, facilities locked down, personnel detained. Five hubs remaining."

Sarah pulled up her list:

Hub 43: Mumbai, India
Hub 44: Cairo, Egypt
Hub 45: Mexico City, Mexico
Hub 46: Sydney, Australia
Hub 47: Wuhan, China

"What's the status on the final five?" Sarah asked.

"Mumbai and Cairo confirmed for raid tomorrow morning local time. Mexico City coordinating with health ministry. Sydney waiting on warrant clearance."

"And Wuhan?"

Torres paused. "Wuhan's complicated. Chinese CDC lost contact with our liaison thirty-six hours ago. Communications blackout. We're trying to re-establish contact through Beijing channels."

Sarah felt cold spread through her chest. "Thirty-six hours? That's before we started the first wave of raids."

"I know."

"Torres, if GenVault knew we were coming to Wuhan before we hit the first eight hubs—"

"They could have evacuated Hub 47. Relocated samples. We're working with Chinese authorities to confirm facility status."

Sarah looked at the map. Forty-two green dots. Five red dots.

Hub 47: Wuhan, China.

Still red.

Still unconfirmed.

"Get me confirmation," Sarah said. "I need to know if Wuhan is secure."

"Working on it. Chinese authorities are cooperating but slowly. Diplomatic channels. Bureaucratic delays. You know how it is."

Sarah knew. She also knew what thirty-six hours of communications blackout meant.

(GenVault knew. They evacuated. Hub 47 was already gone.)

"Find it," she said. "Whatever it takes. We need all forty-seven hubs secured."

She ended the call.

Stared at the red dot over Wuhan.

(Something was wrong.)

(Something had gone wrong days ago.)

---

**Hour 48 - Chinese CDC Emergency Operations, Beijing**
**November 23, 2019**

Dr. Wei Zhang stared at the report from Wuhan.

His team had been trying to reach their field investigator, Dr. Lin Chen, for forty-eight hours. No response. Phone disconnected. Office empty. Apartment abandoned.

And now this: Wuhan Municipal Health Commission reporting unusual pneumonia cluster. Seventeen cases in three days. All connected to Huanan Seafood Wholesale Market.

Wei pulled up the case files:

**Patient 1**: Li Jian, age 41, seafood vendor. Admitted November 20. Severe respiratory distress.

**Patient 2**: Zhang Wei (different Zhang), age 38, market worker. Admitted November 20. Pneumonia, unknown etiology.

**Patient 3-17**: Similar presentations. All connected to Huanan Market. All within 72 hours.

Seventeen cases. Unusual pneumonia. Unknown pathogen.

And Dr. Lin Chen—who'd been investigating suspected biological research facility beneath the market—had disappeared three days before the first case appeared.

Wei picked up his phone. Called Wuhan Municipal Health Commission.

"This is Dr. Wei Zhang, Chinese CDC. I need full epidemiological investigation at Huanan Seafood Market. Possible biological contamination. Isolate all cases. Test for unusual pathogens. And I need access to the facility Dr. Lin was investigating—the research lab beneath the market."

"Dr. Wei, there's no research lab beneath Huanan Market. It's a seafood wholesale facility."

"Dr. Lin reported suspected illegal biotech operation. Hidden facility. I need that location investigated immediately."

Silence on the line.

Then: "We'll coordinate with local police. But Dr. Wei, if there's biological contamination, we need to know what we're looking for."

Wei looked at the case files. Seventeen patients. Severe pneumonia. Unknown etiology. All connected to the same market where a suspected biotech facility operated.

"I don't know," he said quietly. "But whatever it is, it's already spreading."

---

**Hour 52 - Wuhan Municipal Hospital**
**November 23, 2019 - Evening**

Li Mei lay in the isolation ward struggling to breathe.

She was thirty-four. Mother of two children—Jian, age 7, and Xiao, age 4. She worked as a vendor at Huanan Market selling fresh produce. Six days ago she'd noticed workers in the back section of the market acting strangely. Moving equipment. Clearing out storage areas. Nervous. Rushed.

Five days ago, the back section was abandoned. Doors sealed. No explanation.

Four days ago, Li Mei developed a cough. Fever. Fatigue.

Three days ago, her symptoms worsened. Severe respiratory distress. Her husband Qiang brought her to the hospital.

Now she was in isolation. Oxygen mask. IV fluids. Doctors running tests she didn't understand.

"Mrs. Li," the doctor said. Young. Tired. Scared. "Your test results show unusual viral pneumonia. We're trying to identify the pathogen. But your condition is deteriorating rapidly."

"My children," Li Mei whispered through the oxygen mask. "Jian and Xiao. Are they—"

"Your husband brought them for testing. Both showing early symptoms. We've isolated them as well."

Li Mei's heart didn't just break. It shattered into something small and sharp that lodged in her chest right next to her failing lungs.

(Her babies. Jian who still slept with his stuffed rabbit. Xiao who called her "Mama" seventeen times a day because she was four and four-year-olds did that. Both sick. Both in isolation. Both dying because their mother sold produce at a market that happened to have a bioweapons lab underneath it.)

"What is it?" she asked. "What's making us sick?"

The doctor hesitated. "We don't know yet. But Mrs. Li, you need to tell us—were you exposed to anything unusual at the market? Chemicals? Animals? Anything that might explain this?"

Li Mei thought about the back section. The workers clearing out. The sealed doors.

"There was something," she said. "Back section of the market. Workers were removing equipment five days ago. They looked scared. Rushed. Then they sealed everything and disappeared."

The doctor's face changed. "What kind of equipment?"

"Medical equipment. Laboratory equipment. I don't know exactly. But it wasn't normal for a seafood market."

The doctor pulled out his phone. Made a call. Spoke quickly in Mandarin.

Li Mei closed her eyes.

(Her children were sick. Jian and Xiao. Seven and four years old. Infected because their mother worked at the wrong market at the wrong time.)

(Because someone had been operating a lab beneath a seafood market. Because they'd cleared out in a hurry. Because something had been released.)

She coughed. Blood on the tissue.

The oxygen mask felt wrong. Too tight. Not enough air.

(She was dying. She knew she was dying.)

(And her children were dying too.)

---

**Hour 56 - Hartford Safe House**
**November 24, 2019 - Early Morning**

Sarah's phone rang at 3 AM.

Torres. Voice tight.

"We have confirmation from Beijing. Chinese CDC investigated Huanan Seafood Market in Wuhan. Found evidence of hidden laboratory facility in subsection beneath the market. Facility was evacuated approximately five days ago. Containment equipment removed. No samples remaining."

Sarah sat up. "Hub 47 was evacuated before we started raids?"

"Appears so. Timeline suggests GenVault cleared out the Wuhan facility November 19th or 20th. Before our first wave hit on November 21st."

"They knew we were coming."

"Or they had standing evacuation protocols for compromised facilities. Either way, Hub 47 is gone."

Sarah felt something cold settle in her stomach. "What about the pneumonia cases?"

Silence.

"Torres. What pneumonia cases?"

"Wuhan Municipal Health Commission is reporting unusual pneumonia cluster. Seventeen cases connected to Huanan Market. All within the last five days. Unknown pathogen. Severe respiratory symptoms."

Seventeen cases.

Five days ago.

Right when Hub 47 was evacuated.

"It escaped," Sarah whispered. "THRESHOLD escaped from Hub 47 during evacuation."

"We don't know that—"

"Seventeen cases. Unusual pneumonia. Unknown pathogen. Connected to the market where Hub 47 operated. Torres, it escaped. Hub 47 released THRESHOLD during evacuation."

Sarah pulled up her THRESHOLD casualty projections. Hub 47 (Wuhan): Projected casualties 89.3 million across Asia-Pacific region.

If Hub 47 deployed completely, eighty-nine million dead.

If Hub 47 was partially released during evacuation—

"How many casualties?" Sarah asked. Voice hollow.

"CDC doesn't know yet. Chinese authorities are containing the outbreak. Isolating cases. Testing contacts. It might be contained to Huanan Market cluster."

"Or it might spread. We don't know what stage of production Hub 47 was at. We don't know how much pathogen was released. We don't know transmission characteristics."

"Sarah—"

"We secured forty-six hubs. We prevented THRESHOLD deployment. But we were too late for Wuhan. Hub 47 escaped."

Sarah stood. Walked to the window. Looked out at darkness.

Forty-six hubs secured.

One hub escaped.

Seventeen people infected. Unknown how many more exposed.

(This was partial prevention. This was forty-six out of forty-seven. This was better than zero but worse than complete success.)

"What's the Chinese government doing?" Sarah asked.

"Full epidemiological investigation. Contact tracing. Isolation protocols. They're treating it as potential outbreak threat. But Sarah, they don't know it's THRESHOLD. We can't tell them without exposing the entire conspiracy."

"So they're treating it as natural outbreak?"

"Yes."

Sarah closed her eyes.

(Seventeen people infected with THRESHOLD. Chinese authorities investigating as natural pneumonia outbreak. No one knowing that Hub 47 had been building genocide weapon beneath seafood market. No one knowing that GenVault had evacuated in panic five days ago and released pathogen in the process.)

(No one knowing except Sarah.)

(And she couldn't tell them without exposing forty-six other hubs, Morrison's conspiracy, the Order's three-thousand-year plan.)

"We need Morrison's intelligence," Sarah said. "Hub 47 specifications. Pathogen characteristics. Transmission vectors. Mortality rates. He built THRESHOLD. He knows what escaped."

"Morrison's in detention. He's not cooperating."

"Make him cooperate. Tell him Hub 47 escaped. Tell him seventeen people are infected. Tell him if he doesn't help us understand what's spreading, it could kill millions instead of being contained."

Torres was quiet.

Then: "I'll try. But Sarah, Morrison planned this. He wanted four billion dead. Why would he help us stop it?"

Sarah thought about that. About Morrison's mathematics. About Emily. About defensive Protocol proving Morrison wrong.

"Because if Hub 47 kills millions, it proves I failed. But if we contain it using his intelligence, it proves defensive Protocol works even for partial prevention. He wants to know if I can save people even when prevention isn't perfect."

"That's twisted."

"That's Morrison."

Sarah ended the call.

Looked at the map.

Forty-six green dots.

One red dot over Wuhan.

Seventeen infected. Numbers climbing.

(This was the cost of being too slow. This was what happened when you secured forty-six hubs but missed the forty-seventh. This was partial prevention meaning millions might die instead of billions.)

Her phone buzzed.

Morrison: **Forty-six hubs secured. Impressive. But you were too late for Wuhan. Hub 47 evacuated November 19th. Containment breach during transport. Pathogen released at market facility. Defensive Protocol failed where it mattered most. How many will die because you missed one hub?**

Sarah stared at the message.

(He knew. Morrison knew Hub 47 had escaped. Probably knew before she did.)

She typed back:

**Tell me what escaped. Pathogen characteristics. Transmission vectors. Mortality rates. Help me contain it or prove you only care about being right.**

Morrison's response came immediately:

**I'll tell you everything. On one condition: Release Lagos immediately. Forty-six hubs secured proves defensive Protocol can work at scale. He bet his life on that. Honor the bet.**

Sarah looked at the message.

(Morrison would trade intelligence about Hub 47 outbreak for Lagos's freedom. Would help contain the pathogen if Sarah released the man who'd helped her escape.)

(That was the deal. THRESHOLD intelligence for Lagos's life.)

She forwarded the message to Torres.

Added: **Make it happen. We need Morrison's intelligence. Whatever it costs.**

Then she pulled up news from Wuhan.

Chinese state media reporting "unusual pneumonia cluster" at Huanan Market. Seventeen cases. Three deaths. Investigation ongoing.

Three deaths already.

Seventeen infected.

Hub 47 spreading.

(This was partial prevention. This was forty-six successes and one failure. This was better than genocide but worse than complete success.)

Sarah opened her laptop.

Started a new file: **Wuhan Outbreak Tracking**

Patient 1: Li Jian, age 41, seafood vendor. Status: Critical.
Patient 2: Zhang Wei, age 38, market worker. Status: Deceased.
Patient 3: Li Mei, age 34, produce vendor. Status: Critical. (Children Jian-7, Xiao-4 also infected)

She would track every case. Every name. Every death.

(If she couldn't prevent Hub 47 completely, she'd document it completely.)

(If defensive Protocol failed for one hub, she'd prove it worked for forty-six.)

(That would have to be enough.)

Coffee was waiting.

But it already tasted wrong.

(It would taste wrong for five years. But Sarah didn't know that yet. Didn't know Morrison would die suspiciously in detention. Didn't know Wells would vanish like her Nazi grandfather. Didn't know GenVault would profit from the pandemic they created.)

(Right now she just knew: forty-six successes, one failure, and Li Mei's children were dying in isolation wards two floors apart.)

She drank it anyway.
