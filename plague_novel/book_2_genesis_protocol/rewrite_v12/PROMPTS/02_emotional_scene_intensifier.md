# PROMPT 2: Emotional Scene Intensifier

**When to use:** Key emotional moments that should devastate readers (David's death, Lagos reveal, Morrison's trial, etc.)

---

## THE PROMPT

```
This scene should devastate readers but currently feels distant. Rewrite it using these techniques:

1. PHYSICAL SENSATIONS:
   - What does grief taste like? (copper, ash, nothing)
   - What does trauma sound like? (her own heartbeat too loud)
   - What does guilt smell like? (her own sweat, chemical fear)

2. MESSY HUMAN THOUGHTS:
   - Characters think about stupid things during crisis
   - They get distracted by irrelevant details
   - They have intrusive thoughts they're ashamed of
   - Example: "23 million dead and she was thinking about whether she'd locked her apartment door three months ago"

3. BODY BETRAYAL:
   - Her hands shaking isn't enough
   - Make the body do things the character can't control:
   - Teeth chattering despite warm room
   - Suddenly needing to pee
   - Laughing at terrible things
   - Hands that won't stop moving

4. TIME DISTORTION:
   - During trauma, time gets weird
   - "It had been seven seconds or seven hours since Morrison said 'Lagos'"
   - Split-second decisions take paragraphs of internal monologue
   - Hours compress into sentences

5. INTIMATE DETAILS:
   - Focus on ONE thing deeply
   - David's coffee cup, the specific way he folded the letter
   - Morrison's breathing pattern
   - The exact shade of red the emergency lights cast

Rewrite this emotional scene:

[PASTE SCENE HERE]
```

---

## WHAT THIS FIXES

- Abstract emotion → Physical sensation
- Clean thoughts → Messy, human reality
- Controlled reactions → Body betrayal
- Linear time → Trauma time
- General descriptions → Specific intimate details

---

## KEY EMOTIONAL SCENES TO USE THIS ON

1. Lagos guilt scene (Chapter 15)
2. David's letter (Chapter 16)
3. David's death (when Sarah finds out)
4. Marsh's burning house
5. Morrison's breakdown/trial
6. Sarah's capture
7. Final decision/sacrifice

---

## EXAMPLE

**BEFORE:**
> "Sarah felt devastated. The guilt was overwhelming."

**AFTER:**
> "Sarah's stomach decided now was the perfect time to be empty and her throat was doing this thing where it kept trying to close and her brain kept showing her that pregnant woman in Lagos, walking to market, except now the woman was on fire and Sarah could smell it (she couldn't actually smell it, she was in a cell in Massachusetts, but her brain didn't care) and somewhere underneath all of that her bladder was having opinions and wasn't that just perfect, twenty-three million dead and she needed to pee."
