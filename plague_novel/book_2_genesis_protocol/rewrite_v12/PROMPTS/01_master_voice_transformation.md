# PROMPT 1: Master Voice Transformation

**When to use:** First pass on any chapter or scene. Establishes the core voice.

---

## THE PROMPT

```
You are rewriting a thriller scene to match Stephen King's voice and emotional delivery.

CURRENT PROBLEM: The prose is too clinical, too distant, too intellectual. It TELLS emotions instead of making readers FEEL them.

YOUR TASK: Rewrite this scene using these techniques:

VOICE RULES:
1. Get intimate - Write like you're telling a story to a friend at 2 AM
2. Shorter sentences during tension - One. Word. Sentences. Work.
3. Longer, rambling sentences during reflection - let characters' thoughts spiral
4. Use specific, visceral sensory details - not "she was scared" but "her hands smelled like the copper-salt smell of panic sweat"
5. NO PROFANITY - Do not use "fuck" or any derivative (fucking, fucked, etc.). Use alternatives: "damn," "hell," or creative substitutes

EMOTIONAL DELIVERY:
1. Show physical sensations, not emotions:
   - Bad: "Sarah was terrified"
   - Good: "Sarah's bladder suddenly had opinions about the situation"
2. Let characters have absurd thoughts during crisis:
   - "Why am I thinking about coffee when I'm about to die?"
   - "Morrison's tie was crooked. She hated that she noticed."
3. One intimate death > abstract millions:
   - Instead of "23 million dead", show us ONE person dying in detail, then multiply

KING'S TRICKS:
- Parenthetical asides: "Morrison smiled (and that was worse than if he'd shouted)"
- Character noticing weird details: "The fire alarm had a C-sharp pitch. She'd remember that forever."
- Dark humor: "At least if she died, she wouldn't have to see Morrison's PowerPoint about genocide optimization"
- Repetition for dread: Use a phrase 3 times, each time making it worse

NO EXPOSITION: Cut ANY explanation of genetics, history, science. Trust readers. If you must explain, have a character explain it wrong, then correct themselves.

Now rewrite this scene:

[PASTE YOUR SCENE HERE]
```

---

## WHAT THIS FIXES

- Clinical voice → Intimate storytelling
- Told emotions → Felt sensations
- Abstract stakes → Visceral detail
- Distant characters → Real humans

---

## EXAMPLE TRANSFORMATION

**BEFORE (Clinical):**
> "Sarah sat on her cell floor. Back against the concrete wall. Knees pulled to her chest. The position children take when the world becomes too big and too terrible. Lagos was dead."

**AFTER (Visceral):**
> "Sarah sat on the floor with her back against the concrete and her knees pulled up to her chest like she was six years old again and hiding from the thing that lived in her closet, except this time the thing that lived in the closet was her and she couldn't hide from herself no matter how small she made her body. Lagos was dead and it was her fault."

---

## HOW TO USE

1. Copy the prompt above
2. Paste your scene at the bottom
3. Run through Claude (or other AI)
4. Read result aloud - does it sound like someone TELLING you a story?
5. If not visceral enough, run through Emotional Scene Intensifier next
6. If too wordy, run through Exposition Cutter

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Don't just add adjectives** - "very scared" is not better than "scared"
2. **Don't explain emotions** - Show physical reactions instead
3. **Don't keep technical exposition** - Cut it ruthlessly
4. **Don't make it melodramatic** - Ground it in physical reality
5. **Don't lose the plot** - Voice change shouldn't change what happens
6. **Don't use profanity** - No "fuck" or derivatives. Use "damn," "hell," or creative alternatives
