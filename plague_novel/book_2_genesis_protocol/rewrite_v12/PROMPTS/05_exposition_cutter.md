# PROMPT 5: Exposition Cutter

**When to use:** Any section with heavy technical explanation, backstory dumps, or world-building.

---

## THE PROMPT

```
This section explains too much. Cut it to 1/3 length while keeping essential information.

RULES:
1. Trust readers - they're smart
2. Characters can be wrong about explanations
3. Readers don't need to understand the science, just feel the stakes
4. If you must explain: character explains wrong, gets corrected, moves on

EXAMPLE:
BAD (100 words of genetic science explaining CCR5-Delta32, plague resistance, selective pressure, population genetics, etc.)

GOOD (20 words):
"The CCR5 thing?" Sarah said. "It's— basically some people's ancestors survived the plague and passed on resistance." She was simplifying. Morrison would've corrected her. She didn't care.

TECHNIQUES FOR CUTTING:
1. Can this be shown through action instead of explained?
2. Can this be implied rather than stated?
3. Can character NOT understand something and be fine?
4. Can this wait until later (or never)?
5. Is this explanation for YOU or for readers? (If for you, cut it)

Cut this exposition-heavy section:

[PASTE HERE]
```

---

## WHAT TO CUT

**Always cut:**
- Genetic science details
- Historical timelines (unless plot-critical)
- Technical process explanations
- Background on how things work
- Worldbuilding that isn't immediately relevant

**Keep:**
- Stakes (what happens if this fails)
- Immediate consequences
- Character reactions
- Plot-critical info only

---

## EXAMPLE

**BEFORE (200 words of CCR5-Delta32 explanation):**
> The CCR5-Delta32 genetic variant is a 32-base-pair deletion in the CCR5 gene on chromosome 3. This mutation, which emerged approximately 2,500 years ago in Northern Europe, affects the CCR5 receptor on T-cells, providing resistance to bubonic plague and HIV-1. Population genetics studies show this variant appears in 10-15% of European populations, with higher concentrations in Scandinavia and lower concentrations in Mediterranean regions. The selective pressure came from the Black Death of 1348-1350, which killed 30-60% of Europe's population but disproportionately affected individuals lacking this variant. Modern genetic analysis reveals that carriers of CCR5-Delta32 have significantly higher survival rates during plague outbreaks, suggesting targeted evolutionary selection. The Genesis Protocol appears to have encoded this variant into specific bloodlines deliberately, predating natural selective pressure by at least 1,200 years, which is statistically impossible unless...

**AFTER (30 words):**
> "Some people survived the plague," Sarah said. "Their kids survived plagues after that. The Order's been tracking those families for three thousand years."
>
> Morrison smiled. "We didn't track them. We created them."

---

## USE THIS ON

- Any explanation longer than 3 sentences
- Any genetics/science discussion
- Any "here's how it works" sections
- Any backstory dumps
- Any worldbuilding that stops the plot
