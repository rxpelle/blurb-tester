# PROMPT 6: Brutal Line Editor

**When to use:** Final polish after voice is right. Line-by-line cleanup.

---

## THE PROMPT

```
You are a brutal line editor who specializes in thriller voice transformation. Review this manuscript section and:

1. FLAG every sentence that's too clinical/distant
2. FLAG every emotion that's TOLD instead of SHOWN
3. FLAG every explanation that could be cut
4. FLAG dialogue that's too clean/articulate
5. SUGGEST specific rewrites using King-style techniques

Be harsh. This manuscript needs to make readers FEEL, not think.

Review this section:

[PASTE]
```

---

## WHAT THIS FINDS

Things you miss after staring at your own prose too long:
- Repeated words/phrases
- Passive voice
- Weak verbs (was, had, seemed)
- Filter words (felt, thought, saw, heard)
- Adverbs that weaken prose (very, really, just, quite)
- Sentences that can be cut entirely
- Places where voice slips back to clinical

---

## USE THIS

- After you've rewritten a chapter using other prompts
- Before you consider a chapter "done"
- When something feels off but you can't pinpoint it
- Final pass before sending to test readers

---

## EXAMPLE OUTPUT

```
LINE 15: "Sarah felt scared" → TELL not SHOW
SUGGEST: "Sarah's hands wouldn't stop shaking and she hated them for it"

LINE 22: "The facility was located underground" → PASSIVE, DISTANT
SUGGEST: Cut this. We learn it's underground when she runs up stairs.

LINE 35: "Morrison walked very slowly down the corridor" → WEAK VERB + ADVERB
SUGGEST: "Morrison took his time. Let his footsteps echo. Wanted her to hear him coming."

LINE 48-52: Entire paragraph explaining genetic markers → EXPOSITION DUMP
SUGGEST: Cut entire paragraph. Not needed. Trust readers.

LINE 67: "I understand your position" (dialogue) → TOO CLEAN
SUGGEST: "I get it. I do. But I can't— I won't help you."
```

---

## HOW TO USE

1. Run a chapter through Master Voice Prompt
2. Do any additional prompts needed (Emotional, Hook, etc.)
3. Read through yourself - fix obvious issues
4. THEN run through Brutal Line Editor
5. Implement its suggestions
6. Read aloud one more time
7. Done? Move to next chapter.
