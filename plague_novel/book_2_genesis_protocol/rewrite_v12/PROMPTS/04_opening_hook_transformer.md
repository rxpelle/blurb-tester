# PROMPT 4: Opening Hook Transformer

**When to use:** Every chapter opening. Hook readers in first 3 sentences or they skip.

---

## THE PROMPT

```
This chapter opening is too slow. King's rule: Hook readers in first 3 sentences or they skip the chapter.

TECHNIQUES:
1. Start with action or shocking statement
2. Start in the middle of something happening
3. Start with a specific, visceral detail that makes no sense yet
4. Start with a voice that demands attention

BAD OPENING:
"Sarah sat in her cell, thinking about what had happened."

GOOD OPENINGS:
- "The fire alarm shrieked and Sarah ran." (action)
- "Twenty-three million people and she'd killed them all with the word 'no'." (shocking)
- "The coffee cup had his fingerprints still on it." (specific detail)
- "Here's what nobody tells you about being complicit in genocide: you get used to it." (voice)

RULES:
- No setup/context in first paragraph
- No description of setting
- No backstory
- Start with the most interesting moment
- Explain context later (readers will wait if hooked)

Rewrite this chapter opening to hook immediately:

[PASTE OPENING]
```

---

## EXAMPLES

**BEFORE (Slow Start):**
> Chapter 3: Sarah woke up in her cell. It was Tuesday. She'd been imprisoned for two weeks now. The guards brought breakfast at 7 AM as usual.

**AFTER (Hooked):**
> Chapter 3: The guard's name was Marcus and his sister was going to die and he knew it and Sarah knew it and neither of them had said it out loud yet.

---

**WHAT CHANGED:**
- Immediate human stakes
- Mystery (why is sister dying?)
- Conflict implied
- We care about Marcus immediately
- No setup, just start

---

## USE THIS ON

- Every single chapter opening
- Any scene that takes too long to get interesting
- Anywhere readers might be tempted to skim
