# PROMPT 3: Dialogue / Internal Monologue Humanizer

**When to use:** Character interactions, internal thoughts, any dialogue that feels too clean/articulate.

---

## THE PROMPT

```
This dialogue/internal monologue is too clean, too articulate. Real people (especially under stress) don't talk/think this clearly.

REWRITE USING:

1. INTERRUPTION:
   - Characters interrupt themselves
   - "I'm not going to— fuck. Okay. Maybe I'm going to."
   - Thoughts derail mid-sentence

2. REPETITION:
   - When stressed, people repeat themselves
   - "He was going to die. David was going to die. He was going to—"

3. SENTENCE FRAGMENTS:
   - "Lagos. 23 million. Gone. Because she— no. She couldn't—"

4. WRONG WORDS:
   - Characters say the wrong thing, realize it, correct
   - "I'm fine. I'm— no, I'm not fine. I'm really not fine."

5. PHYSICAL INTERRUPTION:
   - (Her voice cracked. She swallowed. Tried again.)
   - Morrison paused to sip coffee. Let the silence work.

6. SUBTEXT:
   - What they're REALLY saying vs what they say
   - "I'm just tired." (Translation: I'm about to break)

7. PROFANITY (Strategic):
   - People curse when under extreme stress
   - But overuse = loses impact
   - Use for emotional peaks only

Rewrite this dialogue/monologue:

[PASTE HERE]
```

---

## EXAMPLES

**BEFORE (Too Clean):**
> "I understand your position, but I cannot cooperate with genocide."

**AFTER (More Human):**
> "I get it. I mean, I understand what you're— no. No, I don't. I can't help you kill people. I won't."

---

**BEFORE (Too Clean):**
> Morrison said, "The mathematics are clear. We save more lives this way."

**AFTER (More Real):**
> Morrison sipped his coffee. Let the silence stretch. "The math," he said finally, "doesn't care what you think is right. Four billion dead versus seven billion dead. That's not philosophy. That's just— it's just arithmetic."
