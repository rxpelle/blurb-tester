### Story Summary
Thomas of Eltville, a scribe in 1347 Mainz, discovers margin dots in manuscripts that reveal a 3,200-year conspiracy. The Order (later called "the Network") has been engineering human evolution through controlled catastrophes—breeding resilient bloodlines, triggering disasters, harvesting survivors' genetics. Thomas, his warrior companion Margarethe, and Jewish physician Maria (age 14) steal the breeding records and distribute copies to eleven cities, reversing the flow of centralized knowledge. They escape to Wales where they teach literacy to children, planting seeds of resistance across generations.

### Timeline Arc
- **1347 CE:** Thomas discovers the conspiracy in Mainz
- **Spring 1348:** Distribution of Genesis Protocol to eleven cities
- **1350 CE (Epilogue):** Thomas and Margarethe in Welsh mountains with son Wilhelm (10 months), pregnant with second child; Marcus the Physician joins as turncoat teacher

## THEMES & PHILOSOPHICAL FRAMEWORK

### Primary Theme: Centralized vs. Distributed Knowledge

**The Order's Model:**
- Knowledge flows **inward** (many monasteries → few controllers)
- Concentration = power and coordination
- Secrecy enables conspiracy
- Reinforcing loop: Control → Records → Prediction → Positioning → More Control

**The Resistance Model:**
- Knowledge flows **outward** (one source → many cities → countless scribes)
- Distribution = resilience and freedom
- Exposure breaks coordination
- Balancing loop: Distribution → Redundancy → Suppression Impossible → Freedom

**Medieval Metaphors:**
- **Order:** "Single deep taproot—cut it, all dies"
- **Resistance:** "Scattered grain—burn one field, others survive"

---

### Core System Dynamics Concepts (Medieval Translation)

**Modern Term → Medieval Language:**

1. **Reinforcing Loop** → "The Wheel That Turns Itself"
   - "Each revolution feeds the next"
   - "Like fire spreading—each flame births more flames"

2. **Stock (accumulation)** → "Treasury" or "Store"
   - "Knowledge accumulated across generations"
   - "A treasury of blood records, growing year by year"

3. **Flow (rate)** → "Current" or "Stream"
   - "Knowledge flows from father to son"
   - "The stream of information that feeds their power"

4. **Delay** → "Lag of Generations"
   - "What they plant now bears fruit in their grandchildren's time"
   - "Three generations must pass before the harvest"

5. **Threshold/Tipping Point** → "Breaking Point"
   - "When the dam bursts, the flood cannot be stopped"
   - "Push far enough, and the tower falls entirely"

6. **Distributed Network** → "Scattered Seeds"
   - "Like grain scattered across many fields—if one burns, others survive"

7. **Centralized Network** → "Single Root"
   - "All power flows to one source"
   - "Like a tree with one deep taproot—cut it, all dies"

**Language Principles:**
- ✓ Never use modern systems terminology
- ✓ Use medieval metaphors (agriculture, architecture, nature, commerce)
- ✓ Characters discover principles through observation, not theory
- ✓ Emphasize time scales medieval people understood (generations, lifetimes, seasons)
- ✓ Make it feel like discovery, not exposition

**Complete Translation Guide:** See [MEDIEVAL_SYSTEMS_LANGUAGE.md](plague_novel/book_1_aethelred_cipher/MEDIEVAL_SYSTEMS_LANGUAGE.md)
