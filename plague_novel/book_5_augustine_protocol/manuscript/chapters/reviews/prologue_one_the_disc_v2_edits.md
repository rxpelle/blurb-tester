# Edit Suggestions (14 edits)

## Edit 1 (Opening dateline / epigraph)
**Source:** Accessibility Editor — Reader Orientation / Historical Identity (major)
**Rationale:** Identifies the protagonist for readers who will not recognise 'Aurelius' + dying mother + Ostia + 387 CE as Augustine of Hippo. The name dramatically raises the stakes of the disc: a reader who knows anything of Augustine carries that knowledge into every sentence that follows.

**Original:**
```
*Ostia, the rented room above the harbour street, the morning of the seventeenth of November 387 CE.*
```

**Revised:**
```
*Ostia, the rented room above the harbour street, the morning of the seventeenth of November 387 CE.*
*The man in the room is Aurelius Augustinus — later called Augustine of Hippo, Bishop, Doctor of the Church, and one of the most consequential minds in the history of the Western world. He is thirty-three years old. His mother is dying.*
```

## Edit 2 (First entry into Monica's room — paragraph beginning 'He pushed it.')
**Source:** Immersive Experience Editor — Sensory Detail / Missing Olfactory Ground (minor)
**Rationale:** The chapter is rich in touch and sound but has no olfactory register until the very end. One smell on entry grounds the reader physically in the sickroom before the emotional work begins, and weaves the closing courtyard smell into a sensory pattern rather than leaving it isolated.

**Original:**
```
He pushed it. The hinge made the small sound it had made all nineteen nights. He stepped in.
```

**Revised:**
```
He pushed it. The hinge made the small sound it had made all nineteen nights. He stepped in. The room smelled of lamp oil and something beneath it — faint, specific — that he had learned in nine nights not to name.
```

## Edit 3 (Monica's request for the box — paragraph beginning 'He had been in the room perhaps the count of a hundred')
**Source:** Immersive Experience Editor — Showing vs Telling (minor)
**Rationale:** The declaration 'He was not ready' is redundant: the self-examination that follows — his admission that he has been carrying the box for seven months without lifting its lid — already demonstrates his unreadiness. Cutting the tell lets the more interesting confession stand alone.

**Original:**
```
He was not ready. He had not known she would speak the word *box.*
```

**Revised:**
```
He had not known she would speak the word *box.*
```

## Edit 4 (Monica's deathbed speech — 'It was given to Simplicianus' sentence)
**Source:** Accessibility Editor — Jargon Management / Unexplained Proper Noun (minor)
**Rationale:** For Augustine scholars the name Simplicianus is charged; for general readers it is a floating Latin name in an already dense scene. One appositive anchors it in the chain-of-custody logic that functions as the scene's thriller mechanism without interrupting Monica's dying cadence.

**Original:**
```
It was given to Simplicianus by my own teacher in the year before you went to Carthage.
```

**Revised:**
```
It was given to Simplicianus — your bishop at Milan, the man who finished your conversion — by my own teacher in the year before you went to Carthage.
```

## Edit 5 (Augustine opening the box — sentence 'He would, on every occasion...')
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Prose / Sentence-Level Error (minor)
**Rationale:** Resolves the dangling preposition — 'carry the sound of this morning with' reads as an incomplete sentence on first pass and remains unresolved rather than artfully suspended on re-read. The restructured version converts the trailing preposition into a positive, thematically resonant claim.

**Original:**
```
He would, on every occasion of opening this box for the rest of his life, carry the sound of this morning with.
```

**Revised:**
```
He would, on every occasion of opening this box for the rest of his life, hear this morning first.
```

## Edit 6 (First sight of the disc — paragraph beginning 'Inside was a thin disc of bronze')
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Genre / Narrative Drive (critical)
**Rationale:** The markings are the disc's intellectual hook — the puzzle the reader must be given a question about. The current version gives them one clause. The revision provides a beat of genuine incomplete recognition: the reader formulates the question 'what does it say?' without receiving an answer. The warmth remains numinous; the markings become the forward-driving mystery.

**Original:**
```
Inside was a thin disc of bronze the size of his palm. It was polished on one face. It was marked on the other with a pattern of small holes he could not, even with the lamp at the angle that gave the best light, read.
```

**Revised:**
```
Inside was a thin disc of bronze the size of his palm. It was polished on one face. It was marked on the other with a pattern of small holes arranged in a double arc that, at the angle the lamp gave, resolved almost into a letter he recognised — and then, as he tilted the disc a degree further, dissolved into a pattern he did not. He held it at three angles. It was not a letter he knew. It was not nothing, either.
```

## Edit 7 (Augustine taking the disc out of the box — sequence ending 'The disc was warm.')
**Source:** Immersive Experience Editor — Sensory Detail / Supernatural Pivot (major)
**Rationale:** The warmth is the chapter's supernatural pivot, but the disc has no physical reality before it warms. Without a prior mundane ground — the texture of the hole-edges, the smoothness of the polished face — the warmth has nothing to violate. Two sentences of physical specificity give the reader something already in their hands when the disc surprises them.

**Original:**
```
He took it out.

*Hold it.*

He held it.

The disc was warm.
```

**Revised:**
```
He took it out. The marked face was rough under his thumb where the holes had been worked — small, precise, not random. The polished face was smooth as river stone.

*Hold it.*

He held it.

The disc was warm.
```

## Edit 8 (Monica's self-assessment speech — 'I have not been a particularly good carrier' passage)
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Plot Logic / Character Credibility (major)
**Rationale:** Monica delivers precise and authoritative instruction about the disc — its warmth, recognising the right man, the generations of carrying — but no account is given of where that knowledge came from, since she never opened the box. One sentence establishing that her teacher gave her the words (not the object) resolves the credibility gap and deepens the oral-tradition premise that underlies the entire series.

**Original:**
```
I have not been a particularly good carrier. I have carried the box in the back of a cabinet in three houses. I have not, in thirty years, opened it.
```

**Revised:**
```
I have not been a particularly good carrier. I have carried the box in the back of a cabinet in three houses. I have not, in thirty years, opened it. My teacher did not ask me to open it. She asked me to carry the words she gave me, and to give them to you when the time came. The words are what I am giving you now.
```

## Edit 9 (Monica's death — paragraph 'A small sound came out of Adeodatus')
**Source:** Immersive Experience Editor — Showing vs Telling / Visceral Moment (major)
**Rationale:** The original describes the category of the sound rather than rendering it. The chapter earns its grief through physical specificity throughout; this is the one moment it retreats into description-of-description. The revision gives the specific phoneme — brief, involuntary, precise — and retains the following sentence to preserve the rhythm of Augustine's restraint.

**Original:**
```
A small sound came out of Adeodatus at the foot of the bed. It was the sound a boy makes when he has been holding himself together and the holding has, at the moment, given way. Augustine did not look at him.
```

**Revised:**
```
A sound came from the foot of the bed — a single caught breath, high and involuntary, the kind that surprises the throat that makes it. Augustine did not look at him.
```

## Edit 10 (Vigil — first 'He thought of' paragraph (Cassiciacum memory))
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Pacing / Redundancy (major)
**Rationale:** Trims the Cassiciacum memory by approximately a third. The reader has already heard Monica's words in the scene; restating them and then extending the gloss overexplains the pivot the reader has already made. The compressed version preserves the callback and the interpretive turn; the Tagaste memory that immediately follows lands more cleanly.

**Original:**
```
He thought of the well at Cassiciacum in May and the way her hand had rested on the rim of the stone — flat, the fingers slightly spread, the way she had laid her hand on his head when he had been a small child and she had wanted him still. She had said: *Aurelius. I have nothing left to want in this world.* He had heard the words then as the words of a woman finished with a long anxious labour. He heard them now, in the rented room with her hand cooling in his, as the words of a woman who had stopped, in the saying, to allow herself to know what she was about to allow her body to do.
```

**Revised:**
```
He thought of Cassiciacum in May — her hand flat on the rim of the well, fingers slightly spread. She had said: *I have nothing left to want in this world.* He had heard it then as relief. He heard it now as permission she had been granting herself, slowly, since the moment of the saying.
```

## Edit 11 (Vigil — 'He thought of Una' paragraph (full section))
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Structure / Tonal Architecture (critical); Immersive Experience Editor — Showing vs Telling / Structural Immersion Break (major); Developmental Editor — Thriller Architecture / Payoff Seeding (suggestion); Accessibility Editor — Reader Orientation / Named Character Without Context (minor)
**Rationale:** Addresses four overlapping notes: removes the backstory-logistics Una paragraph from the vigil's most emotionally concentrated moment; inserts a beat of genuine intellectual attention to the disc's marked face, developing the thriller register the vigil lacked and deepening the reader's question about what the markings say; compresses Una to a single image implying the severed relationship without summarising it; and the phrase 'sent back from Milan before his baptism' gives general readers enough grip on the wound without requiring biographical knowledge of Augustine's unnamed concubine.

**Original:**
```
He thought of Una.

Una was at Carthage. She had been at Carthage for sixteen months. She had not been told, in any letter he had sent, of the baptism. She had not been told of his mother's illness. She would, in some week not many weeks from now, be told of his mother's death by a brother of hers he would write to with the news. She would not be told by him. The not-telling-her was, on his understanding of his own conduct as it had developed across the months since he had sent her back, going to be one of the things he carried that the disc had nothing to do with.
```

**Revised:**
```
He turned the disc in his right hand and brought the marked face into the light. He had seen it for the count of a minute before she had told him to hold it. He looked now with more care. The holes were small and precisely worked — twelve or perhaps fourteen; the light was not sufficient to be certain. Arranged in a double arc, they resolved, at one angle, into something that was almost a letter, or the near-half of a letter from his Greek or from some other alphabet he half-remembered — and then, as he tilted the disc a degree further, became only precision without legible intent. He held it at three angles. He could not read it. He was certain it was not nothing.

He thought of Una — sent back from Milan before his baptism, before he had become the man he had decided, in that year, to become. He would write to her brother. He would not write to her. The shape of what that meant was a thing the disc had nothing to do with, and he set it aside, and sat.
```

## Edit 12 (Monica's dialogue — 'The ship is at the third hour')
**Source:** Accessibility Editor — Jargon Management / Roman Timekeeping (major)
**Rationale:** Modern readers will not know that 'the third hour' corresponds to approximately 9 AM. The vigil's structure and the ship's departure hinge on this time logic; a reader who misses it will experience the closing image — the ship sailing without Monica — as vague rather than precisely weighted.

**Original:**
```
The ship is at the third hour. The ship will be without us.
```

**Revised:**
```
The ship is at the third hour — mid-morning. The ship will be without us.
```

## Edit 13 (Courtyard scene — the November air description)
**Source:** Immersive Experience Editor — Motif Architecture / Closure (suggestion)
**Rationale:** Closes the chapter's thermal arc: cold floor → cool hand → warm disc → hand going cold → disc no longer warm. Augustine has just set the box down; stepping into November air is the natural landing for the motif. 'Nothing in it of the room above' completes the arc without labouring it. 'He stood in it. He let it be cold.' also functions as a beat of physical and emotional transition before the final exchange.

**Original:**
```
The air in the courtyard was the air of a November morning after rain in a town the sea had begun to take its winter back from, and the air smelled of wet stone and the kelp the tide had brought up against the breakwater in the night.
```

**Revised:**
```
The air in the courtyard was cold — a clean cold, the cold of rain and sea and stone, nothing in it of the room above. It smelled of wet stone and the kelp the tide had brought up against the breakwater in the night. He stood in it. He let it be cold.
```

## Edit 14 (Final two paragraphs of the chapter)
**Source:** Developmental Editor — Chapter Hook / Ending (major)
**Rationale:** The chapter's most propulsive line — 'I am beginning the work' — is immediately defused by two paragraphs of interior explanation and a quiet inward arrival. Ending on that exchange faces the chapter forward; everything after it faces inward. The explanation restates what the reader already knows; the quiet final arrival closes on arrival rather than departure. Cutting both paragraphs makes the last word directional — which is what a thriller prologue needs to pull the reader into chapter 1.

**Original:**
```
He did not, he understood as he said the words, know yet what the work was going to require of him in the years to come. He knew only that the words his mother had used in the last hour of her life were the words he was going to be using to himself for the rest of his.

He went up to the room.
```
