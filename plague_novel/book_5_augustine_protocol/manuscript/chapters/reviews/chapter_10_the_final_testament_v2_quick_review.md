### MAJOR (8)

**1. [Developmental Editor] exposition / dialogue authenticity** (First letter (August 25 section) — the letter to Melania the Younger)
- **Issue:** The letter to Melania reads primarily as a reader status-briefing rather than a valediction between two people who have shared nineteen years of covert work. Melania already knows where the fair copies are, who holds the wax under-drafts, and what the arrangement with Possidius is. The logistical inventory ('the community at Tagaste continues under Sophia... the wax under-drafts of the corpus are with her... the fair copies are at Carthage and Constantinople and Lérins and Rome and with you at Jerusalem') is written for the reader, not for Melania. This blunts the emotional blow of the letter's closing — which is excellent — by burying it in housekeeping.
- **Suggestion:** Reduce the status inventory to one sentence signaling completeness ('The arrangements we made hold; Possidius has the chain'), then expand into the personal register. What does Augustine actually feel about Melania that he has never put in plain words? The line 'the gate at Hippo on a wet evening in November 411 was... one of the days that did not pass the way other days passed' is exactly right — it should be earning more room than the logistics catalog is currently taking.
- **Before:** `The community at Tagaste continues under Sophia and now under whatever leadership the Vandals leave it; the wax under-drafts of the corpus are with her at Tagaste under the arrangements we made; the fair copies are at Carthage and Constantinople and Lérins and Rome and with you at Jerusalem. The carrying continues. The carrying will continue with Possidius after me, as we agreed. He has the chain. The chain is not broken. I have nothing to add to the agreements we made.`
- **After:** `The arrangements hold. Possidius has the chain. The chain is not broken. I have nothing to add to what we agreed. I write only to say what I did not say at the gate, and did not say in any of the nineteen years of letters since: that knowing you did the work did not make the work easier to release.`

**2. [Developmental Editor] thriller register / narrative tension** (Entire chapter)
- **Issue:** The chapter is beautifully accomplished as literary historical fiction, but the thriller designation creates an expectation gap that the chapter never closes. The Vandal siege is present only as ambient thumping — it never actively threatens any character or the chain of transmission within the scene. The bronze disc moves from Augustine to Possidius without obstacle. No decision is forced. No one is afraid of being caught. In a developmental sense, this chapter has no scene where the reader fears the chain will break — which is the thriller's one irreducible demand. The elegy is fully earned; the danger is not.
- **Suggestion:** One targeted intrusion would preserve the register while restoring thriller mechanics. A plausible option: in the final section, as Possidius is wrapping the box for travel, a knock at the residence door — Vandal officers doing a pre-siege inventory of church property, or a deacon who has been talking to the Vandal garrison commander — forces Possidius to make a rapid concealment decision. He doesn't need to fail; he needs to sweat. That twenty-second decision is enough to make the reader feel the danger the chapter currently only reports.

**3. [Immersive Experience Editor] Sensory Detail — Smell Entirely Absent** (Entire chapter, most critically the Helena watch section and the vigil section)
- **Issue:** Smell is wholly absent across all three time-sections. A dying man's room in a North African summer — clay jars of soiled linen, lamp wick burning to the oil, tallow candles lit through the night, a wet wind off the sea, and finally a body washed and laid out — carries a dense olfactory reality that the chapter never acknowledges. Sight, sound, and touch are all deployed with care; smell is simply not there. For a death watch, this is the most intimate register of place, and its absence keeps the reader at a measurable distance from the room.
- **Suggestion:** Add smell in at least three anchoring moments: the predawn room during Helena's watch, the lamp-trimming beat (the wick is smoking — give it a smell), and the vigil with the candles. Each addition needs only one clause. The goal is not atmosphere for its own sake but to put the reader's body in the room.
- **Before:** `The household had banked the small brazier in the corner, but Augustine's hands were cold anyway. His hands had been cold since Tuesday.`
- **After:** `The household had banked the small brazier in the corner; it gave off damp charcoal and old olive oil, and underneath both the salt of the sea and the faint copper edge that had been coming from the clay jar at the foot of the bed since Tuesday. Augustine's hands were cold anyway.`

**4. [Immersive Experience Editor] Showing vs. Telling — Narrator Biography Breaks Scene** (Paragraph beginning 'She was thirty-one.')
- **Issue:** Seven consecutive sentences of narrator summary interrupt the chapter's most intimate scene. We have just had silence, a cup of water held to the lips, Helena waiting without filling the silence — and then the narration steps entirely out of the room to deliver a career dossier. The information is necessary to the thriller thread (Helena as next carrier), but the delivery mode is wrong: it reads as author's notes embedded mid-scene, and it cancels the silence that the scene has earned.
- **Suggestion:** Compress to two sentences of Augustine's interiority in his own register. Let her significance to the chain emerge from how she sits and waits and what she says. The rest of her history can be implied or dissolved into the dialogue and action that follows.
- **Before:** `She was thirty-one. She had come up from the lower monastery as a junior member of the household; she had been with them four years. Augustine had begun by giving her the routine work of the bishop's household — the small letters, the receipts, the management of the lay sisters who came in from the city for the relief work. She had managed all of it without complaint and with a quiet attention that he had begun, in the second year, to take note of. He had begun to teach her the deeper part of the work in the third year.`
- **After:** `She had learned, in four years, to be still without performing stillness — to wait without requiring the silence to mean something. That was the thing he had noticed in the second year. It was the thing, on his present accounting, that would carry the work in Numidia when the diocese was gone. He watched her hands on her knees and thought: she does not fully know this yet. She would know it when the time came.`

**5. [Immersive Experience Editor] Emotional Resonance — Central Fear Not Felt From Inside** (Paragraphs 1–5 (stylus / coughing) and the afternoon delirium section)
- **Issue:** Helena names Augustine's defining fear — 'That the work would not have been enough' — and the exchange lands. But across the chapter we never feel this fear from inside his body. We experience his clarity, his tenderness, his careful dispensation of the letters, his philosophical resignation — but not the fear that the entire project of his life may have been insufficient. The emotion is told to us through Helena rather than experienced through Augustine. For a chapter built inside a dying man's last conscious hours, this is a significant gap.
- **Suggestion:** Plant one moment in the first section — after the coughing, before Augustine takes up the stylus again — where the fear surfaces as a physical sensation that he disciplines back. It does not need to be named. A beat where he holds the soiled linen and does not immediately reach for the clean one. Then he reaches for the stylus. That is enough.
- **Before:** `He set down the stylus. He coughed. The coughing took longer than the coughing this morning had taken. The linen square took its share. Possidius, who had been sitting in the chair by the window, came to the bed... Augustine took up the stylus.`
- **After:** `He set down the stylus. He coughed. The coughing took longer than the coughing this morning had taken. The linen square took its share. He sat with the clean linen Possidius had given him and did not immediately set it aside. A thought he did not permit to finish moved through him — something about the fourteen books and whether they would hold when the only voice that had argued for them was gone — and he closed it in the way he had been closing it all morning, which required, on the morning, more effort than the closing of it had required in August. He set the linen aside. He took up the stylus.`

**6. [Accessibility Editor] concept accessibility / jargon** (Paragraph 3, opening section)
- **Issue:** The *Retractationes* is introduced as the chapter's central intellectual object with no gloss whatsoever. A 10th-grade reader doesn't know this is Augustine's systematic self-correction of his own 37-book lifetime output — a project with no real precedent in Western literature. Without that frame, 'I have retracted what I have retracted' reads as vague piety rather than as the culmination of a four-year act of intellectual humility, and the emotional weight of the chapter's first movement collapses.
- **Suggestion:** One anchoring appositive or a brief clause of Possidius's interior reaction is all you need — not a lecture, just one image that tells the reader what the work IS before naming it.
- **Before:** `He was on the last paragraph of the fourteenth book of the *Retractationes*.`
- **After:** `He was on the last paragraph of the fourteenth book of the *Retractationes* — the long backward look at his own work, thirty-seven books corrected and qualified over four years, the project of a man who had decided that his errors deserved the same care as his arguments.`

**7. [Accessibility Editor] concept accessibility / intellectual stakes** (The 'two cities' exchange, third section (afternoon of the twenty-eighth))
- **Issue:** This is the most intellectually charged moment in the chapter — Augustine questioning his own greatest work on his deathbed — but it assumes the reader knows *De Civitate Dei*. Without knowing that Augustine spent thirteen years arguing history moves toward divine judgment (the City of God triumphing over the City of Man), 'Bishop, that is heresy' lands as unexplained alarm and 'That is realism' lands as a mild riposte instead of the radical self-revision it is. The emotional beat is clear; the intellectual one is completely invisible to a general reader. This is the chapter's philosophical climax and it fires in the dark.
- **Suggestion:** Add one sentence of grounded context — either through Augustine's interior thought immediately before the line, or through Possidius's reaction — that tells the reader what 'the two cities' ARE. The smart-friend version: Augustine's entire masterwork said history is a war between the earthly and divine city, ending in God's judgment. He is now saying: maybe it doesn't end. That is what makes it heresy.
- **Before:** `*The two cities may not end in judgment,* he said quietly. *They may continue as a permanent dialectic.*

*Bishop, that is heresy.*

*That is realism.*`
- **After:** `*The two cities may not end in judgment,* he said quietly. He had argued, in the twenty-two books, that history moved toward resolution — the City of God prevailing over the earthly city, the conflict ending in divine verdict. He was not, at the end of the afternoon, certain that was true. *They may continue as a permanent dialectic.*

*Bishop, that is heresy.*

*That is realism.*`

**8. [Accessibility Editor] jargon density / concept accessibility** (Deathbed confession to Possidius ('Include the mistakes'), third section)
- **Issue:** Three distinct theological controversies are dropped in rapid succession — Pelagius, the Donatist confiscations, the grace/choice debate — with no context for any of them. A general reader follows the emotional beat (old man admits he was wrong) but the *substance* of what he was wrong about is invisible. This is precisely the kind of scene where intellectual specificity earns its keep: the difference between a generic deathbed humility scene and this one is that Augustine is naming actual, weighty failures. But without one foothold, a 10th-grade reader experiences only the generic version.
- **Suggestion:** You don't need to gloss all three — that would break the rhythm. Pick the one most load-bearing for your series arc (likely the grace/choice debate, given the 'Architecture of Survival' themes) and give it a single clarifying phrase. Let the others carry their weight from the name alone for readers who recognize them.
- **Before:** `*I was wrong about Pelagius's books in the way I read them in the year of the synod. I was wrong about the Donatist confiscations, which I countersigned for two and a half years and have continued to be wrong about across the rest of my life.*`
- **After:** `*I was wrong about Pelagius's books in the way I read them in the year of the synod — Pelagius said a man could choose good without God's prior help, and I said he was wrong, and the way I said it has done damage I did not intend. I was wrong about the Donatist confiscations, which I countersigned for two and a half years.*`

**1. [Developmental Editor] structure / scene arc** (August 27 section — entire section)
- **Issue:** The section's most significant move — Augustine promising Helena 'a piece of the work I have not given you' — is a deferral. The promise doesn't resolve within the section; it resolves in the following section during the death scene. As a tension device this works, but the August 27 section consequently lacks its own internal arc. It begins and ends in the same emotional register (quiet watch, tender exchange, practical survival logistics) without a turn. One option: after Helena's observation about what Augustine feared, he asks her something she can't answer cleanly — something that reveals a gap in the piece he still owes her, or that makes him change what piece he intends to give. **5. [Immersive Experience Editor] Emotional Resonance — Delirium Memory Sequence Too Compressed** (Paragraph beginning 'When less lucid he was somewhere else.')

- **Issue:** 'Each cycle was requiring more conscious effort. The pneumonia was winning the patient battle it had been winning for months.' This is accurate but clinical — the reader understands it as a medical fact rather than feeling it. The opening of the chapter handled the breath much more viscerally: 'Each breath had become work. Each breath would, in some not-very-distant week, become more work than was worth the doing.' By the death scene, the language has retreated from the body.
- **Suggestion:** One additional line at the body level before the final speech. Not medical vocabulary — the physical sensation of breath as a door that requires more force to open each time.

- **After:** `His breathing grew shallower — each intake a small deliberate act now, the way a man lifts something heavier than he expected to find it. The sound it made in the room was a dry sound. Possidius leaned closer. 'The third thing,' Augustine said.`

**7. [Accessibility Editor] reader orientation / unexplained callback** (Possidius opens the bronze box, final section)
- **Issue:** 'He had been told, in 411, that the sound carried two nights.' This is the only unexplained callback in the chapter and it stands out against otherwise clean prose. 'The sound carried two nights' has evident significance — it implies the disc's sound is a marker of transfer, not mere acoustics — but the mechanism or meaning is never clarified. A general reader either reads it as poetic amplification (losing the thriller implication) or pauses to wonder what it means (breaking the chapter's closing momentum, which is immaculate up to this point).
- **Suggestion:** Either cut the 411 callback entirely and let the sentence stand as present-tense observation, or add four to six words that anchor what 'carried two nights' means. The reader doesn't need the history — just enough to know whether the sound travels spatially, temporally, or in dreams.
- **Before:** `He had been told, in 411, that the sound carried two nights.`
- **After:** `He had been told, in 411, that the sound carried two nights — that the next keeper would hear it in sleep before the box was in his hands. He understood, holding the box now, that the sound carried more nights than that.`

**8. [Accessibility Editor] thriller element / supernatural ambiguity** (Possidius handles the disc, final section)
- **Issue:** The disc's warmth is the chapter's thriller payoff, but at Chapter 11 a reader whose memory of earlier chapters has faded doesn't have a clear framework for what they're experiencing. The deliberate ambiguity ('on his understanding, only the disc acknowledging that the carrying had passed') is exactly the right register, but 'on his understanding' implies an earlier explanation that may now be too distant. Without a brief anchor, the ambiguity risks reading as confusion rather than as the intentional mystery it is.
- **Suggestion:** Add a half-sentence before Possidius interprets the warmth — a clause referencing what the disc has previously been understood to be. Don't resolve the ambiguity; orient the reader just enough to let them experience it as intended.
- **Before:** `The disc was warm.`
- **After:** `The disc was warm. Not the warmth of recent handling — no one had touched it in ten weeks — but the warmth it had been known to carry at the moments of transfer, the warmth Augustine had described on three or four occasions without ever explaining.`

### SUGGESTION (3)

**1. [Developmental Editor] pacing / prose rhythm** (Third letter (August 25 section) — the letter to Eraclius, middle passage)
- **Issue:** The letter to Eraclius is the chapter's most unexpectedly human moment — a bishop praising a subordinate in terms he has withheld for nineteen years. But the praise arrives in four sequential clauses ('you are a better administrator... the brothers love you... the lay brothers respect you... the matrons of the city trust you'), and the enumeration blunts the emotional impact of each claim. The final sentence ('The voice was the voice of a man who depended on you for nineteen years') is excellent; it needs more weight around it.
- **Suggestion:** Cut two of the four praise clauses. Let the ones kept earn their room by being specific rather than typological. The silence of what Augustine doesn't say will do more work than an exhaustive list.

**2. [Developmental Editor] scene grounding / thriller object** (Final section — Possidius opening the bronze box)
- **Issue:** The disc's warmth is well-handled and the restraint about not seeking a sign is exactly right for the series' intellectual register. But the scene gives us no visual detail of the disc itself when the box opens — we get warmth and sound (the hinges) but no surface, no marking, no material quality. For the chapter's most charged thriller object, one grounding image would deepen the moment without overwriting it.
- **Suggestion:** One sentence of what Possidius sees when the box opens — the disc's color, its worn surface, any mark on it — before he picks it up. The warmth then registers against a concrete thing rather than an absence.

**3. [Immersive Experience Editor] Visceral Moment — The Disc's Warmth Deserves One More Sentence** (Paragraph beginning 'He took out the disc.')
- **Issue:** The warm disc is the chapter's strongest visceral beat and the thriller's structural fulcrum. The author wisely refuses to explain it. But after naming the warmth and characterising it ('not coming or going but settled'), the passage moves immediately to interpretation. We don't get one sentence of what a warmth that is 'settled' actually feels like in a man's palms at midnight, holding something in a cold room over the body of his teacher.
- **Suggestion:** Add one clause that stays in the physical register before the mind interprets. The warmth should leave a mark on the hands.
- **Before:** `It was warm in the way the box on a high shelf in a cool room had no business being warm, and it was warm in a way that was new — not the way Augustine had described the warmth on the three or four occasions Augustine had described it, but a different warmth, a steadier one, a warmth that did not seem to be coming or going but to be settled.`
- **After:** `It was warm in the way the box on a high shelf in a cool room had no business being warm, and it was warm in a way that was new — not the way Augustine had described the warmth on the three or four occasions Augustine had described it, but a different warmth, a steadier one, a warmth that did not seem to be coming or going but to be settled, that moved into the palms and stayed there, so that when he finally set the disc down on the desk he could still feel the shape of it against his skin.`
