**1. [Developmental Editor] tonal register / genre contract** (Paragraph beginning 'He went to the upper shelf' (Augustine in the writing room, late section))
- **Issue:** The bronze disc described as warm 'with a heat of its own' despite sitting on a cold shelf for ten years is a hard tonal rupture. Every prior detail in the chapter — the mule smell, the watered wine, the waxed linen thread, the smell of a gut opened eleven days ago — is scrupulously material. A supernaturally warm artifact introduced without preparation risks genre confusion. If this is series mythology requiring a supernatural layer, Chapter 1 is too early and too subtle: the reader has no frame for it. - **Suggestion:** Either collapse the warmth into Augustine's subjective state ('It was warm in his hand — or he was cold enough that morning to feel it so') or, if the disc is genuinely supernatural throughout the series, seed a single preparatory note earlier in the chapter — perhaps during the Marcus scene, a brief reference to Augustine's sense that the world contains more than it shows — so the reader has a frame when the disc appears. As written, the chapter has been an exercise in cold reportage, and this moment asks the reader to upgrade to a different genre without permission.
- **Before:** `It was warm now. The warmth was not the warmth of a thing that had been against another warm thing. The bronze had been on a high shelf in a cool room behind a closed lid for ten years, and it was warm in his hand the way a child's hand is warm, with a heat of its own.`
- **After:** `It was warm in his hand when he opened the lid — or he was cold enough from the night's work that his hands read warmth into anything they held. He could not have said which. He did not try.`

### SUGGESTION (5)

**1. [Developmental Editor] character / interiority** (Cyprian section throughout — approximately paragraphs 1–18)
- **Issue:** The operative is precise, cold, and professionally riveting. But the reader gets nothing to hold onto beneath the surface. One line earns particular curiosity — 'He understood now why he had been sent and not killed himself' — and then the chapter drops it. The implication (he thought the network might send a second operative to kill him after he read the document; or alternatively, the document itself is what forestalled his killing) is left unresolved, which is fine as a series thread. But the reader needs a single interior beat — not backstory, not motivation, just the texture of the man — to make the threat to him feel real. Without it, he's a function rather than a character.
- **Suggestion:** One short sentence of interiority in the walk north. Not an explanation. A sensation: 'The road was empty. He had thought it would not be.' Or a register of what the document cost him: 'He was tired in a way he had not been tired since Antioch, and that tiredness had a shape he recognised.' The chapter has earned the restraint; it hasn't earned complete opacity.
- **Before:** `He had read what was in the packet. He understood now why he had been sent and not killed himself. The cargo would wait.`
- **After:** `He had read what was in the packet. He understood now why he had been sent and not killed himself. He had expected to be followed out of the inn. He was not followed. The road was empty and the emptiness had the quality of a decision made by someone who was not him.`

**2. [Developmental Editor] pacing** (Paragraph beginning 'Forty-one entries. Seven headings.' (Augustine reads the document))
- **Issue:** The document's content is delivered in a three-paragraph summary that gives enough specifics to be intellectually interesting but not enough to be alarming. The reader is told the forty-one conditions map onto Rome and then onto Hippo, but the shift from historical observation to immediate personal recognition ('read them as Hippo') happens quickly and in summary. This is the chapter's intellectual climax — the moment Augustine understands what he's holding — and it's delivered at the same pace as the preceding administrative material. The emotional weight of 'he read them as Hippo' deserves a beat of silence.
- **Suggestion:** After the line 'he read them, after Rome, as Hippo,' add a single paragraph break and one short sentence before the Latin glosses. Not elaboration — just arrest: 'He stopped. He set the papyrus down. He picked it up again.' The structural pause signals to the reader that the intellectual recognition is also a physical event.

He was watching the road.`
- **After:** `He had counted four crossings since he sat down. None had been imperial. He listened for the fifth.`

**4. [Immersive Experience Editor] over-explanation / complete image glossed** (Confessional scene, paragraph describing the girl's clean hands)
- **Issue:** 'closed something in his throat that did not want to be closed' appends an explanatory clause to an already complete and devastating image. A child finding water in a refugee warehouse and washing her hands before coming to ask whether God permitted her grandmother to burn does all necessary work on its own. The interiority clause tells the reader what to feel after the image has already made them feel it.
- **Suggestion:** Cut 'that did not want to be closed.' The shorter phrase is both more restrained and more powerful.
- **Before:** `the thought of that — a child finding water in a refugee warehouse and washing her hands before asking a bishop whether God had permitted her grandmother to be burned alive on her own table — closed something in his throat that did not want to be closed.`
- **After:** `the thought of that — a child finding water in a refugee warehouse and washing her hands before asking a bishop whether God had permitted her grandmother to be burned alive on her own table — closed something in his throat.`

**5. [Accessibility Editor] concept accessibility — Polybius VI reference** (paragraph beginning 'The Latin glosses in the margins gave the phrase in plain Latin')
- **Issue:** The marginalia cites 'cf. Polybius vi' — Polybius Book VI being where Polybius describes anacyclosis, the theory of cyclical constitutional collapse. This is the intellectual lineage from which the document's entire mechanism descends. General readers will skip past it as a learned footnote. Even educated readers may not recognize the significance. The reference is the glossator telling Augustine: this document belongs to the tradition that already mapped civilizational cycles — and that tradition predicted Rome too.
