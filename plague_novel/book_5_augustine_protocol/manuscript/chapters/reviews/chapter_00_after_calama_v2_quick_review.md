### CRITICAL (2)

**1. [Developmental Editor] Thriller Drive / Narrative Stakes** (Autumn coda, paragraph 1)
- **Issue:** The Donatist attack on Possidius — the inciting incident of the entire chapter — is resolved quietly in three sentences: proceedings concluded, two priests convicted, one exiled, 'immediate threat... reduced.' For a thriller, the attack should complicate into larger stakes, not close as administrative tidy-up. Who commissioned the attack? Were the convicted priests principals or proxies? The coda answers questions the thriller should be asking, and does so entirely off-page.
- **Suggestion:** Either remove the resolution from this chapter and leave the proceedings ongoing, or introduce a complication within the resolution — a detail in the autumn correspondence suggesting the convicted priests were intermediaries, not principals. The chapter should exit on an open threat, not a closed case.
- **Before:** `In the autumn the Donatist proceedings in the next valley to Calama concluded with the conviction of two priests and the quiet exile of a third. The attack on the bishop on the road in August was the principal evidence.`
- **After:** `In the autumn the proceedings concluded. Two priests were convicted. A third was exiled to Caesarea. In his October letter Possidius wrote only of the roof. He did not write of the man who had commissioned them, who had not been found.`

**2. [Immersive Experience Editor] Scene Grounding / Sensory Absence** (Paragraphs 1–10 (cart arrival sequence, opening through gate))
- **Issue:** This is Hippo Regius on the North African coast in the second week of August. Augustine has been at the gate for two hours. The cart has been on the road through a summer night and morning. Yet the chapter contains no August heat whatsoever — no sun, no dust, no animal smell, no sweat, no shimmer off the harbour road. The arrival scene is organizationally precise but climatically absent. A man standing two hours at a North African gate in August should feel like a man in a furnace. Augustine is standing in a vacuum. This is the chapter's establishing scene and its most significant immersion failure.
- **Suggestion:** Ground the scene with two or three physical anchors — the quality of the afternoon light, the heat rising off the harbour-road stone, the smell arriving with the cart before the cart arrives. Do not belabor it; let the climate be a condition the scene exists inside, not a description the scene stops to deliver.
- **Before:** `Augustine was at the gate with Stephen.

That had been the second hour. The two of them had been at the gate since.`
- **After:** `Augustine was at the gate with Stephen. The gate arch held the morning's heat the way Hippo stone held it in August, which was to give it back through the soles of the feet. The harbour road was white.

That had been the second hour. The two of them had been at the gate since.`

### MAJOR (9)

**1. [Developmental Editor] Structure / Redundancy** (November disc section (final section, paragraphs 3–8))
- **Issue:** The November disc-check restates the night-of-day-6 scene beat for beat: retrieve box → hinges sound → disc cold → set back → close → shelf → compline. The self-aware line ('he had been the kind of man who took the disc down on no particular occasion') is the chapter's only new contribution here, but the physical action delivers no new information and no escalation. Two disc-checks with identical results and near-identical blocking within a single chapter signals stasis, not mystery.
- **Suggestion:** Compress the November check to two sentences inside the autumn coda, or cut it and let the night-of scene carry the disc question forward. If kept, it must land differently — a fragment of warmth that immediately fades, a new thought about who the man might be — anything that escalates rather than confirms.
- **Before:** `He opened it. The hinges sounded. He took out the disc. He held it in his hand for a count of perhaps fifty.

The disc was cold.

He set it back. He closed the box. He returned the box to the upper shelf.`
- **After:** `In November he took the disc down. Cold still. He had known it would be. He had been the kind of man who checked. He set it back.`

**2. [Developmental Editor] Character Arc / Stephen** (Departure scene and final writing-room scene)
- **Issue:** Stephen's decision not to attend the gate is the chapter's single most loaded act, but the reader receives only Augustine's inference ('a decision he was prepared to be asked to defend') rather than any observable behavior showing what the decision cost him. He sits at the desk with a half-finished schedule. After four weeks of watching this man through Augustine's perception, the reader arrives at the chapter's end with no legible sense of what Stephen has resolved about his own situation.
- **Suggestion:** Give Stephen one visible behavioral detail in the final writing-room scene that shows — not states — where he has landed. It can be as small as where his eyes go when Augustine says 'The schedule is good.' The chapter has trusted the reader throughout; trust it here too, but give the trust something concrete to work with.
- **Before:** `Stephen looked back. Augustine had been intending, as he had walked back from the gate through the cloister, to say a thing that, in the time it had taken him to come from the gate to the writing room, he had decided not to say.`
- **After:** `Stephen looked back. His hands were folded at the edge of the schedule in the way he folded them when he was finished with a thing — not the way he folded them when he was waiting. Augustine had been intending, as he had walked back from the gate, to say a thing that he had decided, in the corridor, not to say.`

**3. [Developmental Editor] Prose Tic / Rhythm** (Chapter-wide (approximately 10 instances))
- **Issue:** 'The count of' appears as a temporal measure roughly ten times across the chapter: 'the count of three,' 'the count of perhaps a hundred,' 'the count of perhaps three hundred,' 'the count of fifteen,' 'the count of perhaps fifty,' and variants. The gesture is elegant the first two or three times — it places Augustine in liturgical time, counting as a monk counts. At ten instances it loses specificity and reads as authorial habit rather than character register.
- **Suggestion:** Reduce to three or four instances, reserved for the emotionally freighted moments: the hand-hold at the pallet, the disc in the palm, the chapter-house standing. Replace the others with varied temporal language: 'for some time,' 'until the breath steadied,' 'long enough for the heat in his chest to settle.'
- **Before:** `He looked at the linen on Possidius's chest and watched it rise and fall for the count of perhaps three, and the heat in his chest settled into itself and did not go anywhere.`
- **After:** `He looked at the linen on Possidius's chest and watched it rise and fall until it had done so twice, and the heat in his chest settled into itself and did not go anywhere.`

**4. [Immersive Experience Editor] Visceral Moment / Bodily Absence** (Paragraph 12 ('Augustine went to the cart.'))
- **Issue:** Augustine approaches the cart and sees Possidius for the first time. What follows is clinical inventory — wound on head, bandage clean, swelling at the shoulder, the face holding 'a distance.' The 'cool hand' is the only physical sensation, and it arrives after the looking. This is the chapter's first visceral peak — first sight of a ten-year friend beaten and left for dead — and we never feel it in Augustine's body. The reader understands the moment intellectually. They do not feel it.
- **Suggestion:** Give Augustine one involuntary physical response at the first sight — something that happens before he can manage it. Not a named emotion. A physical event in the chest or the throat or the hands.
- **Before:** `Augustine went to the cart.

Possidius was on the pallet under the canvas. He was awake. His eyes were open and were tracking and were the eyes of a man who had been on a cart on a bad road for thirteen hours...`
- **After:** `Augustine went to the cart.

The thing that happened when he looked over the side of the cart happened before he had finished crossing the yard to it. Possidius was on the pallet under the canvas. He was awake. His eyes were open and were tracking and were the eyes of a man who had been on a cart on a bad road for thirteen hours...`

**5. [Immersive Experience Editor] Sensory Detail / The Cart's Journey Absent from the Cart** (Paragraph 11 ('The cart came through the gate.'))
- **Issue:** The cart has been on the road for thirteen hours in August with a wounded man on the floor. When it arrives, it carries no evidence of that journey. No smell. No dust on the freedman's legs. No sourness under the canvas. No visible exhaustion in the mule beyond a single generic adjective ('tired'). The cart is named but not rendered. Arrivals earn their weight through what they carry in from the road.
- **Suggestion:** Let two or three physical details make the thirteen-hour journey present in the moment the cart crosses the gate. The smell is the most available and most overlooked.
- **Before:** `The junior officer dismounted. He inclined his head to Augustine in the small specific inclination of a Numidian officer to a bishop he had been instructed by his proconsul to be polite to.`
- **After:** `The junior officer dismounted. The smell of the cart came with him — road dust, animal sweat, the iron-flat smell of used bandage linen. He inclined his head to Augustine in the small specific inclination of a Numidian officer to a bishop he had been instructed by his proconsul to be polite to.`

**6. [Immersive Experience Editor] Pacing / Interior Life — Days 4 and 5 of the Watch** (Paragraph beginning 'Augustine saw it on the fourth day.')
- **Issue:** The build to Augustine's sixth-day recognition — the 'coal under ash' moment — is the chapter's emotional center and is genuinely earned. But the approach through days 4 and 5 is mechanically symmetric: saw it, gave it no meaning; saw it again, gave it the same lack of meaning. This is intellectually clear and emotionally inert. The reader is told what Augustine didn't feel across two days. What's needed is the felt texture of almost-recognition: the thing that snagged at the edge of attention and was set aside. The repetition here is administrative rather than cumulative.
- **Suggestion:** Differentiate the two days. On day 4, let Augustine barely notice — the observation is peripheral, nearly lost in the afternoon's work. On day 5, give him a pause he rationalizes his way out of. The sixth day will then feel like arrival rather than announcement.
- **Before:** `Augustine saw it on the fourth day. He did not, on the fourth day, attach a meaning to it. He saw it on the fifth day and gave it the same lack of meaning.`
- **After:** `Augustine saw it on the fourth day at the edge of the thing he was looking at, which was the linen on Possidius's chest. On the fifth day he saw it again and held it in his attention for a count before he looked back at the linen. He did not, on the fifth day, name what he was holding.`

**7. [Accessibility Editor] Jargon / Undefined Faction** (Final section (autumn paragraph) and earlier references)
- **Issue:** 'Donatist' appears as the villain faction three times with zero explanation of who they are or what they believe. For a general reader, they register as a name only — no threat, no texture, no meaning. The attack on Possidius is the inciting wound of the entire chapter, but the reader never understands why a theological rival would beat a bishop and leave him on a basilica floor.
- **Suggestion:** One sentence of in-world exposition, delivered naturally through a character's thought or letter, is enough: what they want (their claim to be the true church), why they're violent (they see the Catholic bishops as traitors who handed over Scripture under Roman persecution). It doesn't need to be a lecture — Augustine drafting one of his four letters is a perfect vehicle.
- **Before:** `The attack on the bishop on the road in August was the principal evidence. Possidius did not, in his correspondence with Augustine through the autumn, refer to the attack or to the proceedings.`
- **After:** `The attack on the bishop on the road in August was the principal evidence — one more act in the long quarrel between the Donatists, who claimed Augustine's church had sold itself to Rome, and everyone Augustine knew. Possidius did not, in his correspondence through the autumn, refer to the attack or to the proceedings.`

**8. [Accessibility Editor] Reader Orientation / Series Assumptions** (Entire chapter — no orientation paragraph)
- **Issue:** The chapter assumes the reader already knows Augustine of Hippo is one of the most consequential thinkers in Western history — that his friendship with Possidius matters, that the bronze disc is a thriller MacGuffin introduced in earlier books, and that Stephen's emotional restraint is a pattern already established. A reader coming in cold has no tether. The opening plunges into administrative detail (escort colors, post-hours, wound reports) before any human stakes are established. For a series novel this may be intentional, but the chapter provides no re-entry ramp for readers who need a beat of reorientation.
- **Suggestion:** A single embedded moment of backstory in the first three paragraphs — Augustine's seven years as bishop, a sentence on who Possidius is to him — would function as orientation without slowing the arrival scene. The cart coming up the road is the right opening; the question is whether the reader cares yet about who is on it.
- **Before:** `Augustine was at the gate with Stephen.`
- **After:** `Augustine was at the gate with Stephen. In seven years as bishop of Hippo he had received a great many carts on that road. He had not received one carrying Possidius.`

**9. [Accessibility Editor] Concept Accessibility / Emotional Subtext** (Section beginning 'By the fourth day Augustine had begun to notice a thing about the watch')
- **Issue:** Augustine's recognition that Stephen loves Possidius is the chapter's emotional climax, but it is written at maximum indirection. The physical signs are specific and well-observed (shoulders, mouth corners, the quiet hand). The 'coal under ash' metaphor is excellent. But the chapter never tells the reader what category of feeling is being recognized — a general reader may register only that 'something' is happening. Readers unfamiliar with same-sex love as a historical reality, or readers who read Stephen's vigil as devoted friendship rather than longing, will miss the stakes of Augustine's deliberate silence and of Stephen's absence at the gate.
- **Suggestion:** One word or one beat more in the recognition passage would anchor it without naming it explicitly. The current text says Augustine 'recognised what he was seeing' — but recognized *what*? The coal metaphor is about heat arriving, not about what the heat is. The reader needs one more degree of specificity, even disguised as euphemism appropriate to Augustine's theological register.
- **Before:** `On the second of the two — Stephen's eyes on Possidius for three counts, the quiet hand in the lap, the soft corner of the mouth — Augustine recognised what he was seeing.`
- **After:** `On the second of the two — Stephen's eyes on Possidius for three counts, the quiet hand in the lap, the soft corner of the mouth — Augustine recognised what he was seeing. He had seen it before, in his own life, in the years before his conversion. He had called it by its name then. He did not call it by its name now.`

### MINOR (6)

**1. [Developmental Editor] Sentence Openers / He-Stack** (Recovery section: 'Stephen was in the room for every one of the visits...')
- **Issue:** Five consecutive sentences open with 'Stephen': 'Stephen was in the room... Stephen sat on the smaller stool... Stephen did not... Stephen kept the cloth... Stephen kept the watch.' The anaphora is clearly deliberate, and liturgical repetition suits the register, but five identical openers in sequence crosses from rhythm into mechanical repetition. The fifth sentence loses force because the ear has already absorbed three identical constructions.
- **Suggestion:** Vary two of the five, particularly the fourth and fifth. The rhythm is recoverable without the exact repetition.
- **Before:** `Stephen kept the cloth in his hand and the basin at his foot. Stephen kept the watch.`
- **After:** `The cloth stayed in his hands, the basin at his foot. He kept the watch.`

**2. [Developmental Editor] Chapter Hook / Opening Paragraph** (Paragraph 1)
- **Issue:** The first paragraph runs 115 words of logistical provenance — the route, the escort's grudging provisioning, the junior officer's advance ride — before a human being appears. All of it earns its place in the chapter, but burying 'Augustine was at the gate with Stephen' in the second paragraph means the chapter opens on bureaucratic texture rather than on the two men who give it its meaning.
- **Suggestion:** Invert the structure: open with Augustine and Stephen at the gate, then deliver the cart's logistical context as it approaches rather than before it enters. Place the reader beside the watchers from the first line.
- **Before:** `The cart came to the basilica gate at the second hour of the afternoon, having travelled the lower coast road through the night and the morning under armed escort... Augustine was at the gate with Stephen.`
- **After:** `Augustine was at the gate with Stephen when the cart came up the harbour road. It had come the lower coast road through the night and the morning under the armed escort the proconsul's office at Carthage had grudgingly provided after the senior bishop had pressed for three days...`

**3. [Immersive Experience Editor] Sentence-Level / Circumlocution Weakening a Good Image** (Paragraph 12 ('The face under the bandage was the face of a man...'))
- **Issue:** The core image — Possidius's face looking at Augustine 'from a distance the face had not previously held in their friendship' — is exactly right. But the scaffolding around it ('was the face of a man Augustine had known for ten years and who, on the present condition of the face') is circuitous and dilutes the landing. The reader does not need the friendship's duration restated at this precise moment.
- **Suggestion:** Trust the image. Cut the scaffolding.
- **Before:** `The face under the bandage was the face of a man Augustine had known for ten years and who, on the present condition of the face, looked at him from a distance the face had not previously held in their friendship.`
- **After:** `The face under the bandage looked at him from a distance it had not previously held.`

**4. [Immersive Experience Editor] Visceral Moment / Emotional Resonance — Farewell Scene** (Final section: 'The look broke. They embraced again...')
- **Issue:** The farewell scene is the chapter's most concentrated emotional moment — Possidius saying Stephen's name, the small set of the mouth, the exchange of looks between the two bishops. The restraint here is correct. But Augustine's body is entirely absent from it. The coal/heat metaphor was established at the sixth-day recognition as the physical register for this thread. At its culminating scene, there is no echo of it. The intellectual architecture of what is passing between the two men is present; the felt experience of it is not.
- **Suggestion:** Give Augustine one small physical note at the moment the look breaks — an echo of the coal metaphor, something in the chest or the hands. It should be brief. It should close the loop.
- **Before:** `The look broke. They embraced again, briefly, in the way of two bishops at a public gate.`
- **After:** `The look broke. The small heat that had arrived on the sixth day was in his chest again, and he let it be there, and then they embraced again, briefly, in the way of two bishops at a public gate.`

**5. [Accessibility Editor] Jargon / Liturgical Vocabulary** (Recurring — three instances: end of first section, end of second section, final paragraph)
- **Issue:** 'Compline' is used as a structural beat marking the end of each day — Augustine goes to compline, the disc is cold, the chapter closes. It's functioning as a chapter-clock, which is effective. But a 10th-grade reader has no idea what compline is. The word sounds vaguely like 'complete,' which is enough to feel right, but it's doing real structural work here (ritual closure, the day's reckoning) and that work is lost if the reader just sees a foreign noun.
- **Suggestion:** One gloss on first use — 'compline, the last prayer of the day' — costs nothing and activates the word's structural function for every subsequent use.
- **Before:** `He went to compline.`
- **After:** `He went to compline — the last office of the day, the prayer that closed the dark.`

**6. [Accessibility Editor] Concept Clarity / Artifact Mechanics** (Section beginning 'He had not taken the box down in some years')
- **Issue:** The disc's key mechanic — warm means the right person is near, cold means they aren't — is the chapter's thriller spine. It's established well enough. But the single moment of warmth is described in a confusing passive construction buried in a list of losses: 'the warmth that had been in the bronze for the whole of an hour and had not, in any other moment in the sixteen years between then and now, been in the bronze at all.' A first-time reader has to unpack this backward: the disc was warm *once*, at Ostia, the night his mother died. When? Why then? The mechanic is crucial but its one proof-of-concept moment is the hardest sentence in the passage to parse.
- **Suggestion:** Restructure so the moment of warmth is stated directly before the sixteen years of cold are stated. The mother's dying night should be a clear reference point, not a relative clause.
- **Before:** `the warmth that had been in the bronze for the whole of an hour and had not, in any other moment in the sixteen years between then and now, been in the bronze at all.`
- **After:** `On the night his mother died at Ostia, the bronze had been warm for the full hour she held it. It had not been warm since. Not once in sixteen years.`

### SUGGESTION (6)

**1. [Developmental Editor] Chapter Close / Architecture** (Chapter-house scene through final line)
- **Issue:** The chapter has a natural close at the chapter-house scene: Augustine resolves to 'watch better,' a small specific forward intention. 'He went to the cathedral for the prayers' performs the chapter's characteristic close-on-routine with full thematic weight already earned. The November disc-check that follows reopens the chapter after it has closed, and 'He went to compline' is a weaker landing than 'He went to the cathedral for the prayers.'
- **Suggestion:** End the chapter at 'He went to the cathedral for the prayers.' Move the Donatist resolution and the November disc-check to the opening of the next chapter as a time-transition. The chapter's final note is then Augustine's intention — forward-facing — rather than a confirmed non-event.
- **Before:** `He went to the cathedral for the prayers.

---

In the autumn the Donatist proceedings in the next valley to Calama concluded... He went to compline.`
- **After:** `[Chapter ends:] He went to the cathedral for the prayers.

[Next chapter opens:] In the autumn the Donatist proceedings concluded...`

**2. [Developmental Editor] Plot Logic / Thriller Seeding** (Night-of disc scene: 'He had been judging men, in the back of his attention...')
- **Issue:** The chapter places the first disc-check on the same night Augustine recognizes Stephen's attachment to Possidius. The juxtaposition implicitly asks: is Stephen the man? The disc is cold. But Augustine's interior in this scene doesn't acknowledge Stephen in relation to the disc at all — his mind moves to the cart, the wounds, the letters, Monnica. The reader cannot tell whether the juxtaposition is controlled dramatic irony or coincidence. In a thriller, that ambiguity should be authored, not left to chance.
- **Suggestion:** Add one line in which Augustine's mind brushes past Stephen on its way to another thought and moves on without naming him. This signals that Augustine has considered and dismissed the connection — which is both in character and precise about the thriller's irony.
- **Before:** `He had been judging men, in the back of his attention, for sixteen years. He had not, in those sixteen years, judged any of them worthy.`
- **After:** `He had been judging men, in the back of his attention, for sixteen years. He had not judged any of them worthy. He did not, at this particular hour, judge the man two cells away, or the man on the small stool beside him.`

**3. [Immersive Experience Editor] Scene Grounding / Emotional Resonance — Household Tableau** ('This is the household I have made' passage, third week)
- **Issue:** This is a moment of unexpected grace — Augustine realizing his household has made itself around him rather than from his intentions. The thought is affecting, but it occurs in the intellectual register entirely. The three figures he is looking at (Possidius's closed hand on the stylus, Stephen's writing-tablet, Eraclius at the door) are listed but not physically rendered. The thought should feel precipitated by something physical he sees in the room.
- **Suggestion:** Add one sensory anchor to the tableau — quality of light, a sound in the room, the specific stillness of Possidius's hand — so the thought arrives as a response to what his senses registered rather than arising unprompted.
- **Before:** `Augustine looked up, on the morning of the third day of the third week, from the parchment to the three of them at the desk. Possidius in Eraclius's chair to his left... *This is the household I have made,* he thought`
- **After:** `Augustine looked up, on the morning of the third day of the third week, from the parchment to the three of them at the desk. The morning light through the high window fell across the writing-tablet in Stephen's lap and across Possidius's hand resting closed on the stylus. The room had the sound of men at work in a room they had been in for weeks. *This is the household I have made,* he thought`

**4. [Immersive Experience Editor] Interior Life / Emotional Landing — Closing Scene** (Final section: 'He had been, he understood as he set the box back...')
- **Issue:** The November closing is philosophically the richest moment of the chapter's final movement — Augustine recognizing himself as 'the kind of man who took the disc down on no particular occasion.' This is exactly the right note. But the self-recognition arrives entirely as a proposition. Compare the Monnica passage earlier in the same section, which lands 'in his ribs and behind his face, not as memory but as presence.' The closing wants one physical note that makes the resignation felt rather than reasoned to.
- **Suggestion:** Give Augustine one small physical sensation as the recognition settles — something that rhymes with the cold of the disc, something in the hands or the chest.
- **Before:** `He had been, he understood as he set the box back, the kind of man who took the disc down on no particular occasion to check whether the man it was for had arrived in the household. The man had not arrived. The disc was cold. The bronze was bronze.`
- **After:** `He had been, he understood as he set the box back, the kind of man who took the disc down on no particular occasion to check whether the man it was for had arrived in the household. The man had not arrived. The disc was cold. He held the understanding the way he had held the disc — at arm's length, weighing what it weighed. The bronze was bronze.`

**5. [Accessibility Editor] Character Tic / Reader Orientation** (Throughout — 'for the count of three,' 'for the count of a hundred,' 'for the count of perhaps three hundred,' etc.)
- **Issue:** Augustine counts silently throughout the chapter — waiting, holding, sitting. It appears at least eight times. This is a distinctive and effective characterization: the contemplative's mind that measures even grief in discrete units. But it is never explained or grounded. A general reader may read it as an authorial quirk rather than a window into Augustine's psychology. The habit could be doing more thematic work — especially given that Augustine is a philosopher of time — if one brief moment acknowledged *why* he counts.
- **Suggestion:** No need for an explanation scene. A single internal observation would do it: something about the habit of measuring being the only order available when events were not ordered. This would make subsequent counting feel intentional rather than strange.

**6. [Accessibility Editor] Exposition Balance / Opening Density** (Paragraph 1)
- **Issue:** The first sentence is approximately 75 words and delivers seven separate pieces of logistical information (the road taken, travel time, escort source, negotiation duration, escort composition, junior officer's scouting visit, and its purpose) before the chapter has a human center. The sentence is deliberately paced to mimic the cart's slow arrival, and the technique is sophisticated. But it front-loads the chapter's single greatest demand on the reader at the exact moment you need them to commit. A reader who bounces off paragraph one never reaches the disc scene or the Stephen recognition.
- **Suggestion:** Consider splitting the first sentence at 'under armed escort' to create two sentences — the cart and its journey, then the escort and its provenance. The second sentence can carry the administrative detail without losing the slow-cart rhythm. The opening beat should land the image first, then fill in the logistics.
